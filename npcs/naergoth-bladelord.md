# Naergoth Bladelord

**Role:** Gatekeeper. Old cult general. Tragic undead commander. Exposition NPC.

He is **not** the final boss. He is a warning, a witness, and a point of access.

---

## Historical Background

Naergoth was one of the Dragon Cult's first generals – back when the cult was about dracolich ascension, not Tiamat worship. He has outlived his era, his faith, and his allies. Now he is the only one who remembers what the cult was before Severin changed everything.

He does not support Severin out of conviction. He supports him out of inertia – he has nowhere else to go.

---

## Personality

- Old-fashioned, military, direct
- Not fanatical – he is beyond fanaticism
- Respects strength and honesty more than ritual and symbolism
- Bitter that Severin's cult has "dishonored" the cult's origins (dracolich worship is his true faith)
- Can speak of Tiamat with respect, but not love – she is a means, not an end for him

---

## DM Use

### What he does

- Blocks access to the temple
- Prefers to talk rather than fight (he is old, not stupid)
- Can be used to give the party information that makes the finale more tangible

### What he can reveal

- The ritual is already underway
- Severin is deep inside the temple – on Level 3
- The Dragon Cult's total resources have been spent on this one attempt
- The temple channels energy from five chromatic traditions (the five foci)
- The cultists are prepared for the heroes to come
- Some of the cultists do not expect to survive – they only want Tiamat to come through
- There are internal tensions: the old dracolich-faithful (like himself) and Severin's young fanatics despise each other
- The masks' power is central – without all five the ritual is weaker
- The dragons outside are both defense and ritual battery (they feed energy to the ritual)

### What he does not reveal

- Exactly how the ritual is stopped
- Severin's full defensive plan
- All secret entrances
- Tiamat's manifestation stages – unless the party presses him hard (DC 20 Persuasion/Intimidation)
- Traitors or internal disagreements (his pride prevents him)
- Statblocks or mechanical information about the enemies ahead

---

## Dialogue Lines

### Opening (he sees them coming)

> "Jeg vidste, I ville komme. Severin vidste det ikke – men jeg vidste det."

He does not turn around. He looks out over the caldera.

> "Kom. Kæmp mig, eller lyt. Begge dele er acceptable."

### If the party asks about the ritual

> "Det er allerede i gang. Det har været det i dage. Hvad I ser nu er ikke begyndelsen – det er slutningen af begyndelsen."

### If the party asks about Severin

> "En ung mand med stor vision og begrænset historisk hukommelse. Han vil sejre. Eller han vil fejle på en måde, der ændrer verden alligevel."

He looks directly at them.

> "Det er faktisk I, der er den eneste variabel han ikke forstår."

### If the party asks about his loyalty

> "Jeg er ikke loyal mod Severin. Jeg er loyal mod hvad dragekulten engang var. Og det er allerede tabt."

Pause.

> "Gå ind. Stop det, I kan. Hvad der er allerede sket, kan I ikke ændre."

### If someone attempts Persuasion/Intimidation DC 20

> He hesitates. For the first time.
>
> "Tiamat manifesterer i trin. Første trin er allerede nået. Hvis I forstyrrer ritualet nu, vil manifestationen blive... ufuldstændig. Ufuldstændig er ikke det samme som umulig. Men det giver jer en chance."
>
> He looks away.
>
> "Det er mere end nogen anden derinde ville sige."

## DM Notes

**Fight only as a last resort:**
Naergoth is not designed to steal the spotlight. Use him as an RP scene. If the party forces combat, give him 5-6 rounds, then he withdraws or is defeated – the temple is more important than his pride.

**Emotional focus:**
The best Naergoth scene is one that gives the party something to think about. He is not an enemy in the traditional sense – he is a mirror of what the campaign's antagonist is, if you remove the fanaticism.

**Possible Aftermath:**
If the party saves one of the old dracolich-faithful cultists during Well of Dragons (captured or surrendered), that person can tell more about what Naergoth did in his time – and provide additional context for the cult's origins.
