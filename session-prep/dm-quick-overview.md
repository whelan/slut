### Tiamat-head (when manifests)
- AC 22, **HP infinite** — cannot be killed, only pushed back
- Bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 4d8 elemental (color), 1/round, lowest-HP target in reach
- Push back: Frygtløs Athletics DC 18 (1 round), 50+ radiant blind (1 round), DC 20 Persuasion/Performance distract (1 round) — **this is the lullaby**

- **Cannot be perceived by Thayan allies.** Axar perceives him normally.
- Axar gets the kill-shot moment if possible — Thay-arc payoff.

## 🚨 Break-glass triggers

### When party combined HP < 200
**→ Elia (Bronze metallic dragon) crashes through the temple roof**
- Bites at a Tiamat-head for push-back, OR shields the party from a Mask Resonance
- Takes 6d10 damage from impact; survives unless DM specifically pushes for Pyrrhic
- Line: *"Du beskytter den her verden. Jeg beskytter dig. Ikke længere end det."*
- Then she's gone — back to caldera air combat

### When 2+ PCs at 0 HP simultaneously
**→ Daxx's turn: he can spend an action to feed a Superior Healing to a downed PC (8d4+8 = avg 28 HP)**
- Combined with Elia trigger, this is the safety net.

### When Frygtløs is Dominated by Severin
**→ Twilight Counter-Song** (Performance DC 14 on her turn)
- Anstruth Harp +1d4 bonus
- On success: domination ends. Frygtløs gets one free attack with advantage and max damage on Severin (NOT rules-legal, IS right).
- See `epic-character-moments.md` Frygtløs Moment 3.

## ❌ DM mistakes that kill the magic

- Don't say *"What about the lullaby?"* — wait
- Don't roll before the player speaks the action
- Don't reward "attack" as much as creative — build asymmetry
- Don't comment on the last line — close the book
- Don't accidentally bring Leosin back (he's dead, has been for weeks)
- Don't reference Naergoth's quarters (area 4) — party didn't go there
- Don't introduce slaves at the temple — party skipped rooms 17/18

## 🎯 If the session goes off-rails

The party is the campaign. If they negotiate with Severin and walk away, if they let Tiamat through and accept Failure Forward, if they choose a completely unexpected path — go with it. The plan is scaffolding. The story is theirs.

But: **read the last line anyway.** Whatever happened. Slow. Don't comment.
