#### AXAR – Arcana Check DC 17

**Setup:** Axar studies the ritual structure. He looks at the lines of force behind Severin, the way the portal pulses, the geometry of the masks.

**On Success (17+):**

Axar sees the machine. The ritual is not random—it is elegant. Five colors, five positions, five channels running to Severin's back. The masks are not floating freely; they are held in resonance with the portal itself. And there—the line of force running through Severin's spine connects him to the portal like a spine to nerves.

Severin is not the ritual's master. He is the ritual's *channel*. If Severin dies while the portal is open, the channel closes.

> Severin nerver. Han ser Axar direkte i øjnene. I mærker næsten, at han smiler under masken af fem farver.
>
> "Du forstår. Du er den eneste herinde der forstår."
>
> Han gør et skridt mod dværgen.
>
> "Du burde havde været på min side, dværg. Vi kunne have talt om ritualerne uden lyd. Du kunne have set det, jeg ser. Magien under magierne. Orden under kaos."
>
> En pause.
>
> "For sent nu."

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Axar gains **advantage on the first attack against the masks** (he sees their structure).
- Axar knows the death-condition: **Severin death = portal closes, but ONLY if portal is open.** (This is critical for understanding Options A vs. B vs. C later.)
- Axar's Arcana suddenly clicks: **Option C becomes viable.** He can see the counterspell lines, the ritual anchors, the whole binding. He knows he could break this if he had 2 uninterrupted rounds.

**On Failure (16 or less):**

Axar looks and sees beauty, but not meaning. The ritual is too vast. He feels like a scholar studying a dragon—he learns the structure of its scales, but not its mind.

> "Du leder. Det er godt," siger Severin. "Men du finder ikke svar her. Kun spørgsmål større end før."

**No mechanical penalty.** Axar just doesn't get the advantage later.

#### DAXX – Insight Check DC 15

**Setup:** Daxx reads Severin. Not his words—his body language. His breathing. The way his eyes shift between the five colors.

**On Success (15+):**

Severin is *afraid*.

Not wild, panicked fear. Not even obvious. But there is a current running beneath his confidence. He did not expect the group to reach this point. His ritual actions feel defensive, not confident. His smile is bright because the alternative is to scream. He is a man watching his own ship sink while he is still standing on it, and he is pretending the water is not rising.

> Daxx mærker det. Severin ser det i hans øjne—han ser, at Daxx VED.
>
> Severin nerver. Et sekund. Ikke længere.
>
> "Du læser mig som jeg læser drager," siger han stille. "Godt. Det betyder, du har længere at komme på end de andre."
>
> Han vender sig væk igen, tilbage mod portalen.

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Daxx gets **+2 to initiative on Round 1** (he sees the attack coming before Severin's hand moves).
- Daxx gains insight into Severin's round 1 action: it will be a ritual surge or a defensive spell, not an offensive attack. *Severin is stalling.*

**If Daxx is currently disguised as a cultist (Changeling's natural shifter state):**

> Han stopper. Han lukkker øjnene.
>
> "Du er en af de bedste jeg har set," siger han. "Hvis du havde valgt anderledes kunne du have stået her i stedet for mig. En stemme. En mand. Hendes hele verden på dine skuldre."
>
> Han åbner øjnene igen, men ser ikke på dig.
>
> "For sent nu."

**Special note:** If Daxx is in cultist form, he gains an additional choice later: when Severin casts Dominate Monster on Frygtløs in Phase 2, Daxx can attempt to drop his disguise and use a bonus action to call out false instructions to Frygtløs, giving the barbarian advantage on the save (the Domination becomes confusing). This costs Daxx nothing but surprise.

**On Failure (14 or less):**

Daxx reads Severin and sees only confidence. Whatever is underneath is hidden too deep.

> "Du søger efter svaghed," siger Severin. "Der er ingen her at finde."

**No mechanical penalty.** But Daxx doesn't get the initiative bonus. Initiative is rolled normally.

#### FRYGTLØS – Intimidation Check DC 18

**Setup:** Frygtløs draws himself to his full height. He does not need to say anything. The weight of his presence, the size of him, the memory of Naergoth falling to his first swing—it all lands at once.

Severin *stops talking.*

**On Success (18+):**

Severin's rhythm breaks. It is not in the script. He stares at Frygtløs for a moment that stretches longer than it should.

When he speaks again, his voice is smaller.

> "... der er styrke i jer. Jeg bemærkede det. Tiamat bemærkede det. Hun kunne have brugt jer."
>
> Han holder øjenkontakten.
>
> "Hun havde kunne."

**Mechanical Effect:**
- Severin's first attack against Frygtløs is made with **disadvantage on Round 1.** (The giant has disrupted him. He cannot quite shake the image of Frygtløs standing alone, unafraid.)
- In Phase 2, when Severin casts Dominate Monster, the DC does not increase—but Frygtløs gains **advantage on the save** (Severin's connection to him is uncertain; Tiamat's protection wavers when she looks at Frygtløs).

**On Failure (17 or less):**

Severin nods, almost respectfully.

> "Du taler uden ord. Der er æren i det. Tiamat ser det også. Hun ser jer alle."

**No mechanical change.** Initiative rolls normally. But the moment is *earned.* Frygtløs has forced Severin to acknowledge him as a peer, not an opponent.

#### TWILIGHT – Persuasion or Performance Check DC 20

**Setup:** Twilight seizes a moment of Severin's charisma and turns it against him. She may sing a line, speak a truth, or play on the tension she sees in the room.

**On Success (20+):**

Severin pauses mid-stride. Something in Twilight's voice catches him. Not romantically—but as if she has named something he thought was secret.

He spends his entire next action answering her. And the party gets one free round of combat before he can act meaningfully. (They move, cast, attack—Severin responds.)

> Severin vender sig mod hende. Hans hele opmærksomhed.
>
> "Min kære," siger han. "Karisma er, hvad fejlslagne imperier hænger på indtil de falder. Jeg har hørt Waterdeep tale. Jeg har hørt jeres råd tale. Jeg har hørt tanken bag ethvert ord. Den eneste stemme der ikke har talt færdig endnu, er hendes."
>
> Han niker mod portalen.
>
> "Når hun åbner øjnene, vil alle andre stemmer være stille. Det er det, hun er til for. Ikke til at dele verden. Til at ende delen."
>
> Han slutter:
>
> "Du vil væ smuk, når hun ser dig. Du vil væ smuk helt til I er væk."

**Mechanical Effect:**
- **The party gets one full free round of actions.** Severin does not act in Round 1. He stands, listening to Twilight, unable to look away. Red Wizards continue their actions normally, but Severin himself is silent.
- This is an enormous advantage: +1 spell slot spent preemptively, +1d4 or more damage on opening strikes, a healing word used before the real danger begins.
- Twilight may use this moment to cast Tasha's Hideous Laughter or use Cutting Words to set up a barbarian reckless attack. The window is explicit.

**On Failure (19 or less):**

Severin smiles sadly.

> "Du taler som de taler på Waterdeep. Sådan taler mennesker der ikke forstår, hvad der kommer. Der er ingen forhandling her. Kun vidne."

**No free round.** Initiative is rolled immediately. But Twilight has *tried*, and Severin has *acknowledged the attempt*. That matters in the aftermath.

### Surprise Round (if applicable)

If the party won a Stealth check on approach to Level 3 (they did not—they came through the front gate), they might have surprise. **In this case: they do NOT.** Severin was watching the plaza from above. He saw them cross. There is no surprise.

However, if a PC used a spell to mask their approach, or if the DM allows a clever infiltration, grant the party a single surprise round action:

- One spell cast
- One attack
- One movement

Then proceed to Round 1.

### Round 1: Severin's Potential Actions

**Severin does NOT immediately attack.** His first round is ritual. He uses a **bonus action: Ritual Action** to increase the clock by +1.

**Why?** Because Severin still believes he can win by time, not by force. The longer combat lasts, the closer Tiamat comes. Every round that passes is a round closer to her arrival.

**What Severin does instead:**
- **Movement:** Takes 30 ft. up and back, using the portal's edge for cover (total cover from behind).
- **Action:** Uses a 5th-level spell (typically *Dominate Person* on a caster, or *Hold Monster* on a melee fighter—Axar and Frygtløs are targets, but he may wait for Phase 2 to use his 8th-level slot).
- **Bonus action:** Ritual Action (Clock +1).
- **Reaction:** Ready. If a PC moves into melee, he uses *Shield*.

**Red Wizards' Round 1:**
All five Red Wizards continue chanting in their respective chapels. They are protected from casual attack by distance and positioning.

- **If a party member enters a chapel:** That wizard stops chanting and casts *Fireball* (DC 20, 10d6 fire).
- **If a wizard is directly targeted (e.g., sniper shot from Daxx):** That wizard breaks from chanting and returns fire.
- **Ritual count:** 5 wizards are chanting. Ritual progresses normally.

**Party's Round 1:**
Use this round to establish positions. Frygtløs charges toward Severin (he will hit cover and have to navigate). Axar and Twilight hold ground and cast buffs/debuffs. Daxx moves for a ranged position and identifies wizard positions (Arcana DC 10 per wizard to mark them for sniping).

## SECTION 4: SEVERIN PHASE 1 DETAILED TACTICS (Rounds 2–5)

**HP Range:** 230 → 115  
**Key mechanic:** Severin delays Dominate Monster for Phase 2. He relies on the ritual clock and Red Wizard support.

### Round 2

**Severin's Action:**
- Casts *Globe of Invulnerability* (6th-level spell, concentration required, lasts 1 hour but he loses concentration if forced).
- **Globe mechanics (2024 rules):** 10-ft. radius sphere centered on Severin. Any spell of 6th level or lower cast from *outside* the sphere cannot cross into it. Severin is *inside* the globe.
- **What this means in combat:**
  - Ranged spellcasters (Axar, Twilight) cannot target Severin with spells of 6th level or below while he is inside the globe.
  - Melee attacks still land normally (Frygtløs can climb and hit).
  - If Severin is forced to leave the globe (grapple, fear effect, forced movement), the protection ends for him.
  - If someone enters the globe's radius, spells cast from outside still cannot affect them.
- **Movement:** He drifts higher, positioning himself in the center of the portal opening, inside the globe. The portal provides concealment but not cover.

**Severin's Ritual Action (bonus):** Clock +1 (now at 2, if starting at 0).

**Tactics behind this:** Severin is buying time by blocking ranged spellcasters while remaining vulnerable to melee. He expects Frygtløs to charge and climb toward him. He's betting Axar won't burn Counterspell on a 6th-level spell. If forced to move (grapple, fear), the Globe breaks and he becomes vulnerable to ranged magic again.

**Red Wizards' Action:**
- Still chanting. Ritual progresses.
- **If any Red Wizard has been killed:** Ritual count drops to 4. Clock +0 this round. If another wizard is killed before next round, ritual resets and clock goes back to 0. (This is the first major pressure point for the party.)

**Party's Round 2:**
- **Frygtløs:** Can try to climb toward Severin (Athletics DC 16) or stay grounded and throw ranged attacks.
- **Axar:** Assesses the Globe. Can counterspell it (costs a slot), or attack the Red Wizards instead to break the ritual count.
- **Daxx:** Identifies Red Wizard positions. Begin sniping or moving to interrupt chanting.
- **Twilight:** Bardic Inspiration on whoever's next save is highest.

### Round 3–5 (Grouped for pacing)

**Severin's continued actions:**

**Round 3:**
- Casts *Eldritch Blast* (2 beams, 2d10+6 force per beam) at whoever is closest. If Frygtløs is climbing, both beams target him (40d hp total if both hit).
- **Ritual Action (bonus):** Clock +1 (now 3).
- **Reaction:** *Shield* if anyone gets too close.

**Round 4:**

- Casts *Hellish Chains* (5th-level spell, not concentration) targeting Frygtløs (or whoever is climbing/closest). DC 20 STR save or the target is restrained and pulled 5 ft. closer to Severin. Takes 3d6 force damage per turn as long as restrained.
- **Ritual Action (bonus):** Clock +1 (now 4).

**Round 5:**
- Casts *Fire Bolt* or *Eldritch Blast* depending on position.
- **Ritual Action (bonus):** Clock +1 (now 5).
- **Severin's Champion of Tiamat activates:** At Clock 5, Severin gains +2 to all saves and attack rolls. This is narrative and mechanical—his eyes glow brighter, his stance shifts. **Describe this as Tiamat actively protecting him now.**

### Red Wizard Interaction: The Ritual Clock as Pacing Tool

**Critical mechanic:** Each Red Wizard is chanting to focus the ritual. There are 5 wizards.

**If 5 wizards chant uninterrupted:** Ritual progresses +1 per round.  
**If only 4 wizards chant:** Ritual stalls (no progress that round).  
**If fewer than 4 wizards chant for 2 rounds in a row:** Ritual resets to 0 and clock goes back 1.

**What the party can do:**

1. **Kill a Red Wizard silently** (Daxx snipe at disadvantage due to distance, or infiltration). This costs 1 wizard.
2. **Engage a Red Wizard in combat.** That wizard stops chanting (must defend) but is still alive to resume later.
3. **Counterspell a wizard's chant action.** This uses a reaction but does NOT kill them. Next round, they can chant again.

**DM pacing note:** Do NOT make the Red Wizards impossible to reach. They are at range (50+ feet, in the five chapels on the perimeter), but if a party member commits to reaching one, they can. Make it a 2-round climb or 3-round sprint, not an impossibility.

**Example:** Daxx uses his mobility to reach the Blue Chapel where a Red Wizard stands. He arrives Round 3. That wizard must now choose: keep chanting (and eat an attack from Daxx) or stop chanting and cast a spell. Either way, Round 3 the wizard is contested. Round 4, Daxx moves again, or the wizard can resume chanting if Daxx disengages.

**Party strategy choice:** Kill wizards to stop the clock, or focus damage on Severin to end the fight faster. Both are valid. The DM should NOT push them toward any one choice.

## SECTION 5: TIAMAT INTERFERENCE – CLOCK-DRIVEN ESCALATION

As the clock rises, Tiamat's presence intensifies. This is not a separate boss fight—it is environmental hazard + morale effect.

| Clock | Effect | How to describe |
|---|---|---|
| **0–2** | Eyes visible in portal. Shadows moving. | *"The portal swirls with five distinct colors, and in the deepest part, you see movement that is not Severin's."* |
| **3** | Tiamat's voice + Psychic Assault | **Voice:** DC 14 WIS save. Failure: Frightened 1 round. Success: immune to this effect for the rest of combat. **Psychic Assault:** All creatures in the temple that can see a manifestation head take **4d10 psychic damage** (DC 21 WIS save for half). |
| **4** | One head halfway through portal | **Head manifests with 15-ft. zone:** 3d6 fire + cold per round, no save. Also: forced movement DC 13 STR save or pushed 5 ft. toward the portal. **Psychic Assault scales:** 4d10 + 1d10 = **5d10 psychic damage** (DC 21 WIS save for half). |
| **5** | Severin: Champion of Tiamat | Severin gains +2 saves and attacks. His connection to her is visible. **Psychic Assault continues:** 6d10 psychic damage (DC 21 WIS save for half). |
| **6** | One head can attack | **Bite attack (+14, 4d6+8 piercing).** Used 1/round, always targets the lowest-HP PC or whoever is closest. **Psychic Assault scales:** 7d10 psychic damage (DC 21 WIS save for half). |
| **7** | Tiamat partially manifests | **Severin cannot be killed** until the portal is closed. He reaches 3 HP and stays there (minimum 3 HP as long as clock 7+). **Psychic Assault:** 8d10 psychic damage (DC 21 WIS save for half). If clock reaches 7, one of the victory options (B or C) must be executed or the party cannot win. |
| **8** | **Tiamat fully manifests** | Campaign shifts. See "Failure Forward" section. |

### PSYCHIC ASSAULT TIMING

**When does it trigger?** At Clock 3 and every round thereafter.

**Who does it affect?** All creatures in the temple (Level 3) that can see a Tiamat head (or heads, if multiple manifest).

**Can it be resisted?** Yes, WIS save DC 21 for half damage. Creatures immune to psychic damage are immune. *Psychic Scream* and similar protections apply.

**Does it trigger every round automatically?** Yes. If the clock reaches 3, the party takes this damage automatically at the *start* of Tiamat's turn each round (or the start of combat if clock 3+ at initiative).

**Narrative feel:** The assault is not a spell. It is Tiamat's attention pressing directly into their minds. Describe it as: *"Et signal går gennem templet. Eller gennem jer. Eller gennem både. Det er som om hun bare *kigger* på jer — og det er værre end ethvert slag."*

> Det er hjemkomst.

> Hun bider ned mod det dragehoved der kommer gennem portalen. Hendes tænder lukker omkring en skala større end Frygtløs' krop. Hun tager 6d10 damage fra det forsøg at trække sig fri.
>
> Så flyver hun væk igen.
>
> I ser hende ikke igen. Men I ser hendes blodige skala falde ned gennem templet.

**Mechanical effect:**
- Elia deals 3d10 damage to whatever Tiamat head is manifested (if clock 4+, she bites the head halfway through; damage is force, bypasses resistances).
- Elia takes 6d10 damage from the attack and disengages.
- The head is forced back 10 ft. into the portal (effectively buying the party 1 round of time; clock does not advance next round).
- Morale effect: The party realizes they are not alone. Someone else is fighting Tiamat. It is a shift in perspective.

**DM note:** Elia appears once. If triggered, she is committed. She may not appear again in this combat. But her appearance is the moment the party stops feeling alone against a god.

---

## SECTION 6: SEVERIN PHASE 2 TRANSITION (at 115 HP)

### The Moment of Transition

When Severin reaches 115 HP (exactly half), describe it like this:

> Blod løber ned ad Severins ansigt. Han tørrer det af med bagsiden af hånden og ser på det. Ikke forfærdet. Interesseret.
>
> "Tiamat..." siger han lavt. "Nu."

**DM Instruction:** This is a brief transition scene. Severin does not get a turn; the combat continues without pause. But the tone shifts.

---

### Severin Phase 2 Mechanics (HP 115 → 0)

**Globe of Invulnerability drops.** Severin releases concentration. He no longer needs it—Tiamat is here.

**Tiamat speaks through him. His eyes turn all five colors at once.** If a PC is looking at him, they see something inhuman for the first time.

**His first action in Phase 2:** *Dominate Monster* cast on Frygtløs.

- **Target:** Frygtløs (Goliath Barbarian, the one who killed Naergoth, the one Severin marked).
- **DC 20 WIS save.** Frygtløs has advantage if he succeeded on Intimidation earlier.
- **Effect on failure:** Frygtløs is charmed and under Tiamat's control for the duration of combat. He will not fight Severin, will not harm Severin, and will use his actions and movement to block other party members from reaching Severin or the portal.
- **Effect on success:** Frygtløs resists. He is aware of the attempt but not charmed.

**Why Frygtløs?** Severin/Tiamat sees him as the primary threat. His size, his violence, his willingness to strike first. If Frygtløs can be turned, the rest of the party is vulnerable. Also: Severin sees Frygtløs as kindred—both of them are warriors who act without hesitation. Tiamat would prefer to have him.

---

### Phase 2 Action Economy (Rounds 6 onward)

**Severin's actions now escalate:**

| Round | Severin Action | Ritual Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **6** | *Finger of Death* on any PC (5th-level save, DC 20 CON, 7d8 necrotic on failure) OR *Meteor Swarm* if 2+ PCs in 40-ft. range (9d6 fire, DC 20 DEX save). | Clock +1 | Severin is now actively trying to kill, not delay. |
| **7** | Legendary action economy increases. Uses 3 Eldritch Blasts across allies (not concentration). | Clock +1 | Still fighting for ritual completion. If clock reaches 7, his minimum HP increases to 3. |
| **8+** | Casts anything that survives his concentration save (Wall of Fire, Confusion, Hypnotic Pattern, etc.). | Clock +1 | Severin is stalling; he knows the party is close to winning the ritual. |

**Mask Resonance (Recharge 4-6 in Phase 2, was 5-6 in Phase 1):**
- 60-ft. burst, DC 18 DEX save.
- 8d8 damage (1d8 each: acid, cold, fire, lightning, poison). No cover reduces this.
- Triggers automatically when Severin uses a Legendary Action, or once per round if available.

---

### Phase 2 Red Wizards

Red Wizards continue to chant. **Each round a wizard is killed, clock does not advance that round.** This is critical—the party can now see the payoff of interrupting the ritual.

**New Red Wizard behavior:** If a wizard is forced to defend (a PC reaches their chapel), they cast *Fireball* or *Lightning Bolt* and then continue chanting. They do not break formation. They are fanatics.

---

### Phase 2 Rath Modar (If alive)

**Per canonical state:** Rath Modar is one of the 5 chanting Red Wizards.

If Rath Modar has not yet been engaged, he now drops his ritual role and enters combat.

- **Round he engages:** Ritual count drops by 1 (now 4 wizards if 5 were chanting).
- **Rath Modar's actions:** Casts *Fireball* or *Confusion* as opening. Uses *Mislead* to become invisible (concentration). If attacked directly, uses *Mirror Image* or *Globe of Invulnerability* to defend.
- **Death scene (when he falls):** See Section 7 below.

---

## SECTION 7: THREE VICTORY OPTIONS & RESOLUTION

**There is no "right" path.** All three can be won; all three cost something. The party chooses their victory condition by their actions, not by DM design.

---

### OPTION A – Kill Severin (most direct)

**Objective:** Reduce Severin to 0 HP.

**Challenge:** At Clock 7+, Severin cannot reach 0 HP due to the portal's protection. At Clock 6, he can be killed. Below Clock 6, he is vulnerable.

**How it plays:**
- Party focuses damage on Severin over 2–3 rounds.
- Severin uses Legendary Resistance (3/day) to avoid a major spell or save.
- When Severin reaches 0 HP, he falls. The Mask of the Dragon Queen falls with him. The portal convulses.

**The Last Moment (Danish boxed text):**

> Den sidste klinge går ind. Eller pilen. Eller spell-bolten. Severin tøver kun et sekund.
>
> Han ser ned på sit eget bryst.
>
> Han ser op på jer.
>
> "... det her var ikke meningen."
>
> Maskerne slukner i den rækkefølge, han fik dem: hvid, sort, grøn, blå, rød. Hvert lys der slipper hans krop, gør ham mindre. Hvert tab gør hans ansigt mere alment menneskeligt igen, indtil det bare er en mand der falder på knæ foran fem helte og en lukkende portal.
>
> "Hun lovede..." hvisker han.
>
> Han fortæller ikke hvad.
>
> Så falder han forover, og portalen lukker som et lys der blæses ud.

**Portal closure:** Automatic. Portal closes immediately upon Severin's death.

**Mask outcome:** Falls intact. Can be recovered and split into 5 individual dragon masks (magical loot, artifact-tier).

**Clock effect:** Any remaining Red Wizards stop chanting. Ritual count resets to 0. Clock does not advance again.

**Mechanical payoff:** Straightforward. The final blow on Severin ends the ritual.

---

### OPTION B – Destroy the Five Masks

**Objective:** Destroy all 5 floating masks that hover above Severin's head.

**Mask stats:**
- **AC:** 15
- **HP:** 30 each
- **Immunities:** Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Poison
- **Vulnerabilities:** Force, Radiant
- **No actions:** Masks are objects, not creatures. They do not act.

**How it plays:**
- Masks float 5 ft. above Severin's head in a ring.
- Severin defends them desperately:
  - Uses *Shield* reaction to increase mask AC (+5).
  - Uses Legendary Actions to move masks 5 ft. away from attackers.
  - Responds to each mask attack by casting *Eldritch Blast* at the attacker.
- Each destroyed mask reduces Severin by –1 to saves and attacks (cumulative).
- All 5 destroyed = Severin collapses immediately. No final damage needed.

**Mask destruction descriptions (one per mask):**

| Mask | Description when destroyed |
|---|---|
| **White** | *"Den splintrer som tyndis. Frostskår spreder sig udad og bliver til damp. Severin gisper – det er ikke hans åndedrag, der er koldt nu."* |
| **Black** | *"Den smelter. Ikke som metal – som noget der altid har været halvt levende. Tjæren bobler og forsvinder, og lugten af sumpvand er pludselig væk."* |
| **Green** | *"Den knækker midtover som et æggeskal. Indeni er der intet – kun et grønt lys der spreder sig og forsvinder som om det aldrig har eksisteret."* |
| **Red** | *"Den brænder op fra centrum og ud. Et øjeblik er den smuk, så er den aske, og asken er væk."* |
| **Blue** | *"Den eksploderer. Lyn slår tilbage gennem alle fem dragehoved-åbninger på én gang, og templet ryster som et væsen der mister sit hjerte."* |

**Portal closure:** When all 5 masks are destroyed, the portal collapses violently. **The Mask of the Dragon Queen is destroyed** (cannot be recovered). This removes one of Tiamat's main anchors to the Material Plane.

**Clock effect:** Each mask destroyed grants clock –1 (per ritual-clock.md, destroying 3+ masks = clock –1, so destroying all 5 = clock effectively frozen or reduced).

**Mechanical payoff:** Long and technical. Requires party focus fire or area control to protect whoever is attacking the masks.

---

### OPTION C – Break the Ritual Circle (Axar's Signature)

**Objective:** Disrupt the magical binding that connects Severin to the portal.

**Axar's preparation:** At Arcana DC 17 (social check earlier), he already understands the structure. He knows what he needs to do.

**The mechanics:**
- **Action 1:** Counterspell combined with specific verbal components. Arcana check DC 18 + number of intact foci (max DC 23).
- **Action 2:** Dispel Magic targeting the ritual circle itself. Same DC.
- **Concentration:** Axar must concentrate for 2 full rounds without being interrupted.
- **Help actions:** Other party members can assist:
  - **Daxx:** Uses volley + multiattack to keep Severin occupied. Description: *"Daxx skifter form. Tre gange. Hver gang Severin vender sig efter ham, er han et andet sted, en anden krop, en anden stemme. Severin kan ikke nå Axar. Han kan ikke engang finde sig selv."*
  - **Twilight:** Uses Bardic Inspiration on Axar's Arcana check (+1d12) OR uses Cutting Words to sabotage Severin's Legendary Actions that interrupt Axar.
  - **Frygtløs:** Stands in front of Axar. No attacks reach Axar from Severin. Description: *"Frygtløs står foran Axar. Han bevæger sig ikke. Severins Eldritch Blast rammer ham i brystet og driver ham et halvt skridt tilbage – og han bliver stående. Et hoved fra portalen forsøger at bide – og han slår det væk med en arm. Han siger ikke noget. Han skal ikke sige noget."*

**Success condition:** Both Arcana and Dispel Magic checks succeed. Portal snaps shut.

**On success:**

> Portalen knækker som hvis man bryder et trolddoms-bånd med knap.
>
> Lyset fra maskerne falder væk. Ritualets fremadskriden stanser midt i et ord. Maskerne... svæver blot. Der er ingen kraft der holder dem oppe længere.
>
> Og Severin ser ned og ser, at han ikke længere er forbundet. Han kan dø nu. Det ser han tydeligt. Og for første gang siden I ankom, er hans udtryk ikke mester - men menneske.

**Portal closure:** Automatic. Portal closes, Mask of the Dragon Queen drops to the floor (can be recovered and split into 5 pieces).

**Severin's status:** All immunities drop. All legendary resistances disappear. He becomes **effectively CR 12** instead of CR 24. Damage connects normally.

**Clock effect:** Ritual collapses. Any remaining actions clock does not advance. Severin is now fighting for his life, not for time.

**Mechanical payoff:** Requires teamwork and planning. This is Axar's moment, and it only works if the party protects him.

---

### What if Severin reaches 3 HP and cannot be killed (Clock 7+)?

If the clock reaches 7 before Severin is reduced to 0 HP, Severin enters a desperate state. He cannot die—the portal is holding him at 3 HP minimum.

> Severin synker ned i knæ. Blod løber fra hans næse, ører, mund. Maskerne over ham ryster.
>
> "Tiamat... du lovede..."
>
> Han ser op. Hans øjne er hans egne igen for første gang i mange år.
>
> "Hun har glemt mig," siger han stille.
>
> Han ser på jer.
>
> "Luk hende ude. Hvis I kan. Jeg kan ikke længere mærke hende beskytte mig."

**This is a pivotal moment.** Severin is now asking the party to close the portal—essentially asking for help against Tiamat herself. A PC can respond, or not. The party's choice defines the moment.

**Mechanically:** The portal is now vulnerable. Option B or C can be executed without Severin's resistance for one round. The party has a window.

---

### Rath Modar's Death Scene (if he falls in Phase 2)

When Rath Modar is killed, give Axar the killing blow if positioning allows.

**Depending on how he dies, Rath Modar speaks one final line:**

- **Killed by Axar's spell:** *"En anden dværg. Altid en anden dværg. I burde have valgt anden side, dværg. Tiamat ville have lært dig ting, jeg ikke kunne."*

- **Killed by Daxx's arrow:** *"Skifter form... og dog... den samme pil... interessant... fortæl Eseldra... fortæl hende... at hun..."* (dies mid-sentence)

- **Killed by Frygtløs's Vorpal:** (no words—Vorpal decapitation; head rolls; body combusts from Mislead residue)

- **Killed by Twilight's spell:** *"En bard. En. Bard."* (laughing, coughing, silence)

**If Axar doesn't get the killing blow:** He gets the last word instead. Axar speaks one sentence to Rath Modar's corpse. Open-ended—let the player improvise. If stuck: *"Du står over hans krop. Han var den, der lærte Severin lyn-resonansen. Du har en sætning."*

## SECTION 8: TIAMAT'S VOICE & WITHDRAWAL

After Severin falls and one victory option is complete, Tiamat speaks.

> Portalen knykker som et åndedrag, der stoppes midt i. Et øjeblik ser I noget på den anden side – fem hoveder, ikke samme, der vender sig mod jer på én gang. Ti øjne der lukker sig på samme tid.
>
> Fra portalen lyder et brøl. Ikke raseri. Ikke sorg.
>
> Frustration.
>
> *"Dette er ikke slut,"* siger noget, der er for stort til at passe i et rum.
>
> Hendes stemme er ikke vred. Hendes stemme er en aftale, der bliver udskudt.
>
> *"I har vundet aften. Aften er kort."*
>
> Og portalen lukker.

**DM note:** This is the turning point. The party has won this battle, but the tone makes it clear: Tiamat will return. The victory is real and earned, but the world is not saved—it is reprieved.

## SECTION 9: TEMPLE COLLAPSE & ESCAPE (15 rounds)

### The Mechanics

The temple does not collapse randomly. It collapses in stages, with each stage tied to the dying ritual.

**Total escape time:** 15 rounds (approximately 90 seconds in-fiction).

The party must go from Level 3 stairwell → Level 2 → Level 1 → Out.

---

### Level 3 → 2 Transition (Rounds 1–3)

**Hazards:**
- **Falling stone:** DC 14 DEX save or 3d6 bludgeoning.
- **Bone bridges crack:** Forced movement DC 13 STR save or 5 ft. push toward edge (80-ft. fall).
- **Dragon-head openings collapse:** Each one implodes inward. Read once:

> Hver dragehoved-åbning kollapser indad efter tur, som munde der lukkes for sidste gang.

**Narrative:** The five heads withdraw into the portal. As they go, the stone around the openings crumbles. The temperature drops. The ritual light fades.

**Party actions:**
- Movement: Full speed (30–40 ft. depending on Barbarian mobility).
- No attacks. No time for combat.
- Describe it as controlled chaos—knowing exactly which way to run, but running as fast as possible anyway.

---

### Level 2 Exploration (Rounds 4–8)

**Hazards:**
- **Deactivated foci explode:** One per round in order (Black → White → Red → Green → Blue). Each explosion: DC 15 DEX save, 4d8 damage (damage type matches foci: acid / cold / fire / lightning / poison).
- **Collapsing platforms:** Narrow bone bridges are falling. Athletics or Acrobatics DC 12 to cross without falling (no fall damage if the party is quick; the platforms lower rather than drop).

**Special:**
- **Daxx's role:** Ranger's Escape (bonus action, no concentration). He guides the group via the fastest route. Description: *"Daxx ser det hele klart—hans øjne flytter rundt mellem de faldet foci, de krakelerede stenbroer, det hele. 'Derovertil,' råber han."*
- **Bodies:** As they run, the party sees the cultists already caught in the collapse. They are not threats. They are just dead now. Sombre visual, no mechanical effect.

---

### Level 1 (Rounds 9–15)

**Hazards:**
- **Panicked cultists:** They are not enemies. They are in the way, screaming and running. Passage check: DC 12 Athletics or Acrobatics to push through.
- **Collapsing ceiling:** Rocks fall. DC 14 DEX save or 2d6 bludgeoning per round.

**Special:**
- **Frygtløs's moment:** He can clear a path for the group (one action—automatic). He uses his size and strength to literally push people aside and hold the way open. Description: *"Frygtløs går først. Han tager mennesker på skulderne og sætter dem ud til siden som man sætter børn ud af farens vej."*
- **The young boy Fen** (if rescued earlier in the campaign): He stands still in the chaos. He is too young to understand what is happening. He does not run. The party might grab him; they might leave him. Either way, he survives (an NPC recovers him later).

---

### Round 15: Final Threshold

> Bag jer lukker templet sammen. Ikke langsomt. Som en mund der lukker sig. En hånd der griber.
>
> I træder ud på askensletten. Bag jer kollapser templet ind i sig selv som et hjerte der stopper med at slå.

**Final save (DC 16 DEX):** Anyone still inside when the temple fully collapses takes 8d10 bludgeoning damage. Halved on success. Most of the party should be clear by this point.

---

### When They Step Out

> I træder ud på askensletten. Bag jer kollapser templet ind i sig selv som et hjerte der stopper med at slå.
>
> Foran jer er calderaen. Den er ikke længere fuld af drager. Skyerne deler sig. Solen kommer igennem for første gang i fem dage.
>
> Hærene ser jer.
>
> Og de begynder at råbe.
>
> Ikke kampråb. Heller ikke sejrsråb.
>
> Bare lyden af mennesker der har fået lov at leve videre.

**Pacing note:** This is the tonal shift. The combat is over. The war is over. The party steps into daylight for the first time in days. The armies see them emerge. It is the moment of victory.

## SECTION 10: AFTERMATH & VICTORY CONDITIONS

**Three possible outcomes. All are wins, but with different costs.**

---

### MAJOR SUCCESS (Ritual Clock 0–4 at Severin's fall)

> Calderaen er stille. Ikke rolig – stille på den måde, der kommer efter noget meget stort er ophørt.
>
> Dragerne i luften... stopper. Chromatic dragons, der i fem dage har kredset som en storm, bryder formation. Nogle flyver væk. Én – en stor rød – kredser en gang mere over tempelruinerne, og I kan næsten se den kigge ned.
>
> Så vender den også om.
>
> Allierede hære begynder at råbe. Ikke kampråb. Noget der lyder mere som...
>
> Overraskelse. Lettelse. Tro.

**What this means:**
- Tiamat is completely banished. She cannot manifest for years.
- The chromatic dragons are confused, uncoordinated, fleeing.
- The alliance holds. No major factional break.
- Casualties are heavy on the alliance side, but not catastrophic.
- The party's victory is clear and unambiguous.

**Aftermath duration:** 30–45 minutes of scenes.
- Remallia approaches with congratulations.
- Army leaders emerge from their defensive positions.
- The party is asked to help with immediate aid (wounded, missing NPCs, securing the Well of Dragons).
- One or two personal/roleplay moments with NPCs who survived.

---

### PARTIAL SUCCESS (Ritual Clock 5–7 at Severin's fall)

> Portalen lukker.
>
> Men ikke uden pris.
>
> To af Tiamats hoveder nåede igennem – brændte den nordlige hærflanke, rev tre metallic dragons i stykker, og efterlod et ar i caldera-væggen, der aldrig helt vil hele.
>
> Bag jer er der grædende, og foran jer er der grædende, og I forstår ikke længere, hvilken slags I hører.
>
> I har sejret. Men "sejret" er et kompliceret ord i dag.

**What this means:**
- Tiamat's heads manifested. Damage was done. Some metallic dragons died.
- The victory is real, but pyrrhic. The cost is visible.
- Chromatic dragons are confused but not fleeing—they saw Tiamat and now they are *furious* that she is gone.
- The alliance took heavy casualties.
- The world is scarred, but not broken.

**Aftermath duration:** 45–60 minutes of scenes.
- Immediate grief for losses.
- Remallia is relieved but changed.
- Army leaders are furious—not at the party, but at the price.
- Multiple personal/roleplay moments with NPCs dealing with loss.
- One metallic dragon death scene (if Elia or an allied dragon fell).

---

### FAILURE FORWARD (Ritual Clock 8 at Severin's fall or Severin unable to be killed)

If the clock reaches 8 before Severin is defeated, **Tiamat manifests fully.** The campaign shifts to catastrophe mode.

> Portalen tager sig ud gennem hele templets væg. Og ud kommer hun.
>
> Ikke et hoved. Fem hoveder. Ikke bare hoveder – almen. Skulder for skulder så store som tårne. Vinger som storme. Hun er ikke indeholdt af templet længere.
>
> Hendes råb får hele calderaen til at ryste.

**What happens:**
- Tiamat is on the Material Plane. The ritual succeeded.
- The party is still standing (they did not die). Severin is still alive (or not—does not matter; Tiamat is here regardless).
- The party must make an immediate choice: **Fight, Flee, Negotiate, or Surrender.**

**The 10 subsequent rounds are chaos:**
1. Tiamat's heads begin attacking the nearest forces (alliance first, then anyone in the caldera).
2. Chromatic dragons become completely organized around Tiamat. They are no longer confused—they have their queen back.
3. Metallic dragons attempt to evacuate the alliance.
4. The party is in the middle of a divine catastrophe.

**What the party CAN do:**
- **Fight:** Still possible. Tiamat is weakened (by destroyed foci). She is killable, but it will take everything. The temple collapses with her still manifesting.
- **Flee:** Viable. The party escapes with Remallia and key alliance members. Waterdeep burns. The world changes.
- **Negotiate:** Tiamat does not negotiate. But her agents (Rath Modar, surviving Abishai) might pause the immediate assault if offered something (artifacts, knowledge, a sacrifice).

**Aftermath duration:** 60+ minutes.
- No simple resolution. The campaign continues into "post-catastrophe" mode.
- Waterdeep is under threat. The Sword Coast mobilizes. The party is now in a world where Tiamat is awake.
- See aftermath.md for full development of this path.

## SECTION 11: FINAL DM NOTES & TABLE TECHNIQUE

### Pacing the 90–120 minute sequence

| Phase | Time | Mechanic |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry + Pre-combat** | 10 min | Stairs + visuals + social checks. Let it breathe. |
| **Initiative + Phase 1** | 25 min | Rounds 1–5. Ritual clock ticks from 0 → 5. Red Wizards. Severin's defensive actions. |
| **Phase 2** | 20 min | Rounds 6–12. Dominate Monster. Escalated offense. Clock 5 → 7 or 8. |
| **Victory resolution** | 10 min | Final saves, option completion, portal closure, Tiamat's final line. |
| **Temple collapse** | 10 min | 15-round escape. Brief descriptions, full speed. |
| **Aftermath** | 30–45 min | Scenes, NPC reunions, implications, rest. |

### Ritual Clock at the Table

**Print this and bring it:**

```
RITUAL CLOCK – SESSION 0 LEVEL 3

[  ] 0 – No effect. Party arrived quickly.
[  ] 1 – First shadow visible in portal.
[  ] 2 – Elemental energy +1d4 to all element damage.
[  ] 3 – Frontline breaks somewhere outside.
[  ] 4 – Tiamat's head halfway through portal (3d6 fire+cold zone).
[  ] 5 – Severin: Champion of Tiamat (+2 saves/atks).
[  ] 6 – Tiamat's head attacks (bite, +14, 4d6+8).
[  ] 7 – Severin unkillable (min 3 HP). Portal closure needed.
[  ] 8 – Tiamat MANIFESTS FULLY. Campaign shifts.
```

**Use a d10 or physical token.** Move it visibly when it increases. Do NOT narrate what it does—let the visual and the environmental description tell the story.

### Initiative & Combat Management

- **Use a battle grid or theater of mind**, depending on your table's preference.
- **Severin is flying/levitating**. Use vertical positioning. Make the party think about how to reach him.
- **Red Wizards are not in the combat zone.** They are 50+ feet away in their chapels. The party must commit resources to reach them.
- **Legendary actions:** Use Severin's legendary actions defensively (protecting masks) and proactively (Ritual Surge for the clock). Do NOT use them just to attack—that is wasteful.

### When Social Checks are Happening

- **One check per PC, not sequential.** They can happen simultaneously if the party wants.
- **Cut-off point:** After four checks, or when Twilight asks for a second check, or if energy drops noticeably—roll initiative. Do not drag it out.
- **Successful checks grant mechanical advantages in Round 1**, not free rounds of combat. (Exception: Twilight's Performance/Persuasion grants one free round before initiative.)

### Managing Dominate Monster (Frygtløs in Phase 2)

This is the hardest moment for the party's dynamic.

- **Prepare the players beforehand:** *"In Phase 2, there's a save that matters. It will affect you directly. Make sure you're ready."*
- **Let the party try everything possible to break the effect:** Dispel Magic, Greater Restoration, Healing Word, talking Frygtløs down, magical compulsion, *Knock*—all of it. The answer is usually "it doesn't work because Tiamat is sustaining it," but let them try. The effort is right and good.
- **Give Frygtløs one moment of struggle.** If he fails the save, he's not immediately obedient. Let his player say something—a word of resistance, a gritted-teeth acknowledgment, anything. Then he becomes Tiamat's tool.
- **When he's charmed, use him sparingly.** He blocks one PC from reaching Severin, or he takes one action to help Severin, but not every round. The charm is real, but the tension is the point.

### Rath Modar's Engagement

- **Rath Modar should not appear until the party is on Level 3**, or very late in Level 2.
- **When he does engage**, make it a moment. Axar has been hunting him since Mission to Thay. This is the payoff.
- **Give Axar the killing blow if possible.** If not, give him the last word.

### Handling PC Death in the Finale

- **Do not avoid it**, but be clear about stakes.
- **If a PC dies before Round 5:** The party has time to use Revivify or Greater Restoration (if they have it). Let them spend resources on it.
- **If a PC dies during Phase 2 or the escape:** The clock is tight. A death here is consequential. The party must decide if they have time to revive.
- **Twilight is low HP.** She is vulnerable. If she dies, it lands harder. The bard death hits different.

### Twilight's Moment

- **Regardless of what happens**, give Twilight one moment in the finale that is **hers alone.**
- **This could be:** A word that lands on Severin. A song that makes an NPC hesitate. An Insight revelation. A Cutting Words that saves the party in a critical moment. A Performance that rallies the troops outside.
- **It is not mechanically central**, but it is emotionally central.

### Victory Conditions Summary

| Option | Mechanics | Cost | Payoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A – Kill Severin** | Direct damage, simplest. | Time (requires low clock to avoid min 3 HP lock). | Cleanest victory. Mask recoverable. |
| **B – Destroy masks** | High focus fire, technical. | Time + concentration (Severin defends). | Mask destroyed forever. Tiamat permanently weakened. |
