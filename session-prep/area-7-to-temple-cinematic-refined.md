# Area 7 → Temple: Refined Cinematic Sequence

**For:** Prompt 1 (Area 7 to Temple cinematic, locked route 7 → 6 → 23 → Blue Chapel)

**Canonical state (locked):**
- Party at area 7 (Secondary Treasure Chamber). Naergoth dead in room. Shelves looted.
- Route: 7 → 6 (cleared) → 23 (tunnel + plaza) → temple front gate (Blue Chapel, area 1)
- No Long Rest. Party ~50-70% HP, 60-75% slots.
- Sacrifice ritual NOT started (no dragons in plaza, just 2 barbed devils + 4 guard drakes per tunnel)
- Starting ritual clock: **0**
- Party: Axar (Dwarf Wizard), Daxx (Changeling Ranger), Frygtløs (Goliath Barbarian), Twilight (Mark of Shadow Elf Bard)

## Scene 1 — Area 7, Last Quiet

### Boxed Text (Dansk)

> Området er stadig varmt fra kampen. Naergoths krop ligger, hvor han faldt, hans hvide maske for øjnene. Hans sværd ligger ved hans hånd — en fin ting, formet som en drage, med guldbeslag. Det kan ikke hjælpe ham længere.
>
> Hylderne omkring jer er tomme nu. I har taget hvad I kunne bære: malerier rullet sammen i lærred, en bog der lugter af Candlekeep og urdømme, en æske der er for tung til kun at indeholde papir. I efterlader mere end I medtager — og I ved det.
>
> Hør efter.
>
> Draakhornen er stille. Det har den ikke været siden I kom til dette sted. Den stilhed er det største lyd I har hørt i lang tid — som luften selv trækker vejret ud.
>
> Bag den, langt borte, oppefra, kommer en anden puls igennem stenen. Fem toner, eller fem grupper af toner, der søger hinanden. Ikke stilhed længere. Sang. Eller noget, der ligner sang, hvis sung af munde der aldrig skulle have kunnet tale.
>
> Templet kalder. Det venter ikke på jer.
>
> I kan se på hinanden. Ingen af jer er rested. Alle er ved slutningen af noget. Men slutningen af hvad?

### Per-PC First Notice

- **Axar**: The chanting resonance underneath — different from the Draakhorn's drone. Arcane, structured, five frequencies binding.
- **Daxx**: The warmth still rising from Naergoth's body. The silence where pursuit would normally come. A trap waits, but not here.
- **Frygtløs**: The weight of the stone above and around — five hundred feet of mountain pressing down, and something trying to push back from inside.
- **Twilight**: The rhythm underneath the chanting — a pulse like a heartbeat, but too large, too slow. She catches herself humming in rhythm and stops.

### Anticipated Player Questions

**"Can we rest here? We should rest."**
> The warmth in the room is already fading. The chanting is getting louder. You can *feel* the temple above, pulling the ritual forward minute by minute. Rest now = ritual advances to clock 2-3 while you sleep. Movement + stealth now = stay at clock 0. Your choice. (Mechanical: DM notes this choice — if they rest, update clock immediately.)

**"Is Naergoth really dead, or is this a trick?"**
> Daxx — check his body. He's cold already (impossible speed, but this is Tiamat's magic). His eyes are open. He will not move again. No trap here.

**"What's that singing? Can we understand it?"**
> Axar — it's Infernal and Draconic, layered. Five voices, each holding a note. Not words. A structure. The sound of a door being unlocked. (No Arcana check needed — they hear it clearly as pure magic.)

### Mechanical Updates

- **Ritual clock:** Remains **0** if the party moves immediately. If they take a full short rest here, clock ticks to **2** (ritual advances 2 rounds while they sleep). Update checkliste.md if they rest.
- **Draakhorn patrol:** Already dead. No random encounter.
- **Potion suggestion:** If any PC is below 80 HP, this is the last moment to drink before major combat. Twilight especially — she's valuable alive.

### DM Notes

- **Tone-setting beat:** The party has been fighting for hours. This is the moment they choose to *continue without rest*. Play this as consequence: they are exhausted. The tone should be grim, not triumphant. They are choosing to walk toward something larger than them, knowing they are tired.
- **Frygtløs's Naergoth echo:** He killed Naergoth with a swing. The body is here. No second chances. This memory will matter in Scene 7 when they meet the Blue Chapel wizard.
- **Twilight's hum:** She doesn't consciously notice she's doing it. One of the other PCs might remark on it. Let it sit as a small thing — it will echo in Scene 6.
- **The choice to move:** Respect it, whatever they choose. Do not pressure. If they sleep, the ritual ticks, and that is a *mechanical consequence*, not punishment.

## Scene 2 — Through Area 6 (Cleared Treasure Chamber)

### Boxed Text (Dansk)

> I går tilbage gennem skatkammeret. Det er stadig her — bjergene af guld, smaragder så store som æg, et sværd med en gylden drage som hjælte. Alt det I ikke kunne bære. Alt det I ikke gad bære.
>
> Og kroppene. Dragonfangen ligger på siden med sit sværd halvt under hans krop. De to flesh golems er stivnet i den stilling, de døde i — armene løftet som om de prøvede at slå slag efter doden blev dem bekendt. De fire guard drakes ligger spredt omkring, nogle på siden, nogle på ryggen. De begynder allerede at lugte af det, der sker når liv forlader et legeme for længe.
>
> Det ser ud som en begravelsesplads under jorden. Det ser ud som sejren.
>
> I går videre uden at snakke.

### Per-PC First Notice

- **Axar**: The flesh golems' blank faces — vessels animated by magic, now inert. One holds its pose for eternity. It will stand here until the temple falls.
- **Daxx**: The Dragonfang's purple belt — an officer's mark, a rank insignia. He wears it well, even dead. Daxx considers it. Takes it. For later. For Twilight, maybe.
- **Frygtløs**: The smell. The heat of bodies already cooling. The weight of things that were alive an hour ago. This is the cost.
- **Twilight**: The treasure unguarded. Mountains of it, going to waste. She passes three times what a kingdom's ransom would buy and doesn't look twice.

### Anticipated Player Questions

**"Can we grab anything else?"**
> If they pause: 60 seconds only. In the high stakes of the finale, every minute now = ritual advance. You can find surface-level common items (jeweled daggers, gems the size of a fist) but nothing rare without a full search that takes 10 minutes. Do they want to spend the time?

**"Can we take bodies with us? Burn them?"**
> No time and no fuel. The bodies stay. They will be entombed under the rubble when the temple falls (if it falls). Let this sit — it matters for the aftermath.

### Mechanical Updates

- **Treasure note:** If players want specific magic items here, let them find 1-2 common or uncommon items on the surface (per DM lock: shelves already looted). Rare items require time they don't have. Note this in checkliste.md as "loot opportunity available but time-pressured."
- **Ritual clock:** No change. Scene 2 is 60 seconds of movement.
- **Potion suggestion:** Water, if anyone needs it. Fatigue is setting in.

### DM Notes

- **Pacing:** This is a cool-down beat after Scene 1's weight. Let it breathe — 60 seconds of silence. Players will describe what they see. Let them.
- **Daxx and the purple belt:** A small character moment. He's taking something from a man he killed. Not triumph. Pragmatism, maybe. Or a souvenir for someone else. Don't comment. He'll use it or he won't.
- **The smell:** Mention it three times (Scene 2, Scene 3, Scene 4). It becomes a sensory thread — they are walking through death.

## Scene 3 — Northern Exits Tunnel (Between Area 6 and Area 23)

### Boxed Text (Dansk)

> Tunnelen stiger opad. Ikke skarpt — bare en lang, glat opadgående bue. Hver hundrede skridt ændrer luften sig fint. Den underjordiske varme aftager. En anden lugt blander sig indmed — aske, blod, og noget der er længere hen på vejen til råddent. I ved godt hvad lugten er. I har lugtet den før. Den er fra området omkring jer nu.
>
> Lyset ændrer sig. Først bliver det bare mindre brunt og mere gråt. Så — så bliver det virkelig — der kommer lys fra uden for. Ikke lys fra en fakkeltrækning. Dagslys. Eller natlys. Du kan ikke helt sige.
>
> Foran jer, oppe ved tunnelens ende, kan I høre stemmer. Ikke de syngende stemmer fra templet. Vagter. Og deres stemmer er på Infernal.

### Per-PC First Notice

- **Axar**: The temperature gradient — it's dropping in a precise way that suggests magical regulation above. The ritual is controlling the caldera's climate. The more he climbs, the more the ritual's influence fades.
- **Daxx**: Patrol patterns. Two voices, not one. More beyond them (he can hear the slight rustling of armor placement). Ready. Watchful. But not on high alert — they're waiting for something.
- **Frygtløs**: The pressure changing. The mountain is less present. Open sky is somewhere above. And with it, something *breathing*. Something large, moving in the air.
- **Twilight**: The infernal language — she doesn't speak it, but she has heard it in ancient texts. A devil's tongue. And the devils sound *bored*. They are waiting. They want something to happen.

### Anticipated Player Questions

**"Can Axar hear the Infernal? What are they saying?"**
> Yes — Axar Perception DC 12: he hears them complaining in Infernal: *"Severin promised us blood by midnight. When the prisoners come, we feast. Where are the prisoners? The hour is late. They should have come."* (See boxed text below for this discovery moment.)

**"How many guards?"**
> Daxx Stealth scout DC 14: At the tunnel mouth, 2 barbed devils stand facing outward into the plaza. 10 feet behind them, less attentive, 4 guard drakes. Two tunnel exits = two sets of guards like this. The party only needs to deal with one tunnel (their exit).

**"Can we sneak past all of them?"**
> Possible — group Stealth DC 16. Drake Perception is the bottleneck. Success = they pass undetected. Failure = combat with no surprise. Risk is real.

### Boxed Text Moment — Devils' Infernal Dialogue

If Axar makes Perception DC 12:

> En af djævlene vender sig og snakker til den anden. Hans stemme er som rost metal. Hans infernalsk taler hurtigt, ubesværet.
>
> *"Severin lovede os blod ved midnat. Ved Tiamat! Tiden er passeret. Hvor er de fanger, han talte om? Vi venter her, og der kommer *intet*."*
>
> Den anden djævel kalder tilbage: *"Geduld. Ritualet afhænger af timing. De kommer, når Severin er klar. Og så — så får vi hvad vi vil have."*
>
> De begge vendte tilbage til at stirre ud på plazaen, deres tornede hud glinsende i det svage lys.

### Mechanical Updates

- **Ritual clock:** No change from Scene 3 alone. But if the party *fights* these guards and the combat takes longer than 3 rounds, the sound carries — see Scene 4.
- **Discovery:** The sacrifice ritual has NOT started yet. This is critical information. It means the party has a window.
- **Stealth DC notes:**
  - Sneak past (group Stealth DC 16): no combat
  - Combat from surprise (DC 14 maintained): 2 barbed devils + 4 guard drakes, ~3 rounds to defeat
  - Axar Fireball: kills 4 drakes, bloodies 1 devil, very LOUD (dragons notice)

### DM Notes

- **The Infernal dialogue is a gift.** This is the only place the party learns the sacrifice hasn't started yet. Let Axar hear it clearly. He earned the Perception check.
- **Pacing:** This is the transition from the underworld to the surface. Play it as a shift — temperature, air, sound. The party is leaving the warrens.
- **Combat option:** If they fight, keep it to 3 rounds or the sound carries. After 3 rounds, shift to Scene 4 (guards already noticed).

## Scene 4 — Plaza 23 Guards (One Tunnel Only, the Party's Exit)

### Tactical Context

Per `well-of-dragons-raw.md:142`: *"Each tunnel is blocked by 2 barbed devils and 4 guard drakes. The devils' attention is focused toward the plaza, however, not down the tunnels or on what the guard drakes do."*

**CRITICAL:** The party only needs to deal with **ONE tunnel's guards**. The other tunnel's set stays put unless the party's combat is loud.

### Option A: Combat from Behind (Surprise Round Maintained)

> Djævlens nærmeste vender sig først. Hans torner rasler mod hans egen brystplade som tyvekoster. Han ser jer, og hans ansigt — eller det der må være et ansigt — trækker sig op i noget, der kunne være et smil, hvis smil havde tænder som sværdklinger.
>
> *"Endelig,"* siger han på Infernal. *"Vi har ventet."*
>
> Han kalder ikke alarm. Han venter på, at I tager første træk. Hans klo hæves, ild samler sig i hans åbne hånd.

**Tactical:**
- 2 barbed devils (CR 5, 110 HP each): Multiattack (tail/claw), Hurl Flame ranged (3d6 fire, 60 ft range)
- 4 guard drakes (CR 2, 52 HP): Bite + tail attacks
- Expect 3 rounds of combat if the party alpha-strikes. If it goes to 4 rounds, sound carries (dragons notice = automatic 1 failure on plaza crossing skill challenge in Scene 6).
- **Victory condition:** All enemies dead and the tunnel clear by end of round 3.

### Option B: Sneak Past (Group Stealth DC 16)

- No combat, no sound, no alert.
- If successful: move directly to Scene 5.
- If failed: combat with no surprise (enemy readiness is heightened).

### Option C: Twilight Suggestion on a Drake (DC 18 Wisdom Save)

- Removes 1 drake from combat math.
- Devils are Lawful Evil and will *notice* obvious suggestion attempts.
- If the save fails, the devils turn their ire on Twilight: *"Magiker!"* They prioritize her in round 1.

### Option D: Axar Alpha Strike (Fireball, 8d6 damage)

- Kills all 4 drakes (likely) and bloodies 1 devil (~3d6 AoE ≈ 30-40 HP damage).
- Extremely LOUD: dragons above **WILL** notice. Automatic 1 failure on plaza crossing (Scene 6).
- Saves time, costs stealth and player resources.

### Mechanical Updates

- **Combat duration:** Each extra round beyond round 3 = +1 to dragon-notice risk (Scene 6).
- **Sound rule:** Combat under 200 ft from listeners in 3 rounds or fewer goes unnoticed per raw module. Combat over 3 rounds = dragons above hear it.
- **Resource tracking:** This is BEFORE a Long Rest. Track HP and spell slots carefully. Twilight should NOT engage if she's below 50% HP.

### DM Notes

- **Devils speak Infernal.** If players have Infernal, let them understand. If not, the tone is hostile and the intent is clear.
- **The devils are not in panic.** They are waiting. They are confident. Play them as cold, measured.
- **Demoralizing outcome:** If combat is loud and the party knows dragons heard them, the mood should shift — from infiltration to "we are committed now."

## Scene 5 — Emerging Into the Plaza (First View of Temple)

### Boxed Text (Dansk)

> I træder ud af tunnelen, og verden åbner sig over jer som en sår i klippen.
>
> Calderaen omslutter jer som en kæmpe skål af sort sten — en vulkans døde hjerte. Bunden er dækket af drageknogler: ribben på størrelse med bjælker, kæbeben som heste-trug, et kranie der står midt i plazaen som en enkelt stille øje, stirrende mod templet. Og der ligger flere — tusinder af dem — rullet sammen som høje efter høst.
>
> Plazaen selv er ryddet af knogler — en lang, åben kløft der strækker sig 250 fod foran jer og ind mod templet, og det er så åbent, det føles som at gå på is.
>
> Og templet.
>
> Det er en sindssyg struktur, der har trængt sig op fra under knoglerne for at tårne sig over den brændte vulkan. Vulkansk aske smeltet sammen med drageknogler, sten der er gennemsyret af Helvedes magi. Det er fuld af kaotiske vinkler og udragende støttepiller. Og der i det hele, i dets vrede af kornicher og himmelstræbende vinkler, ser I dem — fem tårne, hver toppet af et snoet spir.
>
> Blå. Hvid. Grøn. Rød. Sort.
>
> Hvert spir er som en knyttet næve, der rækker op mod en himmel, der ikke ønsker det.
>
> Højt over jer kredser drager. I tæller dem ikke — I lader være. I ved bare, at I aldrig har set så mange chromatic drager på samme tid, levende og vrede og *venlige* i deres veje mod himlen. De skrika til hinanden. De udgør et bulldrende spektakel, hvor hundreder af stemmer kalder hver anden: *"Hun kommer! Hun kommer! Hun kommer!"*

### Per-PC First Notice

- **Axar**: The temple's structure is *wrong*. It violates geometry he knows. The five spires are positioned as if in harmony, but the chords they make are off. It's singing something that sounds like prayer but tastes like pain.
- **Daxx**: The dragons. Dozens of them. Circling, waiting, excited but undisciplined. They are not hunting *them* — not yet. But they could be, if the wind changed. If something spooked them.
- **Frygtløs**: The *scale*. The open air. The freedom to move after hours underground. But also: the pressure of being seen. The dragons above. The temple itself seems to *push* against the sky.
- **Twilight**: The hymn of the dragons — she didn't expect it to be so *beautiful*. Horrible and terrifying, yes. But the harmony underneath, the way they call in harmony — it's a song she has never heard, and something in her wants to answer.

### Anticipated Player Questions

**"How many dragons? Can we count them?"**
> Daxx can attempt a careful count. Arcana DC 14: "At least thirty. Maybe fifty. You stop counting at forty because the numbers don't comfort you." No exact count — the point is that it's a *lot*.

**"Can they see us?"**
> Not immediately. They are focused on circling, not on scanning the ground. But if the party makes noise, or moves carelessly across the open plaza, they will notice.

**"What do the dragons sound like?"**
> Each one sounds like a different instrument in a symphony — the reds like brass, the greens like strings, the blues like percussion. All playing the same note, over and over: *"She comes. She comes. She comes."*

### Mechanical Updates

- **Ritual clock:** No change from Scene 5 alone. But transition to Scene 6 (plaza crossing).
- **Atmosphere:** This is the turning point from underground to open. The ritual is *visible* now. The temple is *present*. There is no more hiding.

### DM Notes

- **Silent moment.** After reading this boxed text, wait at least 20 seconds. Let players look. Let them describe what they see. Let them absorb the *scale*. Do not move on until someone breaks the silence.
- **The dragons are not attacking.** They are waiting. This is an important distinction — they are *held back* by something (Tiamat's will, the ritual's focus, Severin's command). Use this for tension.
- **Twilight's moment:** She hears the dragon-song as beautiful. Do not make her feel wrong for noticing this. Beauty and horror are not opposites in this moment.

## Scene 6 — Crossing the Plaza (Skill Challenge)

### Framework

**250 feet of open ground. Chromatic dragons circling above. Success/Failure condition: 4 successes / 2 failures, DC 15.**

The party rolls skills as they cross. Each success = progress. Each failure = dragon notice risk.

- **Full success (4 successes, 0-1 failures):** Reach temple front gate undetected. No dragon alarm. Keep surprise round on Blue Chapel Red Wizard (Scene 7).
- **Partial success (4 successes, 2 failures):** Reach the gate, but a young chromatic dragon saw something. It shrieks alarm. Party is spotted. Inside the temple, lose surprise round on Blue Chapel Red Wizard. (Ritual clock no change.)
- **Catastrophic (<4 successes, 2+ failures):** An adult chromatic dragon dives. **DM strongly recommends:** the dragon dives PAST as a warning shot (DC 16 DEX save, 6d10 elemental damage halved on success), then returns to circling. Frame as *"Tiamat would not allow us to interfere — yet."* This is TPK risk given no Long Rest. Do not complete this failure without DM intervention.

### Skill Options (DC 15 each)

| Skill | Approach | PC |
|---|---|---|
| **Stealth** (advantage if Cloak of Elvenkind active) | Bone pile to bone pile, broken-line approach, silent movement | Daxx |
| **Survival** | Time the crossing between dragon flight patterns, read the rhythm | Daxx |
| **Arcana** | Pass Without Trace cast before entering open ground, protect the party's aura | Axar |
| **Performance** | Mimic a cultist sacrifice signal — drag an illusory "prisoner" (Disguise Self) toward temple | Twilight |
| **Athletics** | Sprint a section while others cover with Hide, create distraction | Frygtløs |

### Boxed Text — Full Success (4 successes, 0-1 failures)

> I bevæger jer i etaper. Knogle til knogle. Hver gang en drageskygge går hen over jeres positioner, fryser I. Ikke bevægelse. Ikke åndedræt. Selv jeres hjerte synes at stoppe.
>
> Når skyggen passerer, løber I videre.
>
> Det tager mindst tyve minutter at krydse plazaen. Tyve minutter af at trække vejret gennem åben mund for ikke at lave en lyd. Tyve minutter af at se templet vokse med hver skridt — det bliver mindre fjernere og mere ubevægeligt sandt.
>
> Da I når frem til dets skygge, er I ikke længere bange. I er bare meget meget træt på en måde, der ikke er fysisk.
>
> Ingen drager har set jer.

### Boxed Text — Partial Success (4 successes, 2 failures)

> I bevæger jer i etaper. Knogle til knogle. Det virker.
>
> Og så — halvvejs over plazaen — en ung rød drages øjne rammer plads hvor I *var* en sekund tidligere. Han skrigter — ikke en ord, bare en råben der genlyder gennem hele calderaen.
>
> De andre drager vender sig. De se ikke jer. Men de ser efter jer.
>
> I løber de sidste 125 fod. Ikke stilhed længere. Bare bevægelse.
>
> I når templets dør med dragernes hyl i øret. Ingen har fulgt. Endnu.

### Boxed Text — Catastrophic (Failed attempt, dragon intercepts)

> En voksen grøn drages udbreder sine vinger og dykker. Den er *stor* — størrere end I er høj, længde efter længde efter længde af skaldet legeme.
>
> DC 16 Dexterity saving throw (den starter dykket 40 fod fra jer). Succes: 6d10 acid halved. Fejl: fuld skade, og I er dumpet bagover af slagkraften.
>
> Dragen passerer over jer som en stormvind. Den *kunne* have slået hovedet af. Det gjorde den ikke. Det var en advarsel.
>
> Dragen stiger igen og vender tilbage til sine søstre. Og den lyder som om, den le.

## Scene 7 — Temple Front Gate (Blue Chapel, Area 1)

### Boxed Text (Dansk)

> Templets indgang er en bue af sort sten, sammenflettet med drageknogler. Der er ingen dør. Indgangen er åben — som et munde, der inviterer, eller som en fælde.
>
> Indenfor: en figur i rød kappe. Han står med ryggen til jer, foran et alter af blå krystal. Hans hænder er løftet mod det indre af templet. Hans stemme rejser sig i en tone, I ikke har hørt et menneske lave før — ikke helt ord, ikke helt musik, snarere musiklens skelet.
>
> Han har ikke set jer. Han er helt og holdent optaget af det, han synger.
>
> Hans kappes manchet er broderet med symboler: det Thayanske Brennemærke — flammer omkring en tredobbelt pyramid. Han er en af Rath Modars folk.

### NPC: The Blue Chapel Red Wizard

**Name:** Veksin Modar (Thayan name; distant relative of Rath, recruited by him two winters ago)

**Character:** Youngest of Rath's apprentices. Humming a melody his mother sang — the same melody Twilight keeps catching herself humming. They share a cultural memory. He is twenty-four years old, has a scar on his left cheek from a long-ago teaching incident, and wears a silver ring with a child's name engraved inside: *Vestin* (his son, left in Thay, being raised by distant relatives because the Red Wizard's war came first).

### Per-PC First Notice

- **Axar**: Veksin's posture — not the stance of a warrior or a skilled duelist. The posture of a man *holding something.* He is not the ritual's leader; he is a vessel.
- **Daxx**: His breathing. Steady. Prepared. Not afraid. He has been standing here for hours and he is *ready* for the end, whatever form it takes.
- **Frygtløs**: The scar on his left cheek. A wound. Healed, but Frygtløs has dealt enough wounds to know one when he sees it. This man has been *hurt*.
- **Twilight**: The humming. She knows that melody. It's the one her mother sang. It's the one *she's* been humming since they left area 7. And this young man — this young man is humming it too, like a prayer.

### Combat Options

**Option 1: Silent Kill (Surprise Round)**
- Group Stealth DC 14 (drops to DC 16 if plaza crossing failed)
- If successful: surprise round on Veksin. Party acts first.
- **Mechanical payoff:** If Veksin dies in the surprise round without raising alarm (no verbal component used on his part, spell not cast to alert), then 4 Red Wizards remain active. If the party can keep <5 active for **2 successive rounds**, the portal collapses and the ritual count resets to 0 (major weakening event).
- **Veksin's last moment (silent):** His hand falls. His voice cuts off. The humming stops. His ring slips off and falls to the floor — you see the name inside: *Vestin*.

**Option 2: Loud Combat (Initiative at round 1)**
- If they step on a fallen bone, or one PC decides to openly attack: initiative.
- Round 1: Veksin reacts. He is a 3rd-level wizard (40 HP with Mage Armor, AC 15). He has Cone of Cold prepared. He will use it if 2+ enemies are within 15 feet.
- Round 2: The other 4 Red Wizards notice the disruption in the temple below. They feel the ritual *shift*. Rath Modar drops his ritual role and begins the ascent to intercept them (arrives round 3).
- **Veksin's last moment (loud):** He dies in a flash of blue cold or fire. No final words. Just: gone.

**Option 3: Twilight Suggestion (DC 18 Wisdom Save)**
- Twilight casts Suggestion on Veksin: *"Step out for a moment. The ritual can wait."*
- If he fails: he walks out of the temple, confused and docile.
- Mechanical payoff: **Keeps surprise round on the Blue Chapel** but **does NOT disrupt the ritual count**. The ritual still has 5 wizards active. But Veksin is removed from combat.
- **Veksin's moment:** He walks past the party, looking confused, and steps into the night. You hear him calling out: *"Rath Modar, the air — something is wrong —"* Then silence.

### The Humanization Beat (Frygtløs's Parallel to Naergoth)

This is the anchor for Frygtløs's "should I swing?" moment. Veksin is young. He is afraid. He has a son in Thay he will never see again. He is humming a melody his mother sang, which the party's own bard hums.

**DM technique:** Do not ask Frygtløs directly. Describe Veksin's vulnerability. If Frygtløs swings, that is his choice — no judgment. If he doesn't, that is also his choice — no praise. Silence is the reward either way.

This moment might come in the surprise round, or it might come during loud combat (option 2). Either way, it's available to land.

### Mechanical Updates

- **Ritual clock:** No change in Scene 7 alone. But the outcome branches into Scene 8 mechanics (see Scene 8).
- **Surprise condition:** Depends on plaza crossing success (Scene 6).
- **Ritual-disruption window:** If Veksin dies silently in surprise, 4 Red Wizards remain active. Next scene (Scene 8), if the party can keep <5 wizards chanting for 2 successive rounds, the portal collapses and ritual count resets to 0 (massive weakening).

### DM Notes

- **Veksin is not a major antagonist.** He is a Tier 1 NPC (walk-on, memorable because of one human detail). Let him be human. If the party kills him, that is one choice. If they spare him or redirect him, that is another. Both are valid.

Og på hans bryst: Brennemærket — flammer omkring tredobbelt pyramid.
>
