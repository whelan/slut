### Naergoth Bladelord

I had **Naergoth Bladelord** be found regardless of which entrance the characters used. Before meeting him, the players find some room with paintings, depicting prominent members of the Order. The images show, among others,

- Sammaster (founder, 800-916 DR);
- Algashon Nathaire (leader, 916–1018 DR);
- Tuelhalva Drakewings (leader, 972–1018 DR);
- Naergoth Bladelord (Keeper of the Well, 1337-? DR);
- Severin Silrajin (leader, 1480-? DR).

### Weakening Tiamat

The book claims you can completely stop the ritual to prevent Tiamat’s summoning, but that is very anti-climatic. I’d recommend always having Tiamat appear, and weakening her based on the characters’ actions. Five different events can weaken her:

- Interrupting the ritual
- Destroying the **Mask of the Dragon Queen** or removing it from the Temple
- The **Mask of the Dragon Queen** being incomplete
- Severely damaging the Temple
- Interrupting the human sacrifices

It is likely your characters can interrupt the ritual and have claimed some of the Dragon Masks. They might destroy the Temple through the Zhentarim’s **Earthquake** spells, or by their own volition. They might save the prisoners from being sacrificed if they picked the right set of tunnels. They might drag Severin and the **Mask of the Dragon Queen** out of the Temple.

### Difficulty Options

You can control the difficulty of this encounter with many different options:

- Severin has immunity to all damage as long as any Red Wizard lives
- Severin’s resistances and amount of Legendary Resistances are based on the masks composing the **Mask of the Dragon Queen**
- The length that the Ritual takes
- The amount of rounds Tiamat takes to emerge after the Ritual is interrupted or completed
- Rath Modar or any of the Red Wizards cast **Greater Invisibility** on themselves
- Rath Modar or any of the Red Wizards took **Potions of Flying** instead of having cast **Levitate** (so **Dispel Magic** doesn’t cause them to fall)
- Rath Modar cannot be perceived by any Thayan thanks to his illusory wards (so he must be dealt with by the party)
- The Chromatic Dragons jump into the fight as they realize what’s happening
- **Severin** has a stronger stat block
- **Rath Modar*** has his original stat block instead
- Tiamat doesn’t actually kill her allies when summoned
- Tiamat instantly kills all her allies that were involved in the summoning, as a last sacrifice needed for her to break through
- Any Thayans that the characters summon to fight against Rath Modar remain to fight against Tiamat
- The Metallic Dragons jump in front of some Breath Attack, sacrificing their lives to protect the characters
- More than a single part of the Temple crumbles if a spell like **Earthquake** is cast on it
- Parts of the Temple that crash kill all those beneath them

Adjust to whatever strength level you group is at. Ideally, this fight should be very hard, mostly because it’s so drawn out.

> **At My Table**
> At my table, I set the ritual time limit to 4 rounds, had all enemies with Potions of Flying, Rath Modar could not be perceived by the Thayan allies, Severin had only 3 legendary resistances and resisted only Acid, Poison and Fire (the masks he had), Tiamat didn’t kill any allies when summoned (I felt like this would be humiliating to do and then get defeated and sent back to Avernus), the Thayan allies remained to fight, and parts of the temple that crashed would kill enemies that failed their initial saving throw.

### Combat Flow

Once combat starts, go by initiative order. The first five enemies in initiative focus on the ritual, the others attack the group. If enemies are engaged by characters, they focus on the characters instead (if your players enter by Area 24 of the Well of Dragons, the two Red Wizards in Area 5 of the Temple of Tiamat focus on fighting, for example).

- **Red Wizards and Thayan Allies** essentially attack with **Fireballs** and **Cones of Cold**, using **Greater Invisibility** when in danger, **Flying** for mobility, and **Counterspell** or **Shield** as needed.
- The **Wyrmspeakers** run towards the characters and attempt to prevent them from hindering the ritual.
- **Rath Modar*** has **Mislead** active, and uses **Globe of Invulnerability** or **Mirror Image** depending on who’s attacking him. He attacks with **Fireballs** and concentrates on **Walls of Fire** and **Confusions**.
- **Severin** stands back as much as he can, mostly by teleporting away with his legendary actions. He focuses on attacking with *Flaming Orb* or *Scorching Burst* if he finds two or more enemies in range. If a single enemy is close enough, he uses *Hellish Chains* instead of running away.

### Dialogue on Ritual Interruption

If the characters successfully interrupt the ritual, Severin screams:

> *Fools! The Dragon Queen will suffer because of this, and destroy you all!*

Rath Modar makes jokes and overall behaves as if this is just an inconvenient situation, such as:

> *I seem to remember you cutting the head off of my best pupil. Oh well, he did lose his head often.*

or:

> *Oh my, mighty big swords right next to me, aren’t they? Let’s be careful now and point them the other way.*

### Tiamat’s Emergence

After the ritual is completed or interrupted, Tiamat takes 2 rounds to appear, hopefully weakened enough that your characters can still deal with her.

- She appears, and can’t move away from the portal in the beginning. She also cannot take any actions or reactions for now. Her **White** head emerges.
- In this round, she uses her legendary actions with a **White Dragon Breath**.
- On her next turn, the **Black** head emerges.
- In this round, she uses her legendary actions with **White** and **Black Dragon Breaths**.
- On her next turn, the **Green** head emerges.
- In this round, she uses her legendary actions with **White** and **Green Dragon Breaths**, and a **Black Bite**.
- On her next turn, the **Blue** head emerges.
- In this round, she uses her legendary actions with **Blue/White** and **Green Dragon Breaths**, and a **Blue/Black Bite**.
- On her next turn, the **Red** head emerges. She can now act and move about. She will close in on the nearest enemies and systematically kill them.
- From now on, she uses her legendary actions with **Red** and **Blue/Green Dragon Breaths**, and a **Blue/Green Bite**.

If Tiamat’s Red head emerges, the characters don’t have much time to finish her before she kills them all.

## 23.6 Aftermath

Depending on how well the factions were impressed by the characters, how many alliances the characters established, and how well the fight went at the Temple of Tiamat, the war has many outcomes. The most likely scenario is that most of the factions were impressed, most of the alliances were established, and the fight at the Well of Dragons ended in less than an hour since the characters entered the Well of Dragons. Below is the aftermath for this scenario, which should be adapted based on whatever your group did differently.

### Course of the War

- As the characters headed towards the Well, an unexpected ally showed up; Devils from Avernus were suddenly summoned to help on the fight against the Order of the Dragon. The Order of the Gauntlet had some troubles adjusting to this, but mostly the devils focused on stalling the enemy devil forces. They vanished as soon as the battle was over.
- Less than an hour later, a sudden shaking of the ground was felt all around the Well of Dragons. Cultists thought Tiamat was coming, the Alliance worried the volcano was erupting. This was the Temple of Tiamat collapsing and the Ritual concluding. All the Ancient Metallic Dragons flew straight to the caldera to provide support to the characters, while their Adult counterparts tried to keep the enemies busy.
- A few minutes later, a massive shockwave sounded through the battlefield. This was when Tiamat was pushed back into Avernus. The chromatic dragons wailed in unison, the aura of their beloved queen suddenly gone once again. They panicked, and chaos ensued. Most of the Younglings started rampaging chaotically about, and were brought down. A few of the Adults did the same, causing heavy casualties. The rest promptly fled, but not without injuring or killing as many as they could.
- At this point, the majority of the cultists knew they had been defeated. Those engaged in battle could not retreat, so fought for glory and death on the battlefield, hoping to be rewarded when they join Tiamat in Avernus. The rest, such as the ones holed up in the Well of Dragons, fled. The Devils on Tiamat’s side remained, continuing to follow their orders without fear (they won’t die anyway, simply return to Avernus when defeated here). Most of the mercenaries fled or surrendered, as did the Thayans. Giants and other monsters kept fighting without understanding what was going on. Overall, about 500 enemies were captured and will pay for their crimes. All those that did not surrender were killed in less than an hour. Without the support of the Chromatic Dragons, the war was won.

### Alliance Losses

- On the side of the Alliance, the Harpers, Zhentarim, Elves, and Thayans reported the least losses. In fact, Thay disappeared once no more Thayans were found in the enemy’s hordes; the Zhentarim neglected to report their losses; the Harpers mostly avoided direct conflict; and the Elves focused on ranged support.
- The armies of the Order of the Gauntlet, Waterdeep, Neverwinter, the Dwarves, Baldur’s Gate, Silverymoon and Cormyr suffered heavy losses. Current estimates are at a 50% survival rate, meaning half of the people are not returning home. The values oscillate depending on the faction, with Neverwinter being the highest death count (nearing 20% survival rate), and Cormyr and Silverymoon with 70%. Overall, many thousands of victims.
- The smaller armies of the Emerald Enclave, the Frost Giants, and the Arcane Brotherhood reported mixed results. Their survival mostly depended on the luck of what enemies they face. Bigger creatures, like Treants and Frost Giants, dealt well with monstrous enemies, but could not handle hordes of persistent cultists. On the other hand, humanoid battalions fell under the draconic onslaught, but coordinated well against cultists. Overall, a 70% survival ratio, with a few hundred casualties.

### Metallic Dragons and Wyrmspeakers

- The metallic dragons took the brunt of the assault. They quickly caught the enemies’ attentions and were heavily focused. The problem was exacerbated by their rushed incursion to the Well of Dragons to keep enemies away from the characters. Less than ten dragons survived, three of them Ancient ones: Bronze Nymmurh, Copper Tazmikella, and Silver Otaaryliakkarnos. Both Brass Ileuthra and Gold Protanther fell around the Temple, swarmed by tens of enemies. Ileuthra was ripped to shreds and had bits devoured during the battle. Protanther fought to exhaustion and sacrificed himself to save his comrades. The metallic dragons rallied around him and his corpse was not defiled.
- From all the Wyrmspeaker dragons, only the Black Twins Voaraghamanthar and Waervaerendor survived. The others died in battle, with Red Hoondaarh having been the one to defeat Protanther.

I avoided killing any familiar important allies, like **Ontharr** or **Uther Ravengard**, as these NPCs come up in future adventures. I focused on eliminating most enemies that the characters didn’t care about, and left the ones that can eventually resurface in the story (like Voaraghamanthar and Waervaerendor after the Ring of Myrkul). Once more, adjust depending on your plans and what happened at your table.

### After the Battle

The characters could explore the rest of the Well of Dragons, now mostly empty of enemies. Eventually they’d be joined by faction leaders, to divvy up the wealth among the many cities that needed. Politics quickly became heated, as some suggested an equal division among all, others claimed to have done sufficient to deserve more, and others claimed to have suffered more losses to deserve more. Remallia quickly suggests the characters to fill their bags with whatever they can carry (some thousands of gold and a few magic items from Magic Item Tables D and G), and leave to avoid having to deal with the council any longer. Unless the characters are blatantly rude or offensive, no one will dare stop them from taking whatever they find adequate.

## 23.7 Conclusion

Hopefully, the characters have saved the world by defeating Tiamat. She is not dead, only back in Avernus, plotting her revenge.

The aftermath of the war is enormous. The riches need to be split among the factions, and without Tiamat as a common enemy, most politicians will be advocating for selfish deals. In the next few months, populations will migrate back, towns will be rebuilt, and cities will stabilize.

The Order of the Dragon lingers on, with hidden cells across Faerûn. However, all their prominent leaders have been defeated, and those captured will pay for their crimes. The chromatic dragons might hide for years after this defeat, while plotting their revenge, or go on a furious rampage before the dust has settled. Up to you.

Thousands have perished, but everyone looks at the figures of legend that the characters have become, and feel the future is bright.

If Tiamat was not defeated, however, the adventure is still not over… The characters might be resurrected by their allies to fight another day, the players can make new characters to fight Tiamat in the Material Plane, or their original characters can wake up in Avernus, where the pit fiend Bel wants them to resurrect with devilish powers to defeat the Dragon Queen once and for all.

# Chapter 24: Epilogue

## 24.1 Introduction

Depending on what your characters want to do, this campaign can finish in different ways (or might not even finish at all!). This chapter describes several different endings.

## 24.2 Tiamat Succeeded

The characters were defeated at the Temple of Tiamat. Most of them were killed and devoured by the Dragon Queen. She then rose from the Well of Dragons, and defeated all the forces of good in the area. It was a slaughter. Metallic dragon corpses rained from the skies, armies were decimated, the few survivors escaping in a chaotic rush. However, not all hope is lost.

One of the party members survived. Or maybe they left a clone behind. Or maybe one of their allies took the cost to resurrect them. And now they must bring back their friends and resume the fight. These events might happen quickly after the Dragon Queen takes over, or some years past. If quickly, then the Dragon Queen is busy conquering the remaining bastions of resistance in Faerûn. Hunting down elves, destroying the giant tribes, raising all known cities to the ground and claiming their treasure. While she is distracted, the characters have some days to gather their strength and attempt some desperate plan. They may instead way some years to gather their strength.

A few years after Tiamat has risen to power, the landscape is no longer recognizable. All that is left from Waterdeep, Baldur’s Gate, and Neverwinter is scorched rubble. Chromatic dragons rule the world, and most other races are all but extinct. The dwarves hold the last bastions of resistance, deep underground. And they have a plan to get rid of Tiamat. A common enemy unites old foes, and the Dwarves plan to turn to the Elves for help. The Elves have access to a very specific and rare type of magic, known as High Magic, where they can completely rewrite the fabric of the world. No one know if the elves still exist, where they are, or whether they can actually do such a feat. But it’s worth a shot.

## 24.3 Total Party Kill

The characters were defeated at the Temple of Tiamat. All of them were killed and devoured by the Dragon Queen.

They wake up in a barren dark hellish landscape. A weird **Imp** is staring at them. *“It worked!”*, he screams, as he turns away and starts running. The small fiend was tasked by Bel, the former ruler of Avernus, to capture the characters’ souls and reanimate them in hell. The characters must now defeat Bel’s enemies in exchange for a chance to return to the Material Plane. They’ll grow stronger from experience as they battle the underworld, and emerge before Tiamat, thirsting for revenge.

They can appear next to Tiamat, a mere few seconds after she has defeated previously (time works differently in Avernus), they may show up next to her weeks after the event, or they may rise out of some Hell Portal in Faerûn (such as the one under Dragonspear Castle), and work their way to her from there. Bel might even make a further deal with them, their souls in exchange for additional power to defeat her.

## 24.4 Descent into Avernus

The characters defeated Tiamat, and she returned to her prison in Avernus. But the fight isn’t yet over.

She is planning to repeat the Ritual, but in Avernus instead, where it won’t be disturbed. This is the plot of *Tyranny of Dragons into Avernus*, which continues this campaign up to level 20.

## 24.5 Dragonspear Castle

The characters defeated Tiamat, and she returned to her prison in Avernus.

Now characters have to tie up loose ends. One of those is Dragonspear Castle, which was visited by the characters in the *On the Road* chapter. Last time the characters were there, it was occupied by a myriad of monsters. Some of those were **Rakshasas**, who were planning to dig up the Hell Portal under the keep, and re-open it. No good can come of this, and the fiends have had enough time to start their plans. They must be stopped before they unleash hellish hordes upon Faerûn.

## 24.6 Castle Naerytar and the Ring of Myrkul

The characters defeated Tiamat, and she returned to her prison in Avernus.

Now characters have to tie up loose ends. One of those is Castle Naerytar, which was visited by the characters in the *Castle Naerytar* chapter. The characters have likely eliminated the cultists residing there, and it was abandoned to Lizardfolk and to the Twin Black Dragons that reside in the Mere of the Dead Men. The bog keep holds many mysteries that may have been left to uncover, as does the swamp itself. The Ring of Myrkul can be found within the keep, which can then lead to the Uthtower. Within it lies the Twinned Crown of Uthtower, the remains of the dracolich Chardansearavitriol, treasure, and maybe even the lich Iniarv.

The Black Dragon Voaraghamanthar wants the ring to obtain the Crown for his brother. The characters may have recovered it, and the Twin Dragons are after the characters, or it might still lie in the Castle. But with the dragons methodically exploring it, the Ring will not remain hidden for long. And once found, the secrets of the Mere will unfold with terrible repercussions.

## 24.7 Xonthal’s Tower

The characters defeated Tiamat, and she returned to her prison in Avernus.

Now characters have to tie up loose ends. If they didn’t sell or give away Xonthal’s Tower, it stills holds many mysteries to uncover. Its magical hedge maze can be studied by a knowledgeable Wizard, allowing them to learn to harness its power. Within the tower, one can also learn about feather magic (a school of magic unique to the lands of Maztica), chronurgy (two hourglasses in the dungeon can manipulate or alter the time stream), elemental magic (Xonthal sought to incorporate the most desirable features of elementals from various planes and combine them within a single being), or space manipulation magic.

What happened to Xonthal remains a mystery. At some point in his life, Xonthal underwent an extreme shift in personality. He expelled his students from the tower, set deadly enchantments upon the hedge maze surrounding its entrance, and disappeared from public life. After his disappearance, some residents of Xonthal’s Tower village believed the elusive archmage had died, or even underwent the process of lichdom. Clues to his disappearance can perhaps be found within the Tower, leading to different planes of existence and timelines.

## 24.8 Dragons’ Revenge

The characters defeated Tiamat, and she returned to her prison in Avernus.

However, the dragons that led the Order of the Dragon still linger, and they haven’t forgotten the characters. Some have been defeated, by likely the Twin Black Dragons Voaraghamanthar and Waervaerendor and the Red Dragon Hoondarrh still live, and they’re seeking revenge. They will plot to find the characters in a vulnerable position, and strike. How long can the characters keep alert against such an ominous menace?

## 24.9 Character Arcs

The characters defeated Tiamat, and she returned to her prison in Avernus.

The characters met a lot of new friends, made many new enemies, and might have even fallen in love. Whatever reason made them become adventurers or mercenaries in the first place has certainly changed after this long campaign. Perhaps now they have the means to sustain their families, the strength to avenge their fallen loved-ones, or the knowledge to guide their communities. It is time to wrap up the threads that were left unsolved, the motivations that led their actions so far.

## 24.10 Other Enemies

The characters defeated Tiamat, and she returned to her prison in Avernus.

The characters met a lot of new friends, made many new enemies, and might have even fallen in love. Some of these enemies have escaped, either to plot their revenge for later, or to escape without atoning for their sins. It is time to wrap up the threads that were left unsolved, the evils that have not yet been vanquished.
