## Key Features Refined

### Scene 1 — Tone-Setting After Hours of Combat

- Emphasizes the party is *choosing* to continue without Long Rest
- Frygtløs sees Naergoth's body again; sets up Scene 7 parallel
- Twilight's hum is planted (will echo in Scenes 6 and 7)
- Axar recognizes the ritual resonance structure

### Scene 4 — Devils' Infernal Dialogue (Critical Discovery)

> *"Severin promised us blood by midnight. When the prisoners come, we feast. Where are the prisoners? The hour is late."*

This is **the only moment the party learns the sacrifice ritual hasn't started yet** — critical information for understanding their advantage.

### Scene 5 — ENORMOUS Scale

- Five colored steeples as a visual anchor
- Dragons circling in the air, hundreds of them, calling *"She comes! She comes! She comes!"*
- Caldera as a bowl of death, bones everywhere
- Temple as a wound in the earth
- Includes 20-second silent moment for players to absorb

### Scene 6 — Plaza Crossing (3 Outcomes)

Full Danish boxed text for:
1. **Full success** — 20 minutes of careful crossing, dragons don't notice
2. **Partial success** — young dragon shrieks alarm, party reaches gate spotted
3. **Catastrophic** — adult dragon dives as warning shot, DM-recommended intervention

### Scene 7 — Blue Chapel Wizard Named

**Veksin Modar** (Thayan, age 24, Rath's youngest apprentice):
- Scar on left cheek from a teaching incident
- Silver ring with his son's name: *Vestin*
- Humming the same lullaby Twilight hums
- Humanized before combat — this is Frygtløs's moment to *not* swing first, if he chooses

Three tactical branches:
- Silent kill → ritual disruption window opens (major weakening event)
- Loud combat → Rath Modar engages round 2
- Suggestion → removes wizard from ritual but doesn't disrupt count

### Scene 8 — Rath Modar Recognition

Axar (Arcana DC 10) immediately spots Rath Modar's silhouette in the Red Chapel:
- Thayan robes (ancient cut, distinctive)
- Burning Brand insignia
- Makes eye contact across the temple
- Smiles before returning to ritual

**This is the Thay-arc payoff moment.** Axar recognizes his old mentor/rival leading the ritual. Rath Modar recognizes Axar looking at him.
