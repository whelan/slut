# Level 1 – The Maw: Black Chapel Adaptation

**Purpose:** Replace the Gate of Bone encounter (Encounter A) with an entry-via-Black-Chapel scenario. The party emerges from the lava tube into area 5, behind the ritual, with a surprise round advantage.

**Canonical state:**
- Party enters via lava tube into **area 5 (Black Chapel)** — behind a single Red Wizard
- They have ALREADY seen the temple from below (previous session, room 7 observation)
- Draakhorn patrol already killed (no reinforcement pressure)
- Sacrifice ritual at plaza NOT started (party skipped rooms 17/18)
- Starting ritual clock: **0**
- Naergoth dead; no exposition gap at the gate

---

## The Arrival – Transition from Warrens

*Read this as the party emerges from the lava tube into area 5.*

> Lavastrømmen der fyldte gangens øst-ende er nu stilnet til et dryp. I står på varm basalt, og foran jer åbner kammeret sig.
>
> Det er ikke det blå kapel, I så nedenfor. Det er det sorte. Og det er tæt på.
>
> Inde i kapellet står der en enlig mand i rød silke. Hans hænder glødende fra magien, hans øjne uden lys. Han chanter noget — ikke ord. Det er dybere end ord. Det er som om hans stemme kommer fra jorden selv.
>
> Omkring ham på gulvet: runerne fra før. Blod-røde kanaler i stenen, pulserende.
>
> Midtvejs gennem kammeret, væk fra jer, ligger det store kalejdoskop af kraft. Det er højere end I forventede. Det svinger ikke stabilt. Det hviner.
>
> I den anden ende af den store hal — meget, meget længere væk — ser I:
>
> **Fem mand i rødt.** Hver ved sit hjørne.
>
> Og imellem dem alle, leviterende i et lysekrans af femfarvet energi, står Severin.

**DM-note:** Do not pause here if the party was at the observation window before. This is arrival-beat only. Give players immediate agency: *"What do you do?"* — then roll initiative.

---

## Encounter A – The Black Chapel Red Wizard (Solitary Mage)

**Type:** Tactical combat or Counterspell duel
**Situation:** A lone Red Wizard guards area 5. Four others are present but distant in the foci chapels (they do not react until Round 2).

---

### Discovery

The party has **surprise round advantage** if they did not make noise in the lava tube. If they remain stealthy on entry:
- Daxx can move up to 40 ft. undetected (Stealth DC 14 vs. the mage's Perception passive 13).
- The mage's initiative is rolled, but he acts **last in Round 1** — he did not see them coming.

**If noise was made:** Standard surprise check. The mage's passive Perception is 13. DEX (Stealth) DC 14 to avoid the surprise round.

---

### Enemy Stat Block – Red Wizard (Mage)

*Role:* Guards area 5. Focuses on the ritual action and damage spells once alerted.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| AC | 12 (Robe of the Archmagi, base 10 + DEX) |
| HP | 27 (6d8) |
| Speed | 30 ft. |
| STR/DEX/CON | 8/12/14 |
| INT/WIS/CHA | 15/12/14 |
| Saves | INT +5, WIS +4 |
| Skills | Arcana +5, Insight +4 |
| Damage immunities | Fire (Robe of the Archmagi) |
| Resistances | Cold, Lightning (5d4 elemental resistance per ritual clock) |
| Senses | Passive Perception 13 |
| CR | 6 (2,300 XP) |
| Spellcasting | Evoker. Spell save DC 15 (concentration required for ritual) |

**Spell list:**
- Cantrips: *Mage Hand, Prestidigitation, Ray of Frost*
- 1st-level (4 slots): *Magic Missile, Burning Hands, Mage Armor (pre-cast), Identify*
- 2nd-level (3 slots): *Scorching Ray, Misty Step, Mirror Image*
- 3rd-level (3 slots): *Fireball, Counterspell, Dispel Magic*
- 4th-level (1 slot): *Ice Storm, Greater Invisibility*

**Legendary Actions (if the party delays killing the other wizards):** None. He is a CR 6 solo — treat him as a spellcaster, not a boss.

---

### Tactics – Round 1 (If Surprised)

**The mage is in the middle of a ritual action** when the party enters. If surprised:
- On his turn (which comes last due to surprise), he immediately **casts Counterspell** against any obvious interrupt (if Axar is casting, if Twilight is casting). DC 15.
- If no immediate threat is cast, he uses an **Action Surge equivalent** (bonus action) to continue the ritual action: **+1 to ritual count** (this does NOT tick the clock yet — it's the mage performing his 1/6th of the ritual round).
- He then moves **10 ft. toward the party** (away from the portal) and casts **Fireball** (3rd-level, 8d6 fire, DC 15 DEX save) centered on the party's likely position.

**If not surprised (party made noise):**
- Mage is alert. He casts **Greater Invisibility** on himself (bonus action, concentration) and moves to a different position in the chapel.
- The party must make Perception DC 13 checks to track him as he moves. Once invisible, he casts spells from a concealed position.

---

### Tactics – Round 2 Onward

**Other four Red Wizards notice the alarm.**

At the **end of Round 1**, if the Black Chapel Red Wizard hasn't been killed:
- He uses a **reaction to cast Misty Step** (teleport away) or shout an alarm.
- At the start of Round 2, read the following:

> Fra hver af de fem kapeller høres det samme ordlyd, som om der kommer et svar på en tilråb. Og så — samtidigt — stopper de fem røde trollkarle med at chante. Deres øjne vender mod jeres område.
>
> De er langt væk. De flyver ikke efter jer endnu — men de er klar til det.

**From Round 2 onward:**
- The 4 remaining Red Wizards in the chapel areas (areas 1, 3, 4, 6) will **each take 1 round to reach the Black Chapel party's position** (they are 60+ ft. away, or must navigate down from their chapels).
- While they move, they **cast support spells** (Misty Step, Haste on allies, more Fireballs).
- On arrival, each one uses a **ritual action** (if possible). If all 5 mages are performing the ritual, the clock does NOT tick. If fewer than 5 perform, the ritual stalls.

---

### Immediate Victory Condition – Portal Collapse

**If the party kills the Black Chapel Mage in Round 1 AND prevent the other 4 Red Wizards from using a ritual action for 2 consecutive rounds:**

> Portalens hjerte — hele det kalejdoskop af energi — knitter momentant. Det bliver mindre. Det hviner højerere.
>
> Og så kollapser det helt. En stil af sort lys slukker som en blæst lys.
>
> For et øjeblik er der fuldstændig stille i templet.

**Clock effect:** **–2 immediately.** The ritual count resets to 0. This is the largest single-round clock reversal available to the party.

**Conditions for this win:**
- Black Chapel Mage dies in Round 1.
- The party prevents **5 Red Wizards from performing the ritual for 2 successive rounds** (Rounds 1–2 and 2–3). This means:
  - Either kill 3 of the remaining 4 mages by end of Round 2, OR
  - Use Counterspell / Dispel Magic to block their ritual actions, OR
  - Prevent them from reaching the Black Chapel area.

## Encounter B – The Procession Reframed

**Type:** Moral / sensory scene (NOT combat unless the party intervenes)
**Timing:** Happens during Round 2–3 of Black Chapel combat, or immediately after if combat ends early.

---

### Setup

The party's attention is on the Red Wizards above and the portal. But if they listen—or if Twilight's Perception (passive 20) or Axar's passive Perception (17) triggers—they hear:

> Fra en sidegangen—der hvor Naergoths krop ligger bag jer— kommer der lyd. Ikke kamp. Ikke varsel.
>
> Sang. Svag først. Så stærkere.
>
> Tredive stemmer. Eller flere. De marsjerer.

The party has **3 rounds of combat time** to decide whether to act. If they do nothing, the procession continues past them toward the central apse.

---

### The Scene

If the party turns to look (or if they finish the Black Chapel Mage before Round 3):

> Ud fra gangens mørke kommer de. Tredive mennesker. Kvinder, mænd, nogle der er for unge til at have valgt det her.
>
> De går i rækker. De synger som én stemme. Deres øjne er lukkede eller stirrer lige ud.
>
> I forreste række går en ældre kvinde. Hun bærer et banner med Tiamats symbol — femfarverne vævet sammen som en blodig rede. Hendes stemme er den dybeste. Hendes læber bevæger sig synkront med alle de andre.
>
> De er ikke fangslåede. De søger ikke hjælp.
>
> De marcherer mod ritualet som om de er den sidste sang de skal synge.

**DM-note:** Wait. Do not continue if players are in combat. If they are NOT in combat, wait for them to respond.

---

### Conditional Behaviors

**They go toward the central apse (area 7), not toward the party.** They will be **sacrificed during the transition to Level 2**, visible to the party as they ascend. This creates the horror beat at the apse entrance.

**If Daxx makes an Insight DC 13 check** (as he did in the original Procession encounter):
- One of the cultists (a young man, maybe 20) is **consciously aware**. He looks back toward the lava tube entrance where the party emerged. For a moment, his eyes focus.
- This is **Fen, age 16** — dressed in the procession robes, marching with the others.
- Daxx recognizes him from the warren observation (if the party was there) OR recognizes him as the boy who was in the slave pens (rooms 17/18, if the party visited).

**Fen's state:**
- He is awake, aware, and afraid.
- He was volunteered by a Red Wizard after being told the ritual would make him part of Tiamat's ascension (he believed it).
- If the party attacks the procession, Fen will **try to run back toward the lava tube** — he will NOT fight.
- If the party does nothing, Fen marches toward the apse, resigned.

---

### Choices and Consequences

**Option 1: Let the procession pass (no action)**
- The party focuses on combat.
- Clock **+1** (the ritual proceeds uninterrupted).
- Fen is sacrificed. His body is among the 30 at the apse when the party ascends.
- Frygtløs hears the sound of 30 voices cut short when the sacrifice ritual completes (transition scene, below).

**Option 2: Stop the procession (combat)**
- The cultists fight the party unarmed. AC 10, HP 9 each, 0 damage attacks. They will NOT flee — they **want** the martyr's death.
- Fen breaks free and runs toward the lava tube exit (he will not fight the party).
- If the party saves him: Fen can provide information about the temple layout once he's no longer under cult charm.
- **Clock penalty:** –1 (delays the sacrifice ritual).
- **Combat penalty:** Combat takes >2 rounds almost certainly, which results in +1 to the clock for delaying. **Net result: clock breaks even (–1 + 1 = 0).**

---

### ⚠️ PROCESSION MORALE RULES

**The cultists are NOT soldiers. They are volunteers under magical compulsion.** Their morale is locked.

| Event | Cultist Behavior | Morale effect |
|---|---|---|
| Party attacks the procession | **Do not flee.** They accept the violence as part of their sacrifice. Continue marching until physically separated from the column. | Morale locked. No rout. |
| Older woman (leader) is killed | **Remaining cultists falter for 1 round.** 1d6 cultists stop marching (DC 13 WIS save). Others continue. | Minor disruption; they still march toward the apse. |
| Combat lasts >3 rounds without party intervention | **The ritual completes.** Fen is sacrificed in the apse (party hears screaming). Remaining cultists (20–25 alive) scatter in panic—now they understand their death is coming next. | Major panic; morale BREAKS. Survivors flee toward side exits. |

**Key point:** Morale is not "break if your leader dies" — it is "they are walking to their deaths *willingly*." The older woman is not their leader; she is their *voice*. If she dies, they lose the ritual's unifying focus momentarily, but they press on.

**If the party fully defeats the procession in combat (kills all 30 cultists):** The ritual's human component is completely severed. Clock –2 immediately. But Fen is dead too (caught in the fight), unless the party specifically saves him (Sleight of Hand, grab him, pull him aside). This victory is pyrrhic.

**Option 3: Social interaction (Persuasion DC 15)**
- Twilight or Daxx can try to break the ritual compulsion on 1d6 cultists with a successful Persuasion check.
- Those cultists stop marching. They do not leave the temple — but they do not participate in the ritual.
- **Clock penalty:** –1 (weakens the ritual's human component).
- Fen is **not among those saved** (he is too deep in the compulsion), but Twilight can see his fear. **Character beat:** Twilight recognizes his youth and marks it. *"He's too young."*

**Option 4: Grab Fen (Sleight of Hand / Deception)**
- Twilight uses Disguise Self or Sleight of Hand to pull Fen out of the procession unnoticed.
- **DC 15 Deception check** against the procession's compulsion (contested WIS save, or automatic success if the party is subtle).
- If successful: Fen is out, and the procession continues with **29 cultists** (one less).
- **Clock penalty:** –1 (one fewer sacrifice).
- Fen is **alive and awake**, beholden to the party.
- Fen can provide **Advantage on one Religion or Arcana check** related to the temple (he overheard cultist talk about the foci and Severin's location).

---

### Fen – Character State

| Choice | Fen's state | Impact | Clock |
|---|---|---|---|
| Let procession pass | Dead (sacrificed at apse) | None in this phase; used in aftermath narration | +1 |
| Combat stop | Saved and alive | +1 Ally; info on temple (Advantage, 1 use) | 0 |
| Persuasion partial | Still marches, compulsion broken for others | 1d6 cultists no longer participate | –1 |
| Grab Fen (Sleight) | Saved and alive | +1 Ally; one fewer sacrifice | –1 |

**Frygtløs parallel beat:** If Frygtløs chooses to **let Fen go** (doesn't swing, doesn't grab), this mirrors Naergoth. If he **does act to save Fen**, this shows growth. Do not lecture — set it up, let the player choose.

## Red Wizard Stat Block (Reference)

All Red Wizards in the temple use the **Mage** stat block from the 2024 SRD.

**Modification for ritual requirement:** Each Red Wizard in the temple can use an **action** to perform the ritual. This does NOT prevent other actions or movement. The ritual action is purely to maintain the portal's integrity.

**Counterspell:** Any Red Wizard with 3rd-level spells can Counterspell. DC 15.

## Three Possible Clock Outcomes from Black Chapel Emergence

The party's actions in Encounters A and B will determine the starting clock for Level 2 (Niveau 2 – The Fivefold Sanctum).

| Outcome | Clock change | Narrative |
|---|---|---|
| **Best:** Kill Black Chapel Mage in R1, prevent other 4 from ritual for 2 rounds, stop procession via Sleight/grab | –3 | Portal flickers. Ritual severely weakened. Party hears Severin shouting commands to the other mages. |
| **Medium:** Kill Black Chapel Mage in R2, let procession pass or partially stop | 0 | Portal stabilizes. Ritual is interrupted but recovers. The party enters Level 2 at baseline. |
| **Worst:** Black Chapel Mage survives R2, procession fully passes without interference | +2 | Portal glows brighter. Four additional Red Wizards are now engaged. Ritual accelerates. |

**Clock capping:** The starting clock is capped at **0** per canonical state. Any decrease (–2, –3) counts as progress but does not reveal the clock value to the party until they ascend.

>

>

>

>

>

> Trapperne er steenede. Varmen bliver til flamme — ikke omkring jer, men *i* rummet. I ser rødt gennem småt spalter.
>

>

>
> *"Så kom I ikke for sent alligevel. Tiamat ville være skuffet hvis I gjorde."*

## DM Notes

1. **Frygtløs's decision:** Do not explain the parallel. Let him choose. If he kills Fen, that's his character. If he saves Fen, that's growth.

2. **Daxx as scout:** If Daxx uses his high Perception and moves ahead (Stealth check), he can potentially reach the Red Wizard unseen and set up a bonus action attack from hiding in Round 1. Reward this if the party plans it.

3. **Axar vs. Counterspell:** The Black Chapel Mage will Counterspell Fireball or any obvious ritual-disrupting spell. But if Axar casts something subtle (Magic Missile, Dimension Door prepped for allies), the mage might not counter. This is a Counterspell duel — make it dramatic.

4. **Twilight's moment:** If she uses Bardic Inspiration on an ally being burned by Fireball, or if she casts Tasha's Hideous Laughter on the mage to break concentration, describe her voice cutting through the Red Wizard's chant.

5. **Ritual clock visibility:** Do NOT tell the party the clock value. Instead, describe the portal's state:
   - Clock 0: "The portal stabilizes. Severin's expression does not change."
   - Clock +1: "The portal glows brighter. The shapes inside it shift."
   - Clock +2: "The portal tears at its edges. Something inside pulls harder."

6. **Combat duration note:** This should be **5–10 minutes** if the party kills the Black Chapel Mage quickly. If they struggle, time stretches. Be ready to adjudicate on the fly.

## Transition to Level 2 (Niveau 2 – The Fivefold Sanctum)

Once the Procession encounter is resolved and the party begins climbing the stairs, use the boxed text above. Then:

> Jeres øjne vender sig mod de fem kapeller. I hver en ser I det samme:
>
> En trollkarl i rødt — og sammen med ham, for hver dragekappe vi så nedenfor, en skyggefull humanoid form. Men ikke helt humanoid.
>
> De er Abishaier. Genopstået. Og de venter.

At this point, move to **Niveau 2: The Fivefold Sanctum** encounters. The party is inside the temple. The ritual is underway. The clock is ticking.
