# Level 1 – Black Chapel Emergence (Lava Tube Entry)

**Function:** Party emerges into the temple through area 5 (Black Chapel). Replaces the Gate of Bone encounter entirely. Max 30 minutes + transition to Level 2.

**Canonical state (locked):**
- Party has already seen the temple from below (Scene 6 of room-7-to-temple sequence in tiamat-finale-plan.md)
- Draakhorn patrol already killed (no reinforcement pressure)
- Sacrifice ritual at plaza NOT started
- **Starting ritual clock: 0**

**Tone:** The party emerges from darkness into a cathedral of fire and chaos. They are *inside* the ritual. It is already happening. This is the moment the campaign has been building toward.

---

## The Ascent – Before Level 1 Begins

*Read this as the party is ascending the lava tube, nearing the temple's lower chapel.*

> Stenene omkring jer begynder at blive varme. Ikke behagelig varme. Varme, der får jeres hud til at skrumpe omkring kanten af jer.
>
> Lavatuben stiger stejlere. I kan høre det nu — syngen. Fem lyde, hver især en farve, hver især en hunger. De kører sammen gennem stenene som hvis blodet selv blev musik.
>
> Og så uden varsel —
>
> Luften åbner sig.

**DM-note:** Let the moment land. The party has been underground for hours. They have killed enemies, looted treasure, seen Naergoth fall. Now they step into the ritual space itself. The transition from dungeon to temple is absolute.

## Arrival – Area 5, Black Chapel

### Visual Description

> I træder ud af steget og ind i templet.
>
> Rummet er højere end noget I har set undtagen calderaen selv. Tagtømrene forsvinder op i mørke, og når I følger dem opad med øjnene, forlader de jeres evne til at se. Det er som at kigge op i en uendelig kulde.
>
> Kapellet omkring jer er sort — gulvet er svart sten med sprekker der pulserer med sort energi. En pentagram er graveret i kødet af stenen selv. Og op ad væggene løber kanaler som venerne på et dyr — de er fyldt med sort lys, der flyder op ad som hvis blodet blev trukket *op* i stedet for ned.
>
> Midt i rummet står en figur. En rød trylleformand, udklædt i kultritualer. Hans arme er løftet op. Hans øjne er lukkede. Han chanter på en sprog, der er ikke dansk, ikke Drakonic — det er noget helt tredje. Det er sproget, som templet selv taler.
>
> Han har ikke bemærket jer endnu.
>
> Men ovenfor, helt oppe ved tagtømrene, ser I lysene. Fem chapeller stiger opad omkring jer i en stjerne-form. Hver kapel har sit eget lys — blå, hvid, grøn, rød, sort.
>
> Og helt oppe, hvor himlen burde være, pulserer en hvid portal med fem farver roterende gennem dens kant. Under portalen ligger det centrale alter. Og på det centrale alter, svævende uden synlig støtte, står en figur.
>
> Det er Severin.

**Tactical Features:**
- Black Chapel is a 60-foot-diameter circular chamber, 80 feet tall
- Red Wizard is at center, performing ritual (no armor, Mage statblock, concentration check = broken ritual)
- Five ritual channels run up the walls (3 ft wide, glowing black energy) — no damage crossing them, but visible as part of ritual infrastructure
- Portal floats above center apse at 100 feet — visible but unreachable from this level
- Staircase ascends to Level 2 platforms (exits to the west)

**Surprise mechanics:**
- If party maintained stealth from the lava tube: **Surprise round** (Red Wizard flat-footed, party acts first)
- If combat was noisy or party is loud entering: **No surprise; Red Wizard is alert** but still performing his ritual action. Other four Red Wizards in adjacent chapels (areas 1, 3, 4, 6) do not immediately notice—they are focused on their own ritual chanting

## Encounter A – Black Chapel Red Wizard (Silent Kill)

**Type:** Combat (likely surprise round, tactical focus on the mage)

### Enemy: Red Wizard (Rath Modar's Subordinate)

*Role:* Central ritual caster. If killed before he completes 2 consecutive ritual actions, triggers portals collapse mechanic.

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| **AC** | 13 (arcane robes, no armor) |
| **HP** | 44 |
| **Speed** | 30 ft. |
| **STR/DEX/CON** | 10/11/13 |
| **INT/WIS/CHA** | 17/12/11 |
| **Saves** | INT +5, WIS +3 |
| **Spells** | Cantrips (3), 1st–6th level slots (4/3/3/3/2/1) |
| **CR** | 7 |

**Actions (combat turn only; does NOT perform ritual on his action turn):**
- **Multiattack:** 2× Dagger (+3, 1d4+1 piercing) — ritual performance means no full attack
- **Fireball (3/day, 5th level):** DC 16 DEX save, 8d6 fire, 60 ft range. **Does not use unless threatened in melee.**
- **Counterspell (reaction):** +5, 6th level slot available. Uses against disrupt spells only (Dispel Magic, etc.)

**Ritual Action (same turn as other Red Wizards):**
- At the end of Red Wizard's turn (after any combat actions), he performs a ritual action: *"The dark energy flows."* No attack roll. No save. If he is alive at the end of his turn AND not disrupted, he counts as 1 of 5 ritual-performing Wizards.

**Tactics:**
- First round (if surprised): cast Fireball at the party as his FIRST action (breaks stealth, but maximizes damage). Then move away.
- Round 2+: if in melee with a martial character, cast Counterspell to protect nearby allies. Otherwise: Fireballs or cantrips.
- **Does not flee.** He is fanatical. He will fight until 0 HP.

**Legendary Actions:** None (he is not a boss).

---

### Combat Sequence – Round 1

**Surprise round (if applicable):**
- Party acts before Red Wizard.
- Red Wizard is flat-footed. No concentration yet (ritual is passive until end-of-turn check).
- **Party objective:** kill or incapacitate the Red Wizard silently. If successful, the ritual loses 1 of 5 performers. No alarm is raised.

**If surprise is lost (loud entry or recent combat):**
- Red Wizard uses his first turn action to cast Fireball (centered on the party if clumped, or a defensible position near a wall). Then retreats 30 ft away.
- He finishes his turn with ritual action: *"The black energy flows."* Ritual counter +1.

**If Red Wizard dies in Round 1:**
- No ritual action that round.
- **Porter collapses check:** If the ritual loses 2 consecutive rounds of ritual actions (Red Wizard dead, and no replacement arrives), the portal collapses. Current: Round 1 has no ritual action.

---

### If Other Red Wizards Arrive

**When do they notice?**
- If the Black Chapel fight is loud (Fireball, combat shouts, Shatter spell), a Red Wizard in an adjacent chapel hears it at the end of Round 1 and investigates Round 2.
- **Per raw module:** Red Wizards are at areas 1 (Blue Chapel), 3 (White), 4 (Green), 6 (Red), and flying positions above (8–12). Adjacent to area 5 are areas 1 and 3 (Blue and White chapels, each ~100 ft away through intervening chambers).

**Arrival time:** 2 rounds of travel = Round 3 earliest.

**If they arrive:** Each new Red Wizard performs a ritual action. If 5+ Red Wizards are performing, ritual ticks forward (Clock +1).

## The Silent Victory Condition – Portal Collapse

**MAJOR weakening event (WotC module rule per temple-of-tiamat-raw.md:30):**

> *"If fewer than five Red Wizards perform the ritual for 2 rounds in succession, the portal collapses and the count of successfully focused rounds is reset to 0."*

**How this applies to Black Chapel emergence:**
- Black Chapel Red Wizard is killed silently in surprise round or Round 1.
- Round 1: Red Wizard is dead, no ritual action. Count = 0 ritual actions that round.
- Round 2: If NO replacement Red Wizard arrives, count = 0 ritual actions.
- **Result:** Ritual count resets to 0. Severin loses 2 rounds of progress. This is a **major strategic victory.**

**Narrative consequence:**
The party emerges into the temple and immediately cripples the ritual by 10 rounds of work. The starting clock remains at 0, but they have bought themselves *momentum*. Severin will know the Black Chapel wizard is dead. Rath Modar will know. The cult will be on alert.

## Round-by-Round Tactics for FIRST ROUND INSIDE TEMPLE

### Party Initiative Order (per party sheet)
1. **Twilight** (+8 initiative)
2. **Daxx** (+5 initiative)
3. **Axar** (+1 initiative)
4. **Frygtløs** (+2 initiative) — note: barbarian, will rage if attacking
5. **Red Wizard** (Red Wizard Mage, +1 initiative if awake, flat-footed if surprised)

### Round 1 Actions (Surprise Round if applicable)

**Twilight (acts first if surprised):**
- **Best move:** Counterspell if the Red Wizard tries to cast Fireball. Otherwise, Dissonant Whispers (DC 18 CHA save, 7d6 psychic, forces movement away). Red Wizard save: +2 CHA = auto-fail likely.
- **Alternative:** Cutting Words prepped for the Red Wizard's Counterspell reaction (next round).

**Daxx (acts second):**
- **Best move:** Volley using Dragon Slayer Longbow. 5 attacks (4 regular + 1 bonus from Hunter's Mark variant). vs. AC 13 (mage, no armor) = likely 4 hits = 4d8+4d10+2d6 (dragon slayer) ≈ 70 damage. Kills the mage in one action.
- **Alternative:** Action Surge + attack if needed.
- **Note:** Daxx's Scimitar of Speed is better for melee if the mage survives, but Volley ends the threat immediately.

**Axar (acts third):**
- **Best move if mage still alive:** Disintegrate (8d12+40 ≈ 92 damage, save DC 18). Kills the mage instantly.
- **Alternative:** Counterspell readied if the mage casts. Or Cone of Cold prepped for Round 2 reinforcements.
- **Note:** Axar's Evoker Focus allows Arcana check (DC 13) to discern the ritual's structure and confirm 5-focus weakness. If he makes the check: "You sense five focal points of magical energy — each one is a Red Wizard maintaining the ritual. The ritual cannot continue without at least five."

**Frygtløs (acts fourth):**
- **Best move:** Hold Reaction for the mage's Fireball. If it comes, Rage (resistance to fire) and close to melee. Otherwise, use Attack action to move within striking distance.
- **Alternative:** If the mage is still alive after Daxx/Axar acts, Frygtløs has a clear swing with Vorpal Greatsword (±11 to hit, 2d6+11 ≈ 24 damage base, plus Vorpal escalation). Likely kills on hit given the mage's low AC.

**Red Wizard (if surprised, cannot act in surprise round):**
- **If NOT surprised:** Uses his first action to cast Fireball (8d6, DC 16 DEX save, 60 ft range). Targets the party's likely position near the stairs or center of the chapel. Then moves 30 ft away (likely toward the ritual channels or an exit).

### Round 1 Outcome (Most Likely)

**The Red Wizard dies in surprise round or early Round 1.**
- Daxx's Volley or Axar's Disintegrate kills him before he acts.
- **Ritual action:** No ritual action from the Black Chapel wizard this round.
- **Clock:** No change yet (Round 1 is zero).

**Axar's Arcana Check (DC 13):**
- Success: Party understands the five-focus system. They know interrupting the ritual for 2 rounds resets it to 0.
- Failure: They just killed a wizard. They'll learn the structure as the ritual unfolds.

## The Procession – Conditional Encounter

**Original Procession:** 20–30 cultists marching to be sacrificed.

**Status per canonical state (tiamat-finale-plan.md):**
- **Sacrifice ritual at plaza NOT started.**
- Therefore: **The Procession has NOT yet left the slave pens (areas 16–18).**

**Where does the party encounter it?**

### Option 1: En route to the temple (most dramatic)
- Party is ascending the ritual channels or moving through Level 2 when they hear singing below.
- They can choose to investigate, ignore, or intercept.
- **Scene:** Cultists emerging from a side passage, singing, moving toward the central apse.

### Option 2: At the central apse (highest stakes)
- Party reaches Level 2 or Level 3 and sees the Procession *in real time* being sacrificed by 5 chromatic dragons at the plaza (area 23 above, visible from the temple's high points).
- **This forces moral choice:** Do they rescue the prisoners or pursue the ritual?

### Option 3: Skipped entirely (if party moves fast)
- If the party kills the Black Chapel Red Wizard silently and immediately ascends, they skip the Procession entirely.
- Party-facing consequence: The ritual gains 1 round's worth of sacrificial power. **Clock +1 automatically.**

**Recommended:** Use **Option 1 (encounter en route)** as a natural pause point between Black Chapel and Level 2. It mirrors the original Procession encounter's moral weight.

## Three Possible Clock Outcomes from Black Chapel Emergence

### Outcome A – Best (Silent Kill + No Procession)
- **Red Wizard killed in surprise round, no spellcasting alarm**
- **Party ignores or skips the Procession entirely**
- **Ritual clock remains at 0**
- **Consequence:** Severin is unaware until the party reaches Level 2. The ritual has not gained progress. Party is at full resource (mostly).

**Narrative:** *"The black chapel falls silent. The portal above pulses, but it waits. Severin does not yet know."*

### Outcome B – Medium (Ritual Continues + Some Procession Slowdown)
- **Red Wizard killed after casting Fireball; alarm is raised**
- **Other Red Wizard arrives and performs ritual; Clock ticks to +1**
- **Party stops or slows the Procession via Persuasion or combat; Clock ticks back to 0 or –1**
- **Net: Ritual clock remains at 0–1**

**Narrative:** *"The ritual's pulse accelerates — then falters as the Procession breaks. For a moment, the temple holds its breath."*

### Outcome C – Worst (Ritual Accelerates + Procession Completes)
- **Red Wizard kills/dominates the party (rare), or**
- **Party is forced to flee without fully disrupting the Black Chapel**
- **Procession completes, sacrifices begin**
- **Ritual clock jumps to +2 or +3**

**Narrative:** *"The screaming begins above. The five colors in the portal brighten. Severin's voice echoes through the temple: 'It is time.'"*

## DM Notes & Campaign Adjustments

### If the Red Wizard Escapes
- **Very unlikely** (he has no Misty Step, no flight, low AC).
- If he somehow survives combat and tries to flee via the ritual channels: he can move 30 ft per turn. Party can pursue or cast Hold Person / Counterspell.
- **Do not let him reach the central apse alive.** He is a solo encounter with no backup. The party's combined resources should end him.

### If Party Takes Excessive Damage
- **Healing potions:** Party has 6 Superior (8d4+8 avg 28 HP) + 12 Greater (4d4+4 avg 14 HP) available.
- **Next long rest:** None. Combat is continuous into Phase B. DM must calibrate future encounters with resource depletion in mind.
- **Break-glass option:** If party HP drops below 150 combined before leaving Black Chapel, a metallic dragon ally (Elia, the bronze dragon) can be heard circling above the caldera. She is not fighting YET, but her presence is felt. Saves this card for Phase C.

### Naergoth Callback
- If the party killed Naergoth silently in area 7, Severin's pre-combat line (from tiamat-finale-plan.md §59) applies: *"I dræbte Naergoth. Den gamle hund stod i den port i syv hundrede år. Han åbnede med 'velkommen'. Jeres kæmpe svarede med stål."*
- If the party did NOT kill Naergoth (unlikely but possible), Naergoth may be wandering the temple grounds. His sudden appearance in the Procession or on Level 2 is a dramatic beat.

### Axar's Arcana Window
- **DC 13 Arcana check during or after Black Chapel fight:** Party understands the five-Wizard ritual requirement.
- **DC 15 Arcana check on the ritual channels themselves:** Party learns that each channel corresponds to a focus (Black = area 5, Red = area 6, etc.).
- **DC 17 Arcana check on the portal above:** Party learns that the portal can be collapsed if fewer than 5 Wizards focus for 2 rounds in succession. This is the strategic goal.

### Frygtløs's Moment (Deferred)
- **If NOT triggered in the Procession:** The moment can resurface when a wounded Red Wizard begs for mercy on Level 2 or Level 3.
- **Per tiamat-finale-plan.md §209:** *"plant it as a possible moment inside the temple. Don't force it."*
- **Allow it to happen naturally** if Frygtløs's player gravitates toward the choice.

### Party Morale & Pacing
- **Black Chapel encounter:** 10–15 minutes of combat + roleplay.
- **Procession encounter (if triggered):** 5–10 minutes of moral discussion.
- **Transition to Level 2:** 2–3 minutes of description.
- **Total Phase A time at this point:** ~25–30 minutes. Party should be on Level 2 by minute 30–35 of the session.
