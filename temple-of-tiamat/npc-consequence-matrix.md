# NPC Consequence Matrix – Three Victory Tiers

Maps the fate of key NPCs across Full Victory, Pyrrhic Victory, and Failure-Forward states.

---

## Full Victory (Clock 0–3 at Severin's death)

| NPC | Status | Aftermath Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Ulder Ravengard** | Emerges from temple bloodied but alive. Takes a blade through the shoulder as the archway collapses. | Takes command of reorganized Flaming Fist. By autumn, Waterdeep's military is rebuilt. His final act: write a letter to the party praising them by name. |
| **Remallia Haventree** | Survives the frontline. Finds her scouts in the rubble. Wept when the portal closed. | Becomes the Harper-liaison to the Council of Waterdeep permanently. Establishes a memorial garden in Leosin's honor at Harper headquarters. |
| **Ontharr Frume** | Alive, but one of his Order's chapters suffered heavy losses. He is visibly moved by the victory. | Rebuilds the Order of the Gauntlet as a peacekeeping force. Mentors younger knights personally. His first sermon after the victory: gratitude without pride. |
| **Elia (Bronze Dragon)** | Flies out of the temple, smoking slightly. Left wing scorched but functional. She lands safely outside the caldera. | Establishes a draconic council with other metallic dragons in the Sword Coast region. Visits the party one year later to acknowledge the debt. |
| **Flinx (Fairy familiar, if player has one)** | Unharmed; rode out the collapse on a shoulder. Speaks for the first time in weeks: *"We did it."* | Becomes a link between the party and the Feywild. Brings word that the Summer Court is aware of what happened. |
| **Nyh Ilmichh (Thayan liaison, if invited)** | Escapes via Teleportation Circle. Sends a formal letter of gratitude to the party. | Becomes a key figure in reestablishing diplomatic relations between Thay and the sword Coast. An unlikely alliance forms. |
| **Any party-raised companion (e.g., Fen)** | Survives and is present at the exit. Gratitude is visible. | Becomes part of the party's permanent retinue if the party wishes. If Fen: becomes an apprentice to Axar or seeks training with the Harpers. |

## Pyrrhic Victory (Clock 4–7 at Severin's death; Tiamat partially manifested)

| NPC | Status | Aftermath Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Ulder Ravengard** | Emerges from the temple bleeding from multiple wounds. One eye is damaged (half-blind thereafter). His command tent was partially overrun. | Hands over formal command to a younger officer (Ontharr or equivalent). Retires to oversee fortifications. He writes a will leaving military authority and personal effects to the party, in case he doesn't survive the next "catastrophe." He lives for exactly two more years. |
| **Remallia Haventree** | Emerges with half her scouting force lost. She is quiet, calculating, and angry. Several Harpers died in the frontline collapse. | Works tirelessly to rebuild the Harper network and prevent future infiltrations. By winter, the network is stronger but paranoid. She never fully trusts the Thayan alliance again. |
| **Ontharr Frume** | Emerges from his command post unscathed but visibly older. Many Order of the Gauntlet knights did not survive the final push. | Declares a period of mourning and rededication. The Order becomes more militant, more focused. Within a year, the Order breaks its historical neutrality and sides openly with the Lords' Alliance against Thay. |
| **Elia (Bronze Dragon)** | Takes massive damage during the Tiamat manifestation. Her right wing is badly burned; she can still fly but is weakened. Retreats to her lair in the Moonshae Isles for a year of recovery. | The party does not see her again for 18 months. When she returns, she is fully healed but changed—more cautious, less hopeful. She owes the party a debt, but it is complicated by the knowledge that Tiamat walked the world for six hours before being sealed again. |
| **Ontharr/Remallia (replacement if above die)** | One senior NPC takes a mortal wound and dies in the first weeks after the victory. Their last words: *"It was worth the cost."* | The surviving NPCs dedicate the next phase of the campaign to honoring the dead. A monument is raised in Waterdeep's harbor district. |
| **Nyh Ilmichh (Thayan liaison)** | Survives but is visibly shaken by Tiamat's manifestation. Thay's confidence in its control of the situation is shattered. The Red Wizards abandon further dragon-cult projects. | Becomes a valuable informant about Thayan politics and Tiamat theology. A secret alliance forms between the party and certain Thayan factions to prevent similar rituals. |
| **Any party-raised companion (e.g., Fen)** | Survives but is traumatized by seeing Tiamat's heads. If Fen: he has nightmares for weeks. He speaks of colors that don't exist and sounds that echo. | Becomes a valuable witness to the ritual mechanics. If the party brings him to the Harpers, he becomes a resource for understanding cult psychology. He eventually becomes a healer or scholar, not a warrior. |

## Failure-Forward (Clock 8; Tiamat fully manifests and escapes)

| NPC | Status | Aftermath Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Ulder Ravengard** | Dies defending the collapsed archway. His last stand buys the party time to escape. His final words to a knight: *"Tell Waterdeep they were heroes. Make sure someone remembers."* | Becomes a legend. A statue is erected in Waterdeep. The Flaming Fist is reorganized under a less experienced commander, which creates political instability. |
| **Remallia Haventree** | Survives but loses most of her scouts and many Harpers. She is visibly shaken and angry. She blames the party for not stopping the ritual sooner (unfairly, but that is her grief talking). | She survives the campaign but never fully trusts the party again. She becomes focused on hunting down remaining cult cells across Faerûn. By the time the party meets her again, she has aged visibly. |
| **Ontharr Frume** | Dies in the collapse, crushed by falling stone while trying to protect fleeing soldiers. His body is never recovered. | The Order of the Gauntlet fragments into factions, each claiming to embody his legacy. A schism threatens the organization for the next two years. |
| **Elia (Bronze Dragon)** | Dies in the Tiamat manifestation, struck by one of the chromatic heads. Her death is sudden and brutal. The party may witness it as they flee. | Her death becomes a rallying cry. Other metallic dragons openly declare war on Tiamat and her remaining cults. This shifts the political landscape of the world—draconic involvement becomes overt. |
| **Ravengard's successor** | A younger, less experienced commander takes over the Flaming Fist. He is competent but lacks Ravengard's gravitas and political connections. | The Flaming Fist becomes less coordinated. Waterdeep's defenses are compromised. The party must deal with a weakened ally for the remainder of the campaign. |
| **Nyh Ilmichh (Thayan liaison)** | Dies during the frontline collapse or is reported missing. If missing: she is captured by Tiamat's cultists and enslaved. If she reappears later, she is no longer the same person. | If dead: Thay severs the alliance entirely and withdraws support, leaving the party without eastern resources. If captured: her rescue (or attempted rescue) becomes a side quest in a later phase of the campaign. |
| **Any party-raised companion (e.g., Fen)** | Dies in the temple collapse or is sacrificed during Tiamat's manifestation. If Fen dies, his last image in the party's mind is his face in the crowd as Tiamat's mouth opens. | His death becomes a point of emotional weight for the party. The aftermath includes a memorial scene and character-specific grief beats. Frygtløs (who may have encountered Fen earlier) bears particular weight. |

## Nuances & DM Notes

### NPC Emotional Weight

**Full Victory:** NPCs are grateful and relieved. They speak of a new age dawning. Their words are hopeful but acknowledge the cost.

**Pyrrhic Victory:** NPCs are grateful but haunted. They know Tiamat walked the world. There is an undertone of *"We were lucky"* beneath the gratitude. Some NPCs become more paranoid or militant.

**Failure-Forward:** NPCs are grieving and determined. The loss is immense. The party hears about rebuilding efforts and memorial services. The world feels darker.

### Timing of Consequences

- **Immediate (next session):** NPC arrivals, initial reactions, emergency healing/shelter
- **Short-term (1–4 weeks in-game):** Reorganization, political shifts, formal declarations
- **Mid-term (2–6 months in-game):** Rebuilding initiatives, new alliances formed, first ripples of world change
- **Long-term (1+ year in-game):** Monuments raised, schisms healed or deepened, new eras defined

### Using This Matrix at the Table

1. **Before the session:** Review which NPC consequences apply to your victory tier
2. **During epilogue scenes:** Reference specific details (Ravengard's eye damage in Pyrrhic, Elia's burns, etc.)
3. **After the battle:** Use NPC letters and one-liners (from aftermath.md) to reinforce the emotional beats
4. **In future sessions:** Reference NPCs by their new status (Remallia as the paranoid Harper leader, Ontharr as the militant zealot, etc.)

### Customization

If you have added NPCs or changed the alliance composition:
- **For new NPCs in Full Victory tier:** Give them a role in rebuilding (city guard commander, scholar at Candlekeep, etc.)
- **For new NPCs in Pyrrhic tier:** Give them a wound or loss (lost an eye, lost a limb, lost a close ally)
- **For new NPCs in Failure-Forward:** Make their death or capture the emotional pivot point of that tier
