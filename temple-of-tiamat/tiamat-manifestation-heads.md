# Tiamat Manifestation Progression & Head Mechanics

> **Tiamat is not a traditional boss statblock.** She is a divine presence that escalates throughout the finale. This document covers her manifestation stages (Ritual Clock 0–8), five independent heads with separate attack mechanics, lair actions, divine resistances, and the strategic landscape of the final battle.

---

## I. Manifestation Stages & Sensory Progression

### Clock 0: The Arrival
**Mechanical effect:** None active yet. The party has just entered the temple.

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Templet åbner sig som et sår i klippen. Luften smager af aske, blod og røgelse. Stenene selv prøver at trække vejret i takt med ritualets lys. Fem farver — sort, blå, grøn, rød, hvid — pulserer som et hjerte uden krop. Severin står uberørt ved portalen, armene løftet, mens skyggerne fra dragehoveder allerede glider hen over væggene. Intet fjendtligt bevæger sig. Alting venter.

**What the party senses:**
- Metallic taste in the mouth (blood and copper)
- Draconic runes along the walls glow faintly
- All dragons within 100 miles cry out in unison (now past, but echoing)
- Weightlessness to the air—gravity feels negotiable
- Faint breathing sound, not from any visible source

**Severin's state:** Untouched. Confident. +0 to saves/attacks.

---

### Clock 1: The First Shadow
**Mechanical effect:** The first head's shadow becomes visible in the portal. Nonmagical sensations only.

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Bag Severin åbner portalen sig som et øje, der løfter sit låg. Intet kommer gennem. Kun skyggerne. De er større end muligt. Et drage-hoved — størrelse ubestemmelig — trækkes igennem som gennem tåge. Det er ikke der. Men det *kan* ses. Og mens du ser på det, føler du dig *set af det*.
>
> Dine hænder ryster. Blodtasten intensiveres. De fem lysfarver stiger i styrke.

**What the party senses:**
- One massive head-shaped shadow presses against an invisible veil in the portal
- The shadow does not move naturally—it writhes, twists, tests the barrier
- A voice (not heard, but *felt* through the skull): wordless, ancient, hungry
- The party's heartbeat synchronizes with the portal's pulsing
- Spell slots feel *heavier* (flavor only—no mechanical change yet)

**Severin's state:** More animated. Begins chanting. Still fully vulnerable.

**Tiamat's will:** She is aware of the party now. One head focuses its attention downward.

---

### Clock 2: Chromatic Intensity
**Mechanical effect:** All spells dealing elemental damage (fire, cold, lightning, acid, poison) gain +1d4 damage.

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Lyset i templet skifter farve — én gang hver anden sekund. Så sort som molybden, så blå som højde, så grønt som forråd, så rødt som blod, så hvidt som luft uden ende. Hver farve smerter at se på. Hver farve har vægt.
>
> Severin skriger noget — ikke på dansk, ikke på dansk. Det er Drakonisk, men ørene forstår det ikke. Hjernet gør.
>
> Den anden skygge begynder at presse gennem portalen.

**What the party senses:**
- A second massive shadow joins the first
- Two distinct draconic presences now focus downward
- The air shimmers and ripples like heat-haze, but it's cold
- Each color of light leaves a faint afterimage (chromatic blindness creeping in)
- The ritual's sound becomes a harmony—multiple voices in a language that predates speech

**Severin's state:** Ecstatic. +1 to all saves and attack rolls (Champion of Tiamat, partial).

**Ritual clock increase:** Most likely to increase now — the party must act.

---

### Clock 3: The Frontline Breaks
**Mechanical effect:** Outside the temple, the allied army's front line breaks at one point. The party hears this as distant screams, cracking stone, and the roar of chromatic dragons.

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Fra verden uden for templet høres det — ikke som en lydende begivenhed, men som et følelse gennem klippen. En krystallinsk høj-lydighed. Tusinder af stemmer som én. Et drage-skrig som tordenskjold, der slår ned.
>
> Imens inden i templet pulserer tre skygger nu. Den tredje hoved — dens øjne rødt som glødende sten — fokuserer ned mod jer.

**What the party senses:**
- Three massive shadows press through the veil
- One head's eyes are visibly *red* and lidless
- A psychic **wave** passes through the area (WIS save DC 18): 
  - On success: immunity to fear for 1 round
  - On failure: Frightened of Tiamat's presence for 1 round (–1 to attacks and ability checks)
- The three heads begin to *speak*—not to the party, but to Severin, and the party hears only fragments

**Severin's state:** Champion of Tiamat now fully active: +2 to saves and attacks, resistance to all damage except bludgeoning.

**Each head begins to turn its attention individually:**
- **Black head:** Focuses on the strongest-looking warrior (Frygtløs by default)
- **Blue head:** Focuses on the spellcaster with the most powerful aura (Axar by default)
- **Green head:** Focuses on whoever shows the most cunning or deception (Twilight by default)
- **Red head:** Focuses on whoever killed the most cultists (Daxx or the party's highest damage dealer)
- **White head:** Turns its hunger toward the smallest, fastest target

---

### Clock 4: The First Head Emerges
**Mechanical effect:** One head physically presses halfway through the portal. It can now attack (see Head Mechanics, below).

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Portalen splitter sig. Fire skygger vipper til side, og den femte — rød som en smeltet kniv — begynder at *trækkes* gennem.
>
> Dens mund åbner sig. Tænder så store som sværd grinder mod hverandres kanter. Øjet, der ser jer, er ikke øjet på en drage. Det er øjet på en gud, der fortviler over at være fanget i et hulefald af kød og ben.
>
> "Jeg **kommer**," siger den ikke med ord, men det ord er overalt, fra overalt, i jer.

**What the party senses:**
- **One head is now visibly physical** — scales touch the material plane, breath weapons visible
- Extreme heat radiates from the Red head, or extreme cold from the White head (depending on which emerges first based on DM choice)
- A **3d6 zone of damage** (fire or cold, depending on head) extends 30 feet around the portal, centered on the head
- The head speaks directly to the party for the first time (see "Head Speaks Moments," Section VII)

**Mechanical change:**
- That one head can make one bite attack per round (+14 to hit, 4d6+8 piercing damage)
- The head has **AC 17** and regenerates 10 HP per round while any Red Wizard is alive and chanting
- Damage to that head is visible — the other heads *react* to their sibling's pain

**Severin's state:** Desperate. Uses Ritual Action twice per turn now (if available). Begins calling for reinforcements.

---

### Clock 5: Severin's Apotheosis
**Mechanical effect:** Severin becomes **Champion of Tiamat** fully: +2 to all saves and attacks, resistance to all damage except bludgeoning/slashing.

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Severin løfter begge hænder til himlen — eller hvad der skal være himlen. Hans øjne bliver hvide. Hans hud begynder at skinnetre med chromate farver. Han skriger noget der kunne være triumf eller agoni.
>
> "Se mig!" råber han. "Se mig som **valgt**!"
>
> To hoveder — sort og hvidt — begynder nu at trykke gennem portalen. Deres andedrag mødes i midten af rummet og danner en sky af is og sod.

**What the party senses:**
- Two more heads emerge (typically Black and White, representing complementary destruction)
- A **50-foot cone of mixed breath** (acid + cold, or fire + poison) erupts from the portal once per turn (on a surprise round if the party is not ready)
- Severin now has **300 HP instead of 230**, glows with divine power, and no longer appears humanoid

**Mechanical change:**
- Severin cannot be moved by forced movement (he is anchored to Tiamat's will)
- Any attack against Severin within 10 feet of a manifested head deals half damage
- Severin gains one additional Legendary Action per round: **Divine Conduit** (he teleports up to 30 feet)

**Party morale check:** This is the moment players often say "Oh, shit." DM should emphasize the *wrong-ness* of what Severin has become.

---

### Clock 6: Three Heads Attack (Lair Actions Begin)
**Mechanical effect:** Three heads are now half-manifested and can attack. **Lair actions begin.** The temple itself becomes a weapon.

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Tre dragehoveder — sort, hvidt, blåt — fylder nu helt halvrummet af portalen. De kæmper om pladsen, og deres bevægelser får templet til at skælve.
>
> En søjle af ben krumler og falder. En alterkrystal eksploderer i hvidt lys. Ritualets farvelysse splintrer og omleder — og i hvert hjørne af templet åbner små porte sig. Dæmoner, ikke fra Avernus men fra Tiamat selv — dele af hende, der altid var her — begynder at krybre ud af spalterne.
>
> Grønt hoved — det med løgnens tunge — presser sig gennem. Dets stemme fylde hele rummet, og hver ord er en løgn der føles som sandhed.

**Lair actions (Tiamat takes 3 lair actions per round, used at initiative 20, losing ties):**

1. **Bone Cascade:** Choose one creature the party can see. It must make a DEX save DC 20 or take 4d6 bludgeoning damage as bone pillars collapse. On success, it takes half damage and is not knocked prone.

2. **Chromatic Vortex:** A 20-foot radius sphere of chaotic color appears at a point Tiamat can see within 60 feet. Each creature in that area must make a WIS save DC 18 or be Stunned until the end of its next turn.

3. **Ritual Surge:** Severin regains 30 HP (if below max). If all five heads are manifested, the ritual clock increases by 1 immediately.

4. **Draconic Summon:** Tiamat summons 1d4 Barbed Devils or 2d6 Cultist Fanatics in unoccupied spaces she can see within 60 feet. These act on initiative 10 at the start of the next round.

5. **Suffocating Presence:** All creatures within 30 feet of a manifested head must make a CON save DC 20 or take 2d10 psychic damage as they feel the weight of Tiamat's attention.

**Three heads attack:**
- Black head: bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 2d6 acid
- White head: bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 2d6 cold
- Blue head: bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 1d10 lightning (can recharge on 5–6; bite then triggers a 60-foot-long lightning line: DEX save DC 20, 5d6 lightning on failure, half on success)

**Manifestation vulnerability:** Three heads are now partially manifested. They each have **75 HP** (see Section III for splitting damage).

**Severin's state:** Still +2 to saves/attacks, but now can use Ritual Surge once per round as a bonus action (doesn't reduce ritual clock if successful).

---

### Clock 7: Severin's Immortality
**Mechanical effect:** Severin becomes **unkillable** while any head is partially or fully manifested. He cannot be reduced below 3 HP and regains that much at the start of each of his turns.

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Fire dragehoved nu — sort, hvidt, blåt, rødt. Kun det grønne mangler.
>
> Severin står og ler. Det lyder ikke som menneskelig latter. Det lyder som drage-blod, der lyder gennem menneskelig stemme.
>
> Hans øjne åbner sig *bagud*, og I ser at hans øjne ikke længere er hans. De er *hendes*. Han er ikke længere værten.
>
> Han er alteret.

**What the party senses:**
- Four massive draconic heads fill the upper half of the temple
- A **divine aura** surrounds Severin: 10-foot radius sphere. Any creature that ends its turn there must make a DEX save DC 20 or take 2d10 radiant + 2d10 necrotic damage (mixed light and shadow)
- The Black, White, Blue, and Red heads can each take **one attack action per round**
- Lair actions increase to **4 per round**

**Party realization:** Severin cannot be killed. The focus must shift to destroying the foci, sealing the portal, or stopping the ritual entirely.

**Four heads attack:**
- Black head: bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 2d6 acid + **special** (on hit, the target must make a CON save DC 18 or take 2d6 additional acid damage at the start of each of its turns for 1 minute; a creature can repeat the save at the end of each of its turns)
- White head: bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 2d6 cold; **Frigid Aura** (on hit, the target's speed is reduced by 20 feet until the end of its next turn)
- Blue head: bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 1d10 lightning; **Recharge 5–6** (on hit, the creature is knocked prone if it's Large or smaller)
- Red head: bite +14, 4d6+8 piercing + 3d6 fire; **Frightening Roar** (on hit, the target must make a WIS save DC 20 or be Frightened until the end of its next turn)

**Damage spread:** Attacks from any of the four heads are now serious threats. The party must choose: focus fire on one head, or defend?

---

### Clock 8: Full Manifestation — Divine Catastrophe
**Mechanical effect:** Tiamat manifests fully. The campaign enters **"Survival/Redirection" mode.**

**Boxed Text (Danish):**
> Portalen eksploderer.
>
> Fem dragehoved — sort, hvidt, blåt, rødt, grønt — tager nu plads der skulle være luft. De passerer gennem virkelighedens tæppe som det ikke eksisterer. Deres skuldre følger. Deres vinger begynder at folde ud.
>
> Ikke som en drage. Som fem drager der kun er dele af *en* væsen.
>
> Tiamat er her.
>
> Og verden er ikke længere større end hendes skygge.

**What the party senses:**
- **Reality warps.** Gravity inverts in some areas, then returns. Time seems to stutter.
- **All five heads fully present.** Each can attack independently. Lair actions become **5 per round**.
- **Severin ceases to be relevant.** He is devoured by Tiamat on her first turn after full manifestation (unless the party has already killed him, in which case Tiamat consumes his corpse and regains 100 HP).
- **A psychic shockwave** radiates from Tiamat (60-foot radius, centered on the portal):
  - WIS save DC 20
  - On failure: Frightened, Paralyzed, or Stunned (party's choice of effect for their own narrative arc) until the end of the next round
  - On success: immunity to fear from Tiamat for 1 round

**Manifestation state:**
- **AC 17** (all heads together; attacking separate heads grants no AC bonus, but see Section III)
- **Hit point maximum:** ~500 HP total across all heads (or 75–100 HP per head; see Section III)
- **Regeneration:** 25 HP per round (if the ritual is still ongoing)
- **Legendary actions:** 5 per round, chosen from the list below
- **Legendary resistances:** 3/day (recharge dawn) — she automatically succeeds on a save, a save DC increases by 10 for 1 round, or she gains +5 to AC until the start of her next turn

**Tiamat's five all-five-manifest actions (any mix per round):**

1. **Multiattack:** She makes up to 5 bite attacks (one per head).
2. **Bite (any head):** +14 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Damage varies by head (see Section II).
3. **Breath weapon (any head):** Choose one head. That head exhales (see Section II for damage/range).
4. **Sweeping Lair Action:** Tiamat uses a lair action (see Section IV).
5. **Legendary Resistance:** Automatically succeeds on a save.

**Victory conditions shift now:**
- **Destroy all five masks** before Tiamat becomes *fully* operational (8 rounds)
- **Collapse the portal** by removing the Mask of the Dragon Queen (requires a second player to grab it and move it 120+ feet from Severin's corpse)
- **Banish Tiamat back to Avernus** (requires Arcana DC 25 to even attempt; Axar can do this if he had relevant spells/knowledge from earlier)
- **Survive long enough for allied forces to seal the Well** (the dwarves have demolition charges; if the party can hold for 10 rounds, the Well's structure collapses and forces Tiamat back)

## II. The Five Heads: Independent Combat Mechanics

Each head has its own personality, attack pattern, and special mechanic. **They share one massive HP pool OR each has a separate 75–100 HP pool.** (Recommendation: separate pools per head = 75 HP each = 375 total Tiamat HP, plus Severin's 230 = 605 total encounter HP at Clock 8.)

### Black Head – Tiamat of Decay and Contempt

**Personality:** Contemptuous, mocking, ancient. Speaks to the party about their inevitable failure. Constantly insults. "You are insects in an anthill I will not even look at once I am through."

**AC:** 17 | **HP:** 75 (per head) | **Regeneration:** 10 HP/round (while ritual ongoing)

**Bite:** +14 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 4d6+8 (27) piercing + 2d6 (7) acid damage.

**Special – Acidic Aura:** Any creature that ends its turn within 10 feet of the Black head must make a DEX save DC 18 or take 1d6 acid damage.

**Legendary Action (1/turn, usable only during Tiamat's turn if Black head is manifested):**
- **Acid Spray (2 actions):** The Black head targets up to three creatures within 30 feet. Each must make a DEX save DC 20 or take 3d6 acid damage. On success, takes half.

**Breath Weapon – Acid Breath:** 60-foot line, 8 feet wide. DEX save DC 20. 8d6 (28) acid damage on failure, half on success. **Recharge:** 5–6.

**Tiamat's goals (Black head focus):** Break the party's frontline defender. Corrode magic items. Dissolve confidence through relentless mockery.

**If destroyed:** Tiamat can no longer use Acid Breath or Acidic Aura. All acid hazards in the arena are reduced by 50%. Tiamat's AC increases by 1 (she has lost the protective acid film around her scales).

---

### Blue Head – Tiamat of Control and Superiority

**Personality:** Authoritative, commanding, superior. Speaks of order and hierarchy. "You have always known you are beneath me. Now you feel it."

**AC:** 17 | **HP:** 75 | **Regeneration:** 10 HP/round

**Bite:** +14 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 4d6+8 (27) piercing + 1d10 (5) lightning damage.

**Special – Commanding Presence:** Any creature of Tiamat's choice that starts its turn within 30 feet of the Blue head and can see it must make a WIS save DC 18 or be unable to use reactions until the end of its turn.

**Legendary Action (1/turn):**
- **Thunderous Rebuke (2 actions):** One creature the Blue head can see within 30 feet must make a STR save DC 20 or be knocked prone and pushed 10 feet away.

**Breath Weapon – Lightning Breath:** 90-foot line, 10 feet wide. DEX save DC 20. 11d6 (38) lightning damage on failure, half on success. All creatures in the area are knocked back 5 feet. **Recharge:** 5–6.

**Tiamat's goals (Blue head focus):** Control the battlefield. Isolate a spellcaster. Enforce surrender through overwhelming authority.

**If destroyed:** Tiamat loses the Commanding Presence aura. The Lightning Breath recharge increases (6 only). Tiamat's initiative is reduced by 2 on each of her turns.

---

### Green Head – Tiamat of Lies, Poison, and Illusion

**Personality:** Seductive, deceptive, manipulative. Speaks in riddles and false truths. "Everything you believe is a lie, and I am the only truth."

**AC:** 17 | **HP:** 75 | **Regeneration:** 10 HP/round

**Bite:** +14 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 4d6+8 (27) piercing + 2d6 (7) poison damage.

**Special – Poisonous Cloud:** When the Green head uses its breath weapon (see below), the area is difficult terrain and filled with toxic mist until the start of Tiamat's next turn. Any creature that starts its turn in the mist must make a CON save DC 18 or take 1d6 poison damage.

**Legendary Action (1/turn):**
- **Mirage (1 action):** The Green head casts *Mirror Image* (as if cast with a 9th-level slot). Each mirage lasts until the end of Tiamat's next turn. It can create up to 3 mirages. (This is flavor and rules-light: reduce Green head AC by 2 per mirage, max 3 mirages, until dispelled by magic or the next attack hits a mirage.)

**Breath Weapon – Poison Breath:** 60-foot cone. CON save DC 20. 10d6 (35) poison damage on failure, half on success. Creatures that fail become Poisoned (disadvantage on all ability checks and attacks) until the start of Tiamat's next turn. **Recharge:** 5–6.

**Tiamat's goals (Green head focus):** Sow distrust. Poison the party's confidence in their perceptions. Make the party question whether they are really winning.

**If destroyed:** Tiamat loses the Poisonous Cloud aura and Mirage ability. Poison Breath damage decreases to 6d6 (21). The party gains +1 to WIS saves against fear/illusion for the remainder of the battle.

---

### Red Head – Tiamat of Fury, Majesty, and Dominion

**Personality:** Wrathful, majestic, overwhelming. Speaks of conquest and power. "Your defiance is noted. It will be punished."

**AC:** 17 | **HP:** 75 | **Regeneration:** 10 HP/round

**Bite:** +14 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 4d6+8 (27) piercing + 3d6 (10) fire damage.

**Special – Frightening Presence:** Any creature that starts its turn within 30 feet of the Red head and can see it must make a WIS save DC 20 or be Frightened until the end of its turn.

**Legendary Action (1/turn):**
- **Inferno (3 actions):** The Red head's next breath weapon deals double damage and affects a 90-foot radius instead of a line/cone (centered on the head).

**Breath Weapon – Fire Breath:** 90-foot line, 10 feet wide. DEX save DC 20. 13d6 (45) fire damage on failure, half on success. **Recharge:** 5–6.

**Tiamat's goals (Red head focus):** Maximize damage. Terrify the party. Make them *feel* her rage.

**If destroyed:** Tiamat loses the Frightening Presence aura. Fire Breath damage decreases to 8d6 (28). The party gains advantage on WIS saves against her fear effects for 1 round.

---

### White Head – Tiamat of Hunger, Instinct, and the Primal

**Personality:** Animalistic, primal, hungry. Does not speak in words—only in sensation, hunger, the drive to *consume*. It is the most alien head.

**AC:** 17 | **HP:** 75 | **Regeneration:** 10 HP/round

**Bite:** +14 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. Hit: 4d6+8 (27) piercing + 2d6 (7) cold damage. If the target is a creature and the hit total exceeds the target's AC by 5 or more, Tiamat can choose to grapple the target as well (escape DC 20).

**Special – Grapple & Swallow:** If the White head has a creature grappled and hits with its bite on the White head's next turn, the target must make a STR save DC 20 or be swallowed whole. A swallowed creature is blinded, restrained, and unable to breathe or speak. A creature can use an action to escape by making a STR save DC 20. The White head can have only one creature swallowed at a time. A creature is considered in the belly of the head for the purposes of *magic missile*, *spiritual weapon*, and other effects that target "a creature you can see."

**Legendary Action (1/turn):**
- **Hunger-Snap (1 action):** The White head makes a bite attack against a creature it can see within 15 feet.

**Breath Weapon – Cold Breath:** 60-foot cone. CON save DC 20. 9d6 (31) cold damage on failure, half on success. Creatures that fail are slowed (speed halved) until the start of Tiamat's next turn. **Recharge:** 5–6.

**Tiamat's goals (White head focus):** Grapple and consume a PC. Remove threats one by one. The most direct and physical of her heads.

**If destroyed:** Tiamat can no longer grapple or swallow. Cold Breath damage decreases to 6d6 (21). The slowed condition is removed from creatures already affected.

## III. Shared vs. Individual HP Mechanics

**Recommendation for the table:**

### Option A: Separate HP Pools Per Head (RECOMMENDED)
- Each head has **75 HP individually**
- Total Tiamat HP across all heads: **375 HP**
- Destroying a head removes that head's attacks and special abilities permanently
- A destroyed head leaves a gaping wound; Tiamat can still be fully manifested with fewer heads
- At Clock 8, if any head is destroyed before full manifestation, that head's slot does not manifest—she can only attack with 4 heads instead of 5
- **Advantage:** Tactically clear. Party can target weak heads. Victories feel incremental.
- **Disadvantage:** Can end the ritual faster than DM wants if party is efficient.

### Option B: One Massive HP Pool
- **Total Tiamat HP: 500–600 HP** (for level 15 party of 4, accounting for high resources)
- Heads are attack vectors, not separate entities
- Damage can be allocated to any head, but destroying a "head" (hitting it for critical damage) removes its attacks
- **Advantage:** Tiamat feels like one entity. No confusion about which head is which.
- **Disadvantage:** Less clarity. Can drag on too long.

### Tracking Damage:

**Option A example:** "The Black head takes 28 damage (you hit it perfectly with Fireball). It's at 47/75 HP. Its Acid Breath will still recharge next round."

**Option B example:** "You deal 28 damage. Tiamat is at 472/600 total. The Black head is damaged but still operational."

**Recommended for this campaign:** **Option A**. The party is tactically sharp and will appreciate the ability to target weak heads. It also rewards focus-fire tactics.

## IV. Tiamat's Lair Actions & Environmental Destruction

Lair actions occur **at initiative 20 (wins ties)** each round Tiamat is manifested. At Clock 6–7, she has **3 per round**. At Clock 8 (full manifestation), she has **5 per round**.

The DM chooses which actions to use, but they should escalate as the battle continues.

### Tier 1 Lair Actions (Clocks 6–7)

1. **Bone Cascade:** Choose a 20-foot square within 60 feet of Tiamat. All creatures in that area must make a DEX save DC 20 or take 4d6 bludgeoning damage and be knocked prone. On a successful save, the creature takes half damage and isn't knocked prone.

2. **Chromatic Vortex:** A sphere of swirling color appears at a point within 60 feet. Any creature that starts its turn within 15 feet of that point must make a WIS save DC 18 or be Stunned until the end of its next turn. The vortex persists for 1 minute.

3. **Ritual Surge:** Severin regains 30 HP (if below max). The ritual clock increases by 1.

4. **Draconic Summon:** Tiamat summons 1d4 Barbed Devils or 2d4 Red Wizard Mages in unoccupied spaces within 30 feet. They act on initiative 10 at the start of the next round.

5. **Suffocating Presence:** All creatures within 30 feet of Tiamat must make a CON save DC 20 or take 2d10 psychic damage as they feel the weight of her vast consciousness pressing down.

### Tier 2 Lair Actions (Clock 8+)

6. **Portal Expansion:** The portal expands. All creatures within 30 feet of the portal must make a STR save DC 20 or be pulled 15 feet closer to it. If a creature is pulled into the portal, it is transported to Avernus (automatic failure, TPK; don't use this unless you want consequences).

7. **Tiamat's Claw Crush:** Tiamat causes the temple's floor to rupture. Choose up to 3 creatures within 60 feet. Each must make a DEX save DC 20 or take 5d6 bludgeoning damage. If a creature fails by 5 or more, it falls into a 30-foot-deep chasm beneath the temple floor.

8. **Mask of Reality:** Reality warps. Choose one creature within 30 feet. It must make a WIS save DC 20 or suffer one of the following:
   - Disadvantage on the next attack roll or ability check it makes
   - It experiences a brief vision of the Nine Hells (psychic damage 2d10)
   - Its perception inverts; for 1 minute, it perceives the world upside-down (visual difficulty, but no mechanical effect unless they try to read/understand text)

9. **Draconic Herald:** The temple echoes with Tiamat's voice. Each creature within 60 feet that can hear her must make a WIS save DC 20 or be Frightened until the end of its next turn. On a success, the creature is immune to this effect for 1 minute.

10. **Overwhelm:** Tiamat makes an extra attack (from any head).

### Using Lair Actions Tactically:

- **Early battle (Clocks 6–7):** Use **Bone Cascade** and **Summoning** to pressure the party and slow their advance toward Severin.
- **Mid-battle:** Use **Chromatic Vortex** and **Suffocating Presence** to restrict the party's movement and spell casting.
- **If party is winning:** Use **Portal Expansion** to threaten total party death, forcing them to make hard choices.
- **If party is losing:** Use **Draconic Herald** and **Summons** to feel merciful, or pull back lair actions to let the party have a chance.

**Note on Counterspell:** Lair actions cannot be countered. They are manifestations of the arena itself, not spells.

## V. Divine Resistances & Immunities

### Damage Immunities & Resistances:

- **Immunity:** Bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from nonmagical weapons
  - *Exception:* If all five foci are destroyed before Clock 8, this immunity becomes **resistance** instead
- **Immunity:** Charmed, exhaustion, frightened, paralyzed, petrified, poisoned
- **Immunity:** Psychic damage (she channels it; instead, all creatures within 60 feet take 1d6 psychic damage)
- **Resistance:** Acid, cold, fire, lightning, necrotic, radiant damage (half damage from these types)
  - *Exception:* If foci are destroyed, remove the corresponding resistance (see Sabotage Effects below)

### Legendary Resistances:

Tiamat has **3 legendary resistances per day** (recharge at dawn or when the ritual resets to 0).

- **Use 1:** She automatically succeeds on a save she just failed.
- **Use 1:** She redirects an attack that would hit her to a creature within 30 feet she can see (that creature makes a DEX save DC 20 to avoid; Tiamat takes half damage instead).
- **Use 1:** She gains +5 to AC until the start of her next turn.

### Vulnerability (if all foci destroyed before Clock 8):

If the party successfully sabotages all five foci on Level 2 before reaching Level 3, Tiamat gains **vulnerability to one damage type** (party's choice, or DM's choice based on how the ritual was disrupted):
- Black foci destroyed → vulnerability to fire (the acid foci collapse, so the ritual's power channels through fire instead)
- Blue foci destroyed → vulnerability to psychic (the lightning structure fails, leaving her mind unguarded)
- Green foci destroyed → vulnerability to cold (the poisonous mist fades, leaving her scales brittle)
- Red foci destroyed → vulnerability to lightning (the fire cycle breaks, leaving her vulnerable to electrical shock)
- White foci destroyed → vulnerability to acid (the cold reserves deplete, leaving her belly exposed)

## VI. Anti-Party Divine Powers

These are effects that are *not* Tiamat's direct attacks, but manifestations of her divine wrath.

### Frightened Aura (Clock 3+)

All creatures within 60 feet of Tiamat that can see her must make a WIS save DC 20 at the start of their turns or be Frightened. A creature frightened by this aura has disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks while within the aura. On a successful save, the creature is immune to this aura for 1 minute.

### Psychic Assault (Clock 5+)

At the start of each of Tiamat's turns, she targets one creature she can see within 60 feet. That creature must make an INT save DC 20 or take 2d10 psychic damage. On a success, it takes half. This is *not* a legendary action—it happens automatically.

### Temporal Distortion (Clock 6+)

Time moves strangely near the portal. At the start of each round, roll initiative again for the party (not for Tiamat—she acts on 20). This represents reality warping making it hard to act in sequence. (Use only if you want chaos flavor; it is not required.)

### Reality Tears (Clock 7+)

Cracks in the arena appear as the boundaries between worlds thin. At the start of each round (after lair actions), choose one or two random creatures within 60 feet of the portal. Each must make a DEX save DC 20 or take 4d10 force damage. If a creature fails by 5 or more, it is transported to a pocket of the Abyss and must use an action to find its way back (effectively skips its next turn). It reappears in the arena at the start of the round after.

### Psychic Feedback (if ritual is interrupted but Tiamat is still manifested)

If the party successfully interrupts the ritual (all five Red Wizards killed or incapacitated in a single round), Tiamat screams. All creatures within 120 feet must make a WIS save DC 20 or take 4d10 psychic damage. Tiamat loses her Regeneration trait but gains +2 to all attack rolls and ability checks as her focus sharpens.

## VII. Head-Speaks Moments (Clock 2–5)

Each head speaks to a different PC, focusing on that character's deepest fears, greatest victories, or defining choices.

### When heads speak:

- **Clock 2:** One head speaks briefly (3–4 sentences)
- **Clock 3–4:** Two heads speak (each 4–5 sentences)
- **Clock 5:** All five heads speak simultaneously, creating dissonance

### The Black Head speaks to Frygtløs

**If Frygtløs has killed enemies:**
> *"Du slog først på Naergoth. Dit vold er musikk for mine øre. Jeg ser alle de liv du har taget, og jeg vil have dem — jeg skal have det samme, igen og igen, fra dig og din stamme, til dette rige er tomt for blod du ikke selv spildte."*

**If Frygtløs has shown mercy:**
> *"Svaghed. Du er sterkere enn noen annen, og du vil aldri bruke det. Du er som min søster — hun som kunne ha hersket og valgte ikke. Hun døde i fornedrelse."*

**Visual:** The Black head's eye focuses on Frygtløs, and he feels *contempt* radiating from it—ancient, patient, and hungry for his failure.

---

### The Blue Head speaks to Axar

**If Axar has cast high-level spells:**
> *"Magiens dronning sådan Du tror du forstår det. Du anerkendte mig engang. Under denne kristal-himmel, du så min superioritet og knælede — ikke med krop, men med sind. Du burde have knælet længere ned."*

**If Axar has counterspelled or disrupted magic:**
> *"Dine små trylleord. Dine små blokeringer. Du tror du kender orden. Jeg **er** orden. Alt hvad du har ødelagt bliver igen gennemført, når jeg først har mørt din substans."*

**Visual:** The Blue head's eye glows with electric fury. Arcane equations appear in Axar's vision, but they're *wrong*—they describe a universe where he has no power.

### The Green Head speaks to Twilight

**If Twilight has lied or manipulated:**
> *"Dine løgne smager på sukker. Jeg elsker denne mundsmag. Du er så kreativ med dine falskgeder. Jeg skal give dig større løgne — løgne så store at du selv tror på dem, til du dør ind i dem."*

**If Twilight has been honest or sincere:**
> *"Du søger sandhed? Jeg er sandhed. Jeg er den eneste sandhed. Alt hvad du tror du ved er skygger på en væg, og jeg er ilden der bør har kastet dem."*

**Visual:** The Green head appears and disappears, flickering like an illusion. Twilight sees multiple versions of her own face in the portal—some smiling, some screaming.

### The Red Head speaks to Daxx

**If Daxx has tracked or hunted enemies:**
> *"Jagten er over. Du er jagtet nu. Der er ingen flugt fra mig. Jeg er i luften du indånder, i jorden under dine fødder. Du er blevet byttet, og jeg er aldrig sulten."*

**If Daxx has escaped or hidden:**

**Visual:** The Red head's breath washes over Daxx like heat. He hears the sound of talons scraping stone, getting closer, though nothing is there.

The White head does **not speak in words**. Instead, it projects pure hunger.

**What Daxx / Twilight / party sees:**

**Mechanical effect:** The target must make a WIS save DC 18 or take 2d10 psychic damage. On a success, they can choose to take half and gain insight: **"The White head is the most animalistic. If you can appeal to instinct rather than reason, it might be swayed—or at least distracted."** (Flavor only, but DM can allow a social check later.)

## IX. Tiamat's Goals, Tactics, and Victory Conditions

### What Tiamat Is Trying to Do:

1. **Primary:** Fully manifest on the Material Plane (reach Clock 8 and hold it)
2. **Secondary:** Consume Severin's life force to establish a *permanent* anchor (killing Severin does NOT stop the ritual if the clock reaches 8)
3. **Tertiary:** Devour at least one PC to establish a "gateway"—a bloodline tie to the Material Plane
4. **Fallback:** If forced to retreat, do maximum damage before returning to Avernus

### Tiamat's Tactics (By Clock):

**Clock 0–4:** Focus on defending the ritual. Use Severin and Red Wizards as meat shields. Do NOT attack the party directly until Clock 6. Use lair actions to deny party movement, not deal damage.

**Clock 5–6:** Transition. The first head can now attack. Use it to target the most dangerous PC (usually Frygtløs or the healer). Each successful hit should feel *devastating*.

**Clock 7–8:** Go on offense. All heads attack. Use lair actions to isolate and eliminate threats. If a PC drops to 0 HP, use an lair action to finish them (they cannot be revived once consumed by Tiamat's mouth).

### What Stops Tiamat:

**Hard stop:**
- All five Dragon Masks destroyed *before* Clock 8 (the ritual cannot complete without them)
- The Mask of the Dragon Queen is removed from the temple (Severin's corpse must be left behind, or she is carried out; requires a PC to grab Severin and drag him 120+ feet while running from combat)

**Soft stop (weakening):**
- Foci sabotaged on Level 2 (reduces Tiamat's resistances and HP max)
- Severin killed before Clock 6 (no longer gains benefits from Tiamat; ritual stalls at current clock value)
- All Red Wizards killed in one round (ritual resets to 0; Tiamat must begin again, but this costs time)

**Failure forward (party doesn't "win," but can still mitigate):**
- Clock 8 is reached. Tiamat fully manifests. Party must now:
  - Banish her back to Avernus (requires rare magic or Axar's Arcana knowledge)
  - Collapse the Well of Dragons (requires dwarven demolition, 10 rounds to set up)
  - Seal the portal by destroying all five masks simultaneously (chaotic, but possible)
  - Accept the catastrophe and flee (world changes, campaign shifts tone)

### Victory Conditions:

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| **Ritual clock stopped at 0–2** | Tiamat does not manifest. Severin dies. Cult is broken. Full victory. |
| **Ritual clock stopped at 3–5** | Tiamat partially manifests (1–2 heads). She can be defeated or banished. Pyrrhic victory. |
| **Ritual clock reached 6–7** | Tiamat manifests strongly. Three+ heads active. Party in real danger. Success requires all foci destroyed + one mask destroyed + perfect tactics. |
| **Ritual clock reached 8** | Tiamat fully manifested. Party cannot win via traditional combat. Must redirect (collapse portal, banish, or flee). Heroic but costly victory. |

## X. Summary: Running Tiamat at the Table

### Pre-battle Checklist:

- [ ] Decide which head manifests first (typically Red or Black for drama)
- [ ] Set initial ritual clock based on campaign state (typical: 1–3)
- [ ] Mark Severin's HP progression (230 → 300 at Clock 5)
- [ ] Prepare head personalities and "speak" moments
- [ ] Print a visible clock tracker (d10, tokens, or index card)
- [ ] Have lair action list ready to use on initiative 20
- [ ] Know which foci have been destroyed (affects resistances)

### Running the Battle (Simple Flow):

1. **Round 1–3:** Party enters. Ritual ticks. Heads speak. No direct Tiamat attacks.
2. **Round 4–6:** First head manifests and attacks. Lair actions start. Severin becomes more powerful.
3. **Round 7–10:** Multiple heads active. Party targets foci or Severin. Lair actions intensify.
4. **Round 11+:** Final push. Either ritual is stopped or Clock 8 is reached. Campaign either concludes or pivots.

### Key Moments:

- When first head manifests → party realizes this is *not* a dragon, but something worse
- When Severin becomes immortal → party shifts focus from killing Severin to stopping the ritual
- When Clock 8 is reached → world is ending; everything changes
- When a head is destroyed → morale boost; they *can* win
