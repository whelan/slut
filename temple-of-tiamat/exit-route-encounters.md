# Exit Route Encounters — Plaza 23 & Blue Chapel

> Two linked ambush points on the party's locked escape route from the Well of Dragons to the Temple of Tiamat.
>
> **Party state:** ~50–70% HP (no Long Rest available), Ritual NOT started, party exiting via single tunnel only (no multiple-route branching).

---

## ENCOUNTER 1: PLAZA 23 TUNNEL GUARDS (Party's Exit Tunnel)

### Setup

**Location:** Area 23, Northern Exits — bleak plaza flanked by bone heaps, clear line of sight up each tunnel to the party's entry point (250 ft east–west, 100 ft north from temple gate).

**Enemies present:** 2 Barbed Devils + 4 Guard Drakes (one tunnel only — focus on the party's exit route).

**Objective for cult:** Block the tunnel, delay infiltration, alert the temple.

---

### Four Branches: Decision Points

#### BRANCH A: Combat from Behind (Stealth DC 14)

**Trigger:** Party rolls Stealth (group check), beats DC 14.

**Surprise round (1 round):** Party acts first. Devils/drakes unaware. No sound yet. Then initiative.

**Sound consequence:** Any spell 3rd level or higher, or any large AOE (Fireball, Shatter, Thunderwave) = automatic alert. Chromatic dragons in the caldera (flying overhead) make Perception checks. Dragons only move if ritual is actively running (see Encounter 2 branch).

**Combat duration expectation:** 3–5 rounds if party focuses fire on devils first. Barbed Devils are deadly (3 attacks, poison multiattack, reactions).

**Resources cost:** 1–2 spell slots, likely healing (Twilight Bardic Inspiration, potions).

---

#### BRANCH B: Sneak Past (Group Stealth DC 16)

**Trigger:** Party rolls Stealth (group check), beats DC 16.

**Result:** Guards do not detect the party. No combat. Party may slip past to the Blue Chapel tunnel (Area 24). The moment they reach the temple interior (Area 1), see Encounter 2.

---

#### BRANCH C: Twilight Suggestion on Drake (DC 18 Charisma Save)

**NPC name:** One drake is slightly isolated (the pair on the southeast edge of the plaza).

**Twilight's Vicious Mockery / Suggestion spell:** Target one drake with a spell requiring a Charisma save.

**Suggestion DC 18:** *"Walk away from the tunnel entrance and sleep in the bone pile for an hour."*

**Result if successful:** Drake is out of combat for 1 hour (or until combat starts; if combat erupts, it wakes). Now 1 Devil + 3 Drakes remaining. This slightly favors the party and costs Twilight 1 spell slot (3rd level).

---

#### BRANCH D: Axar Fireball (Wizard Alpha Strike)

**Trigger:** Party decides the plaza is clear enough for a mass-damage spell.

**Axar casts Fireball (20d6, 8d6 half):** 2 Barbed Devils (poor Dex) take half; 4 Guard Drakes take half or none.

**Result:** Devils/drakes likely to die or be severely wounded. **BUT:** Fireball is unmistakably loud. Chromatic dragons in the air (Perception DC 8) all hear it. If the ritual has NOT started, dragons do not immediately attack — cultists are still deploying. If ritual HAS started, see Encounter 2 branching consequences.

**Cost:** 1 × 3rd-level spell slot, initiative order, risk of dragon involvement.

---

### Initiative & Round-by-Round Tactics

#### Barbed Devil (AC 15, HP 110 each)
- **Turn 1 (Round 1):** Move into melee range of nearest PC. Multiattack: 2× Glaive + 1× Stinger (poison, CON DC 15).
- **Turns 2–3:** Stay in melee, use stinger on anyone trying to move past. Use reactions to counter-attack anyone who breaks engagement.
- **Goal:** Hold the tunnel entrance and prevent the party from advancing.

#### Guard Drake (AC 12, HP 22 each)
- **Turn 1:** Move to flank with devil. Bite (only one attack, lower damage).
- **Turns 2+:** Attack nearest PC, especially casters. Use prone bite if enemy is in melee.
- **Goal:** Hold terrain and support devils.

#### Party Tactics (if combat occurs)
- **Frygtløs:** Frontline. Uses Greataxe + Barbarian Rage. Attacks devils first (higher threat).
- **Daxx:** Dual Shortswords or Longbow. Targets drakes (lower AC, faster kills). Bonus action Hide / Sneak Attack if possible.
- **Twilight:** Stay back. Vicious Mockery (avoid concentration conflict) or cast a damaging cantrip. Save spell slots for post-combat healing or Suggestion.
- **Axar:** Tactical positioning. If the plaza is clear, Fireball the devils + drakes for sure kills. If uncertain, use Scorching Ray or hold action.

### Setup

**Location:** Area 1 — Entrance/Blue Chapel, inside the Temple of Tiamat. Cold stone, draconic light, 5 Red Wizards chanting in the five chapels (Areas 1, 3–6, plus 5 more in the spires above). Ritual is at X rounds complete (you define based on pacing).

**Primary enemy:** 1 Red Wizard (standing in Area 1 — the Blue Chapel), AC 15 (Mage Armor), HP 40, INT 17. This is **Veksin Thorm**, the young apprentice.

**Secondary enemies nearby:** 4 other Red Wizards (in Areas 3–6 and spires 8–12), unaware of the party's approach **unless** plaza combat was loud.

**Objective for cult:** Maintain the ritual. Alert Rath Modar if infiltrators are detected.

---

### NPC: Veksin, Red Wizard of the Blue Chapel

**Name & Detail:** Veksin Thorm, youngest of Rath Modar's apprentices (age 26). Quiet, methodical. During the ritual, he hums a soft lullaby — a childhood song his mother sang before the Dragon Cult took him.

**Mechanical trait:** Veksin has been assigned to Area 1 (the least-trafficked chapel) precisely because Rath Modar trusts him least. He is competent but nervous. If combat erupts, he will try to flee (use action to Disengage and move toward the sanctuary) rather than stand and fight.

**Stat block:** Red Wizard (Mage), AC 15, HP 40. Spells: Mage Armor (active), Fireball (2× 3rd-level slots), Counterspell, Shield, Magic Missile, Scorching Ray. INT 17 (+3 spell save, 15 to hit spell attacks).

---

### Three Branches: Decision Points

#### BRANCH A: Silent Kill (Stealth DC 14, Sneak Attack)

**Trigger:** Party rolls group Stealth check, beats DC 14 (or DC 16 if they failed to sneak past plaza guards).

**Surprise round (1 round):** Veksin is not expecting combat. Party may use surprise to **eliminate him before he can alert others**.

**Key mechanic — Ritual Disruption Window:**
- If Veksin (1 of 5 Red Wizards performing) dies **silently** (i.e., before he can use an action to cast *Sending* or shout to Rath Modar), the ritual loses 1 channel-er for 1 round.
- At the start of the next round (round 1 of initiative), Rath Modar checks: "Are all five performing?" He is in the Red Chapel (Area 6), does not see Area 1. Possible actions:
  - **If 1 round has passed:** Rath Modar suspects trouble. He calls a Red Wizard from spire 8 or 9 to replace Veksin. Takes 1 round to move and resume ritual.
  - **If Veksin dies in surprise, combat is over in 1 round:** Portal wavers but ritual does not reset (need 2 consecutive rounds of <5 wizards).
- **Opportunity window:** Party has **2 rounds** of ritual disruption to sabotage, move, or commit a secondary action before a replacement arrives.

**Optimal kill sequence (silent):**
1. Daxx: Bonus action Hide, Action Sneak Attack Shortsword (2d6 + DEX + sneak attack damage, ~18–25 damage). Veksin takes 1/3 to 1/2 HP in one hit.
2. Axar or Frygtløs: Action to finish. Veksin is dead before his turn in initiative.
3. No alert. Ritual opportunity window opens.

**Cost:** 1 round of setup (Stealth), risk if any PC fails the check (triggers combat with Veksin alerting nearby wizards).

---

#### BRANCH B: Loud Kill (Combat Erupts, Initiative)

**Trigger:** Party fails Stealth check (or chooses to attack openly). Combat starts. Veksin reacts on his turn.

**Veksin's turn (if initiative roll is high):**
- **First action:** Cast *Sending* (1-round casting ritual, not an action — so this is bonus action OR he just shouts across the temple).
- **Shout:** *"Infiltrators in Area One! Alert Rath Modar!"* (automatically heard by all wizards within the temple; Perception DC 8 to understand the words).

**Cascade effect:**
- **Round 1:** Veksin alerts others. Four remaining Red Wizards (Areas 3–6 and spires) become aware.
- **Round 2:** Rath Modar (AC 15, HP 77, see appendix D) either:
  - **Abandons ritual** and uses action Dimension Door or Greater Invisibility to position near the party.
  - **Orders the Abishai** (resurrected wyrmspeakers, if present in the temple) to engage.
- **Round 3+:** Veksin is likely dead by now. But now you have 5 remaining Red Wizards attacking and potentially Rath Modar engaging.

**Combat duration:** 5+ rounds. Veksin dies fast (low HP). The real threat is reinforcement.

**Cost:** Major escalation. Ritual continues (Rath Modar can maintain it alone, though weakly). Party must commit significant resources.

---

#### BRANCH C: Suggestion ("Step Outside")

**Trigger:** Twilight uses *Suggestion* (DC 18 Charisma save). Veksin makes the check: INT 17, but CHA is likely ~12 (–1 modifier). He can resist if he rolls well.

**Suggestion DC 18:** *"Step outside the Blue Chapel and stand in the plaza for 10 minutes. Meditate on the Drakonei." (Or similar.)*

**Result if successful:** Veksin abandons his ritual station for 10 minutes. Party now has a **ritual opportunity window:** Veksin's absence = 4 of 5 wizards. Ritual wavers, no progress made. However, Veksin is still alive, still ritually bound, and **still alert if party does anything loud**.

**If failed:** Veksin realizes he was targeted. Immediate alert (as in Branch B).

**Cost:** 1 × 3rd or 4th-level spell slot. If successful, party has breathing room but Veksin is a loose cannon outside the chapel.

## DECISION MATRIX: Consequences Across Both Encounters

### Scenario 1: Quiet Through Both (Best Outcome)

**Encounter 1:** Stealth DC 16 success. No combat. No sound.

**Encounter 2:** Silent kill (Stealth DC 14) on Veksin.

**Result:**
- Ritual continues but with 4 of 5 wizards (temporary disruption).
- Rath Modar is **unaware** of party's presence.
- No dragon alarm.
- **Ritual clock:** No penalty. Party has full surprise for temple tactics.
- **Remaining challenges:** Rath Modar + 8 Red Wizards in spires, Severin in sanctuary, Abishai (if present).
- **Party advantage:** Opportunity for sabotage, positioning, or additional prep before Rath Modar notices.

---

### Scenario 2: Loud at Plaza Only

**Encounter 1:** Combat erupts. Loud spell (Fireball) or extended fight (3+ rounds).

**Encounter 2:** Stealth DC 16 success. Party slips past.

**Result:**
- Chromatic dragons hear the sound in the plaza. They do not immediately attack (ritual still running, not yet threatened).
- **Ritual clock:** If ritual is running, 1 dragon circles the caldera (not yet diving). If ritual hasn't started, dragons remain idle.
- **Rath Modar:** Unaware (no active alert from Area 23).
- **Remaining challenges:** Same as Scenario 1, but dragon threat is elevated. Any additional loud action triggers dragon strafing.
- **Party penalty:** Dragon patrol is now "aware" of noise. Any further loud spells = dragons move to investigate (add 1 round per loud spell before they arrive).

---

### Scenario 3: Loud at Both (Worst Outcome)

**Encounter 1:** Combat. Loud spell or extended fight.

**Encounter 2:** Combat with Veksin. He alerts Rath Modar and other wizards.

**Result:**
- **Ritual clock:** All Red Wizards are now aware of infiltrators. Rath Modar abandons (or weakly maintains) the ritual and engages.
- **Dragons:** Chromatic dragons circle the temple. If combat is still ongoing at the end of Round 3, roll for dragon arrival (1d4 dragons descend to engage party, attempting to protect the ritual).
- **Abishai:** Resurrected wyrmspeakers (Rezmir as Black Abishai, Neronvain as Green Abishai, etc.) are alerted. They move to intercept the party.
- **Ritual penalty:** If Rath Modar is engaged in combat, ritual weakens. Needs 5 of 5 wizards each round. With Rath Modar fighting, ritual stalls (no progress). If party kills 2+ Red Wizards, ritual resets.
- **Party penalty:** Massive escalation. 5 Red Wizards + Rath Modar + up to 4 dragons + Abishai. Party is outnumbered and outmatched.

---

### Scenario 4: Loud at Plaza, Silent Kill at Chapel

**Encounter 1:** Combat. Dragon alert triggered.

**Encounter 2:** Silent kill on Veksin. No Rath Modar alert.

**Result:**
- **Ritual clock:** Ritual stumbles for 1 round (Veksin absent). Replacement arrives next round. No reset.
- **Rath Modar:** Still unaware of the party. Still performing the ritual in the Red Chapel.
- **Dragons:** Circling. Will investigate if combat continues in the temple. If party moves quickly (no additional loud spells in Encounter 2), dragons do not immediately attack (they wait for ritual to complete or for another loud signal).
- **Party advantage:** Moderate. Rath Modar is still committed to the ritual. Party can sabotage, move toward foci, or position for a surprise attack on Severin.
- **Danger:** Stealth becomes critical. Any additional loud spells trigger dragon strafing.

## Appendix: Monster Stat References

### Barbed Devil (AC 15, HP 110)
- Multiattack: 2× Glaive (+7, 2d8+4 each) + 1× Stinger (+7, 1d6+4 poison, CON DC 15).
- Reaction Goad: If enemy moves away, Barbed Devil makes a melee attack.
- Resistances: Cold, Fire, Lightning; nonmagical B/P/S.

### Guard Drake (AC 12, HP 22)
- Bite: +4, 1d6+2 damage.
- Simple, fast. Likely KO'd in 1–2 hits.

### Red Wizard / Mage (AC 15, HP 40)
- Spellcasting: INT 17 (+3 spell save, +5 spell attack).
- Cantrips: Fire Bolt, Light, Mage Hand.
- 1st level: Mage Armor (active, +3 AC), Magic Missile, Burning Hands.
- 2nd level: Scorching Ray, Misty Step.
- 3rd level: Fireball (2 slots), Counterspell, Lightning Bolt.
- Reaction: Shield (+5 AC vs one attack/round), Counterspell.

### Veksin (Modified Mage for narrative)
- Same stat block, but INT 17 (competent but inexperienced).
- Personality: Nervous, obedient, not a fighter. Will attempt to flee if cornered (uses Disengage action).
- 1-round to alert Rath Modar (either via *Sending* or shouting across the temple).
