# Aftermath Sequence – Tyranny of Dragons Finale

The battle is over. Severin has fallen or ascended. Tiamat has been sealed, partially manifested, or emerged in full. This document guides 30–90 minutes of epilogue gameplay that transforms victory into legacy.

---

# Part 1: Temple Escape (All Victory Tiers)

## Overview

**Duration:** 10 rounds (in-fiction: ~1 minute; table time: 15–20 min)  
**Objective:** Leave the Temple of Tiamat before total collapse (clockwise crumbling from Level 3 downward)  
**Escape route:** Level 3 → stairwell (crumbling) → Level 2 hubs (platforms falling) → Level 1 floor (splitting) → outside (archway collapse)

The temple does not disappear. It *breaks*. Each round, one structural failure occurs. A platform drops. A pillar falls. Lava vents erupt. The PCs and any NPCs with them must navigate this controlled chaos.

### DM Mechanics

**Round-by-round collapse events:**

| Round | Event | DC / Type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Level 3 stairwell cracks; 20 ft section collapses 30 ft down | DEX save DC 15 (Acrobatics +5 for half) or fall 30 ft |
| 2 | Level 2 eastern bridge sways and snaps (if party used it) | DEX save DC 16 or fall into the chamber below (20 ft, 7d6) |
| 3 | A pillar supporting Level 2 crashes down, blocking one route | Trigger: can navigate around (detour adds 1 round) or go through (strength DC 17) |
| 4 | Lava vent on Level 1 erupts (30 ft cone): 6d6 fire damage | DEX save DC 15 half |
| 5 | The ground beneath Level 1 splits into a 40 ft chasm | Athletics/Acrobatics DC 14 to cross, or 20 ft drop into molten ground (8d10 fire) |
| 6 | The temple's outer wall (Level 1, northern side) fractures and separates from the interior | A 10 ft gap opens; creatures on the wall fall outward 40 ft into rubble (5d6) |
| 7 | The main archway at the exit begins to collapse inward | Dexterity save DC 17 or take 11d6 bludgeoning damage (half on success); exit blocked for 1 more round if failed |
| 8 | Level 2 platform containing any remaining foci collapses entirely | 15d6 bludgeoning; no save (only creatures still on it take damage) |
| 9 | The ritual dais on Level 3 drops 60 ft, crushing the ritual chamber below | Massive tremor; anyone still on Level 3 or 2 makes DC 18 DEX save or falls 60 ft |
| 10 | Final archway collapses; any creature still inside is buried (15d10 bludgeoning, no save) | This is the hard deadline |

**Adjustments by victory tier:**

- **Full Victory:** Clock 0–3. Collapse triggers every 2 rounds instead of every round; PCs have extra time.
- **Pyrrhic:** Clock 4–7. Collapse as listed. Standard timing.
- **Failure-Forward:** Clock 8. Collapse triggers every round, staggered; add one extra event (event 11 = portal rim collapse).

### NPC Assistance (Skill Challenges)

Any allied NPC remaining in the temple can provide one of these aids (call once per escape sequence):

**Frygtløs (Barbarian):**
- Hold a collapsing section open for 1 round; all allies in the zone may move without triggering an escape save
- "I'll hold it. *Go.*"

**Daxx (Ranger):**
- Find a faster route (Survival DC 13); reduces escape time by 1 round for the whole group
- Alternatively, scout ahead; +2 to next escape save for all who follow his path

**Axar (Wizard):**
- Cast *Dimension Door* or *Telekinesis* to assist one ally over a chasm/gap (saves them the acrobatics check)
- Alternatively, *Counterspell* an environmental hazard (vent, pillar) once; negates one collapse event

**Twilight (Bard):**
- Inspire allies (Bardic Inspiration) to grant each party member +1d8 to an escape save
- Alternatively, *Phantom Steed* to carry up to 2 allies at speed, bypassing 1 terrain challenge

### NPC Status at Exit

**Ulder Ravengard** (if present in temple): Emerges bloodied but alive. Takes a blade through the shoulder as the archway collapses. "Worth it."

**Elia (bronze dragon, if alive):** Flaps upward as the temple falls, wings spread against the ash. She lands outside. She is *breathing heavily*.

**Surviving Red Wizards** (if any escaped the ritual battle): Flee ahead of the party; arrive outside first. Attempt to scatter into the army chaos.

**Surviving cultists** (minor): Most are crushed or flee. Use this as brutal narrative punctuation — the cult's final screams.

**Thayan allies** (if Nyh Ilmichh's delegation remains): They use *Greater Invisibility* and *Teleportation Circle* to escape independently. They meet the party outside, standing apart, acknowledging with a nod.

## Boxed Text – The Escape

> Trappen knager. Så giver den op.
>
> Du mærker ikke dit eget fald. Du ser bare stenen suse forbi, og så er du på niveau 2 igen, på dine fødder, uden at vide, hvordan du kom dertil. Adrenalin.
>
> Bag jer styrter søjlen.
>
> Ikke over jer. Bare nær. Nær nok til at I mærker varmen fra massiviteten – det uselviske tryk af giga-tons jord, som hvis den skulle se jer, ville forglemme jer på øjeblikkene.
>
> Før jer åbner gulvet sig.
>
> Ikke helt. Bare noksat til at I ser rødt lys nedefra. Magma? Eller hendes øjne, stadig åbne nedefra.
>
> I løber.
>
> I ved ikke, hvor I løber hen, men I løber som om det vigtigste i verden var at være *væk* fra det. Og det er det.

# Part 2: Victory Conditions (Three Tiers)

## Tier 1: Full Victory

**Trigger:** Severin falls at ritual clock 0–3. All five foci destroyed OR at least three foci destroyed by transition to Level 3.

### Scene 1 – The Moment of Sealing

As the party exits the temple and the final archway collapses, read:

> Portalen bag jer lukker ikke gradvist. Den *vrider* sig.
>
> Tiamat's stemme – alle fem hoveder på én gang – bryder ud i et brøl, så højt at verden selv knælter.
>
> Og så bliver det stille.
>
> Ikke roligt stille. *Absencens* stille. Som hvis hele verden holdt vejret.
>
> Over jer drejer skyerne stille og stille, fra deres rotation, til deres normale kreds. Asken sætter sig. Solen (er der sol? ja, der er sol) kommer gennem skyerne.
>
> Og der er fred. Rigtig fred. Det første ord I tænker på er "paz," og det føler helt urigtigt i en verden som denne.

**Mechanical note:** This is not a spell or illusion. The ritual is genuinely disrupted. The portal collapses. Tiamat is sealed back into Avernus, *wounded but conscious*.

### Scene 2 – What the Party Sees

> Calderaen under jer er stille som en grav. Kultisten dreger er væk – langt væk, eller døde. Ingen brøler. Ingen kampe. Bare det langsomt stillehastende støv, der falder fra rødt lys der ikke er der længere.
>
> Over jer kredser metallic drager – fem, måske seks – og hver gang de kredser, synger de. Det er ikke ord. Det er bare lyde af relief, der bliver til melodi.
>
> I er trætte. I blæder fra steder I ikke vidste, I kunne blø. Jeres tøj er ødelagt, jeres magi er opbrugt, og halvdelen af jer er et eller andet sted mellem bevidsthed og søvn.
>
> Men der er ingen ved jer i templet. Og Tiamat er ikke her.

### Scene 3 – The First NPC Arrival (30 seconds later)

**Ulder Ravengard** appears first, on foot, armor cracked. He has perhaps 40 soldiers behind him — the remnant of the Flaming Fist's frontline.

> Han ser jer, og han stopper simpelthen. Hans mand stopper også.
>
> Han knæler ikke. Men hans øjne bliver fugtige.
>
> *"Gud give mig kraft,"* siger han.
>
> *"I er derhenne. I er *her*."*

Allow the party a moment to respond. He offers water, healers, a safe perimeter. He does not try to debrief them immediately.

### Scene 4 – Remallia Haventree (5 minutes later)

She arrives with a dozen Harpers and the Emerald Enclave scouts. She is composed, but there is something different in her posture — she is *relieved*.

> *"Leosin skulle have været her,"* siger hun.
>
> Hun folder dette noget væk — hun gør det godt, at glemme hvad hun just sagde.
>
> *"Men han ville have været stolt. Alle ville have været det."*
>
> Hun ser på jer.
>
> *"Hvad gjorde I? Vi hørte templet styrte. Vi så lyset fra portalen slukne. Og så — stilhed. Hvad gjorde I herovre?"*

The party can recount. Remallia listens. At the end she says:

> *"Det er det jeg troede. Den ritualist var ikke klar til modsæt. Severin var ikke klar til jer."*
>
> *"Vær taknemlig for at I havde noget, der ville overraske ham."*

### Scene 5 – Ontharr Frume (15 minutes later)

The Order of the Gauntlet leader arrives with clerics and knights. He is visibly moved.

> *"Det er gjort,"* siger han.
>
> Han er ikke sikker på, hvem han taler til.
>
> *"En gud ville have erobret verden i dag. Og i stedet — en dør blev lukket."*
>
> Han beder — ikke til Helm, men til tysthed, til at verden kunne få åndehold.

He offers his healers without question.

### Scene 6 – The Metallic Dragons

**Elia** (bronze dragon, if alive) lands on the caldera rim. She is smoking slightly. Her left wing has scorch marks.

> *"Du er for lille til at gøre, hvad du gjorde,"* siger hun til Frygtløs.
>
> Det er ikke en kompliment, præcis. Det er bare sandhed.
>
> *"Men du gjorde det alligevel. Jeg troede ikke, jeg ville se det igen: en dør til Avernus, der blev lukket, i stedet for åbnet."*
>
> Hun bøjer hovedet – et stort hoved, så stort som et små hus.
>
> *"Tak. I navn for hele min art. Vi skylder jer alt."*

If Elia died in a Pyrrhic scenario (see Tier 2), a surviving metallic dragon (perhaps the copper Tazmikella or the silver Otaaryliakkarnos) arrives in her stead, bearing the same message but with grief beneath it.

### Scene 7 – The NPC One-Liners (Read Slowly)

Each NPC who survives approaches one PC and speaks one line. This is not dialogue. This is *memorial*. Read them as written, then let the table sit in the words.

**To Frygtløs (Ulder Ravengard speaks this):**

> *"Du stillede dig foran verden. Det vil ikke blive glemt."*

**To Axar (Remallia Haventree speaks this):**

> *"Dit sind kastede Tiamat tilbage. Vi er taknemlige for at det ikke blev os selv."*

**To Daxx (A Harper scout speaks this):**

> *"Du vendte dig ikke væk, selvom verden bad dig do det. Det betyder noget."*

**To Twilight (Ontharr Frume speaks this):**

> *"Verden behøvede en stemme, der vågnede det fra drømmen. Du gjorde det. Fortsæt med at synge."*

### Scene 8 – First Light (Next Morning)

The party has rested (short or long rest, DM's choice). The sun rises on the caldera.

> Solen kommer op over Tiamat's gravsted.
>
> Eller det er ikke hendes gravsted, fordi hun ikke er død. Bare lukket inde igen. Sult efter luft i mørke.
>
> Kuldestadet glider dårligt – det ser ud som et sår i jorden, der havde været åbent i tre timer og så blev lukket igen, mens det stadig blødede.
>
> Over jer kredser de metallic drager stadig, mindre nervøst nu.
>
> Og for første gang siden I kom til denne fjeldkrans, blæser vinden fra nord. Naturlig vind. Bare vind.

**Mechanical note:** PCs gain the following benefits for 24 hours after exiting the temple:
- Advantage on death saves (the world is helping)
- +2 to recovery from exhaustion
- Any ally who died during the finale can be resurrected without penalty if a *Resurrection* spell is available (the sealing grants divine leverage)

## Tier 2: Pyrrhic Victory

**Trigger:** Severin falls at ritual clock 4–7. Tiamat breaks through partially (1–3 heads manifest visibly before being forced back).

### Scene 1 – The Cost

The temple collapses as written. But as the party exits, the world is *different*.

> Portalen slider ikke lukkes. Det *stivner*.
>
> Tiamat's fem hoveder – de er der, alle fem, i det samme moment. Deres øjne åbner sig på én gang.
>
> Og så stopper hun. Midt i skridtet.
>
> Hendes sorte hoved kan nå til halvfjerdstykkerne omkring templet. Hendes røde hoved knytter sig uden at kunne slippe. Der er en *grænse*, en usynlig væg mellem verdener, og hendes kroppe slår mod den igen og igen.
>
> Så glider hun tilbage, udelukkende.
>
> Ikke fordi hun tabte. Fordi hun *måtte*.
>
> Og portalen – den lukkes ikke. Den *forbliver*. En stabil åbning til intet andet. Et ar i verden.

The Well of Dragons is now a scar on the world. The boundary between the Material Plane and Avernus is *thin here*, permanently.

### Scene 2 – The Allied Losses

Ulder Ravengard arrives with fewer soldiers. He is hurt — not just physically, but in how he moves.

> *"Vi mistede hele østflanken,"* siger han helt stille.
>
> *"Dværg-saperne holdt broen i tyve minutter efter at han så hendes. Tyve minutter. Og så gik broen."*

**Elia is dead.** A bronze dragon does not go quietly. A surviving metallic arrives instead: the copper **Tazmikella**, grieving.

> *"Elia fløj ind foran hendes kløer. For at købe jer tid."*
>
> Hun lukker øjnene.
>
> *"Jeg skal møde hendes drager. Jeg skal sige til dem, at deres mor døde mod en gud, ikke mod en slag. Det vil ikke være nok. Men det er hvad jeg har."*

**The dwarven sapper captain is dead.** His name was **Durok Steelhelm**. He died setting shaped charges against Severin's last magical barrier. A young dwarf, perhaps his son, hands the party his hammer.

> *"Han sagde, hvis han ikke kom tilbage, skulle jeg give jer denne. Han sagde, I ville forstå."*

**A Harper is dead.** Not named, but Remallia speaks about them.

> *"Tolv døde i dag. Tolv af mine folk. Og alle af dem vidste, de kunne dø. Og alle af dem kom alligevel."*

### Scene 3 – The Quiet Vigil (Night of Aftermath)

The party is invited to stand at the caldera rim. Ulder, Remallia, Ontharr, and any surviving faction leader are there.

They stand in silence for a while.

Then, one by one, they speak the names of the dead. Not in a formal way. Just names. *"Leosin. Elia. Durok. Tharsus. Melen. Kessa ..."*

The PCs can add names if they wish. They do not have to.

At the end, no one says anything. They just turn and walk back to camp.

### Scene 4 – What Each PC Carries

**Axar** can still see the ritual structure when he closes his eyes. For months afterward, he cannot sleep in full darkness without ritual lines crawling at the edges of his vision.

**Daxx** has forgotten his original face. He looks in a mirror and cannot remember what he was before the Changeling thing became *home*. He will eventually remember. Not today.

**Frygtløs** (if dominated by Severin during combat) remembers every moment of it. Every muscle he moved without choosing. It will take him years to stop flinching when he hears Severin's voice in his dreams.

**Twilight** tries to sing that night. One note is gone from her register. She does not know which one. She will be looking for it for the rest of her life.

### Scene 5 – The Burden of Vigilance (One Week Later)

Remallia approaches the party with a proposal.

> *"Det ar derhenne – i kalderaen. Det kommer til at blø. Ikke fysisk. Men magisk. Hver dag vil det tiltrække hvad der ønsker at come gennem."*
>
> *"Jeg kan ikke værne det alene. Harpers er for få, og verden har andre krige at kæmpe."*
>
> *"Men i — hvis I var villig — kunne I være vakterne. Ikke for at lukke porten. Det kan ingen gøre. Men for at se til, at der ikke kommer noget *farligt* gennem."*
>
> *"Jeg ved, at I er trætte. Jeg ved, at I har gjort nok. Men jeg må spørge alligevel."*

The party can accept, decline, or defer. All are valid.

### Scene 6 – The Epilogue (Six Months Later)

The party gathers at a place of their choice — a tavern, a hall, a hillside. They have not been together since the battle.

> I sidder rundt om et bord som før.
>
> Twilight begynder at synge. Det er ikke en kamp-sang. Det er ikke en helte-sang. Det er bare en sang — en tune hun husker fra barndommen, før alt dette blev reelt.
>
> Frygtløs synger med. Han kan ikke rigtig synge. Det er det, der gør det smukt.
>
> Axar gemmer sine bøger væk for første gang den dag.
>
> Daxx forbliver i sin egen form i fire timer i træk. Det er længste tid siden templet.
>
> I taler ikke om hende. I taler ikke om templet. I taler om Leosin. Om hærene. Om Severin — ikke som fjende, men som en mand, der valgte forkert. I taler om jer selv — ikke som helte, men som mennesker, der overlevede hinanden.
>
> Det er ikke et happy ending. Det er et *ægte* ending. Det er nok.

## Tier 3: Failure-Forward (Tiamat Manifests)

**Trigger:** Ritual clock reaches 8. Tiamat fully manifests on the Material Plane. Severin is dead or ascended.

### Scene 1 – The First Hour

The party escapes the temple. But there is nowhere to run.

> Hun er på den anden side af verden, før timen er omme.
>
> I er stadig i ruinerne. I er i live. I forstår ikke, hvorfor.
>
> Måske var I ikke værd at dræbe. Måske var I ikke værd at se. Måske er det, hvad guder gør med insekter, der har irriteret dem: de glemmer dem og fortsætter.
>
> Lyden af Baldur's Gate, der brænder, lyder ikke som en by, der brænder. Det lyder som en by, der bliver *fjernet*.

### Scene 2 – The First Week

**Baldur's Gate falls.** Ulder Ravengard dies (his last act: buying the party and any civilians time to flee). The Flaming Fist is broken.

**Neverwinter is besieged.** Dagult Neverember refuses to flee. He dies three weeks later.

**Waterdeep holds the line** with the Dragonward, but the wards are weakening. The city is frightened.

**The Lords' Alliance, as an organization, ceases to exist.** Factions save themselves.

**The Cult of the Dragon surges.** Thousands join, watching their god conquer the world. They are winning. People join winners.

### Scene 3 – What Remains

The party still lives. Somewhere. The world is changed, but it is not *ended*.

Ulder Ravengard is dead. But King Melandrach of the Elves lives, though his forests are burning.

Remallia Haventree lives. So does Ontharr Frume, though his Order is scattered.

Metallic dragons are dying, but they are *fighting*. Tazmikella (if alive) gathers survivors. So does the silver Otaaryliakkarnos, the bronze Nymmurh.

The question is not "How do we win?" It is "What do we save?"

### Scene 4 – Four Paths Forward

The campaign has changed. It is no longer about defeating Tiamat. It is about surviving her, containing her, or finding some desperate way to challenge her again.

**Mission 1 – Bring Bahamut to the Material Plane:**
Discover how to summon the Platinum Dragon himself. It will require journeys to Mount Celestia, a price each character must pay personally, and an alliance with metallic dragons who have withdrawn into ancient hideaways. This is not certain to work. It might fail. But it is the only path to a god-level opponent.

**Mission 2 – Find Tiamat's One Weakness:**
She is not invincible on the Material Plane. She is *powerful*, but not boundless. Something exists that she cannot withstand — something personal. An old shame. A loss. A name she cannot speak. The party can spend the campaign hunting for it, traveling to the Elemental Planes, ancient dragon lairs, the Far Realm itself.

**Mission 3 – Contain Her Growth:**
While Tiamat is occupied burning and conquering the Sword Coast, she cannot move globally quickly. The party can rally survivors, establish sanctuaries, and prevent *more* of the world from falling. This is not victory. It is *resistance*.

**Mission 4 – Seek the Underdark:**
The dwarves have not fallen. Mithril Hall, Ironforge, and a dozen smaller kingdoms are holding the Deep. There is a rumor — perhaps only a rumor — that the ancient dwarves once sealed something worse than Tiamat, and the secret lies in the lost library beneath Mount Waterdeep. If the party can reach the Deep and survive its perils, they might learn something that changes the game.

### Scene 5 – Boxed Text for the End of This Tier

> Verden er anderledes nu.
>
> I ved ikke, hvor I skal hen. I ved bare, at I ikke kan være her. Hun er 300 mil væk, men hun er også *overalt*. Hendes øjne ser alle horisonter på én gang.
>
> En drage — du genkender ikke arten — sætter sig ned ved jer bål. Den taler ikke. Den bare sidder.
>
> Efter et stykke tid, siger den:
>
> *"I mistede i dag. Det ved I. Men I overlevede. Det ved hun også. Og det betyder, at morgen dagen er ikke afgjort endnu."*
>
> *"Gå. Find andre, der ikke har givet op. Der er flere, end I tror."*

# Part 3: Epilogue Letters (Four Survivors)

These letters are written by NPCs who witnessed the aftermath and lived to record it. They are formal but warm, acknowledging the party's sacrifice and the world's debt.

**Note:** Leosin is *dead* and cannot write. These letters are from his allies.

---

## Letter 1: From Remallia Haventree (Harper Master)

*Addressed to: The Council of Waterdeep / All Allied Leaders / The Heroes of the Well (open letter)*

> My friends,
>
> I write this by lamplight in what was Leosin's quarters in Waterdeep. His journals are here, his maps, his careful notes from years of work. Most of what you know about the Cult of the Dragon, you know because Leosin paid for that knowledge with his blood and his silence.
>
> He paid one last time, before the Well. Six other Harpers paid with him. He never made it to the caldera. But I know he would have wanted you to know that the map those six lives purchased — it worked. It got you where you needed to be. And you *were* needed.
>
> When Tiamat's roar echoed across the battlefield, every Harper in my company fell to one knee. We thought it was the end of the world. Then the light changed. The ritual broke. And I understood what Leosin had seen in you, even when you did not see it in yourselves.
>
> You are not saviors. You are not prophets. You are just people who, when given the choice between leaving and staying, chose the harder thing.
>
> The Harpers will remember. And so will I, as long as I draw breath.
>
> In memory of Leosin Erlanthar,
> *Remallia Haventree*  
> Master of the Harpers, Sword Coast

---

## Letter 2: From Ulder Ravengard (Grand Duke of Baldur's Gate, Flaming Fist Commander)

*Addressed to: The Party*

> I am not good at words that are not orders. So I will keep this brief.
>
> I came to the Well of Dragons expecting to die. I brought soldiers who expected the same. Some of them were right. But the ones who will go home — they will go home because of what you did in that temple. Because you broke a god's doorway instead of just knocking on it.
>
> The bodies of my people are being prepared for burial. In three weeks' time, I will visit every family and tell them the truth: their daughter, their son, their brother — they died because a ritual was stopped. Because the world was not burned clean. Because you people refused to let it happen.
>
> That is not nothing. In a world that has learned to accept everything, that is everything.
>
> When you are ready, come to Baldur's Gate. The gates will be open. There will be a meal. And there will be silence for those we lost.
>
> *Ulder Ravengard*  
> Grand Duke, Baldur's Gate

---

## Letter 3: From Ontharr Frume (Order of the Gauntlet)

*Addressed to: All Faithful, All Seekers, All Those Who Stood*

> The Order of the Gauntlet has been humbled.
>
> We came to the Well believing that strength of arm and purity of faith would be enough. We were wrong. Or perhaps we were right, but we did not have the *kind* of strength that mattered.
>
> I have spent thirty years of my life seeking those who would stand against tyranny. I have tested warriors, blessed knights, ordained priests. I thought I knew what courage looked like.
>
> I was wrong.
>
> Courage does not look like certainty. It looks like a dwarf who does not know if his magic will work, standing against a possessed man in a temple that is falling apart. It looks like a ranger who has lost his shape, choosing to be present anyway. It looks like a barbarian learning, very slowly, that the answer to violence is sometimes *not* more violence. It looks like a bard whose voice is the only constant in a world of change.
>
> These people taught me something I will spend the rest of my life understanding: that faith is not certainty. It is showing up anyway.
>
> The Order of the Gauntlet will rebuild. But we will rebuild differently now. We will rebuild in their image.
>
> May all of you who read this find the strength to do the same.
>
> *Ontharr Frume, High Marshall*  
> Order of the Gauntlet

---

## Letter 4: From King Melandrach of the Elves (Misty Forest)

*Addressed to: The Heroes of the Well of Dragons / All Peoples of the Sword Coast*

> I write this in a language you may not speak, and ask that it be translated, so that the words lose nothing in the changing.
>
> My people have watched the world for ten thousand years. We have seen empires rise and fall like waves. We have seen dragons hunted to near extinction, only to return. We have seen gods walk the earth.
>
> And we have learned this: the only thing that truly matters is what you choose in the moment you must choose.
>
> When I learned that a cult sought to summon Tiamat to our world, I sent soldiers. Not because I believed they would be enough — I did not. But because you do not refuse to fight a god because you might lose. You fight because fighting is the only thing that matters in that moment.
>
> I did not know you — the heroes of whom I now write. I know you now. You chose the moment. You did not let the moment choose you.
>
> My forests are still here. My people are still singing in the Misty Forest. This is because, at a certain moment, in a falling temple beneath a dying caldera, you chose correctly.
>
> We will remember. The songs we sing in the Misty Forest will remember. And when my grandchildren ask why the world still stands, I will tell them your names.
>
> *Melandrach, Eternal King*  
> The Misty Forest

---

## Letter 5: From Elia (Bronze Dragon, if alive; or from Tazmikella, if Elia died)

*Addressed to: The Guardians of the Well*

> You are smaller than us. Shorter-lived. Fragile in ways that dragons are not.
>
> And yet, you stood at the gateway to infinity and said "no."
>
> I have lived for four hundred years. I have watched the slow dance of continents. I have nested in mountains and hunted in seas that no longer exist. I have felt the turn of ages in my bones.
>
> And I have never seen anything as brave as what you did.
>
> Tiamat will sleep now. Not peacefully. But she will sleep. And that sleep was purchased with your blood, your magic, your choices, and your refusal to accept what seemed inevitable.
>
> When you are old — if you are ever old — remember this: you made a difference that will echo across centuries. Dragons will speak your names in the deep places of the world. The metallic ones will speak them with gratitude. The chromatic ones will speak them with something that might be respect.
>
> I do not know if we will fight together again. I hope we do. But if we do not, know this: you have the thanks of my entire kind, and we are a kind that does not give thanks lightly.
>
> *Elia, Dragon of Bronze*  
> [Or, if Elia is dead: *Tazmikella, Dragon of Copper, in memory of Elia her sister*]

# Part 4: Character Epilogue Moments (Per-PC Scenes)

These are individual scenes between the DM and each player, exploring what the finale means for that character specifically. Run these one at a time, separately from the rest of the table, if possible. Or run them very slowly, letting each have their moment in the group's attention.

---

## Frygtløs – The Decision to Be Different

**Setting:** The party rests three days after the finale. Frygtløs is alone.

A figure approaches: **Veksin** (Blue cult wizard, if spared during the battle) or a **young cultist** (if captured rather than killed).

> *"Jeg tænkte, du ville dræbe mig,"* siger han.
>
> *"Da jeg så jer komme op ad trappen. Jeg tænkte, det var min tid."*
>
> Han sidder sig ned. Ikke truende. Bare træt.
>
> *"Men du valgte ikke det. Og jeg har brugt tre dage på at prøve at forstå hvorfor."*

**This is the moment.** Frygtløs killed Naergoth. He fought with rage and violence. But he is being offered a choice: does he regret it? Does he accept his nature? Does he choose differently next time?

The scene has no "right" answer. It is purely reflective.

If Frygtløs says he regrets Naergoth's death: The cultist nods. *"Da Naergoth åbnede med 'velkommen' — han var ikke ved at angribe jer. Han var ved at høre jer an. Hvis I havde hørt ham ud ... jeg ved ikke hvad han ville have gjort. Men jeg ved det var ikke det han gjorde."*

If Frygtløs says he doesn't regret it: The cultist nods too. *"Så er vi begge bare mennesker, der gjorde, hvad de troede var rigtigt. Og verden fortsætter."*

Either way, the cultist stands.

> *"I har købt verden tre hundrede år af stilhed. Det betyder noget. Selv hvis halvdelen af hvad I gjorde, var forkert — det betyder noget."*

He walks away. Frygtløs never learns his name.

---

## Axar – The Ritual's Whisper (Never Fully Silent)

**Setting:** Two weeks after the finale. Axar is in a library, trying to read.

The ritual structure keeps appearing at the edges of his vision. Not hallucination. Not magic. Just his *mind*, replaying the shapes of power.

> Han kan se den i den stille luft foran ham. Fem farver, roterende. Og hendes øjne, stadig åbne i det der blev døren.
>
> Han forsøger at læse, men ordene blender sammen. Han lukker øjet og ser langt bedre.

**An unknown Thayan mage sends a letter:**

> *Axar Runes,*
>
> *Galvan var svag. Tiamat fandt ham, som hun altid finder de svage. Du gjorde godt i at sende ham tilbage til hvad der engang mente ham.*
>
> *Men tag denne advarsel: hvad du så i templet — de fem farver, strukturen — det er ikke væk. Det vokser. Tiamat er tilbage i Avernus, men hendes ritual eksisterer stadig på dette plan, lige nu, langsomt.*
>
> *Hvis du en dag vil se det afsluttes *helt* — ikke bare lukket, men *ødelagt* — så send mig ord. Der er flere i Thay, der ønsker at se det ske.*
>
> *— En som kan se hvad du så*

**This is the opening:** Axar's post-finale journey might be learning to master or destroy the ritual structure he glimpsed. Or it might be learning to accept that some doors, once opened, never fully close.

He keeps the letter. He does not yet answer.

---

## Daxx – The Face Beneath the Mask

**Setting:** One month after the finale. Daxx stands before a mirror.

He is in his own form — the form he was born with, before he learned to be a Changeling.

He does not recognize himself.

> Han ser ud over øjnene i spejlet. De er hans øjne, men de hører ikke til ham længere.
>
> Han prøver at smile. Det smil er fremmed.
>
> Han tænker på de andre former, han har været. Daxx the Red. Daxx the Blue. The merchant. The guard. The girl.
>
> Alle af dem føles mere ægte end denne.

**A Harper scout (or a fellow Changeling, if one exists in the party's contacts) appears:**

> *"Det sker til alle os,"* siger vedkommende.
>
> *"Vi bliver så dygtige til at være andre mennesker, at vi glemmer hvem vi var. Og så, en dag, prøver vi at gå tilbage, og det passer ikke mere."*
>
> En pause.
>
> *"Der er to veje: Du kan dræbe den oprindelige person helt — blive hvad du blev. Eller du kan acceptere, at du er både."*

Daxx does not have to answer now. But the scene plants the seed: his journey forward is about integration, not choosing one face.

**Optional:** He later finds a prisoner who survived the Well of Dragons (a callback to the slave-pen storyline). This person remembers him from before — remembers the original face. *"It was you. Isn't it?"* And Daxx has to decide: does he reclaim that person? Ignore them? Become both at once?

---

## Twilight – The Song That Survives

**Setting:** Three weeks after the finale. Twilight is in a tavern, singing.

The song goes well. The crowd is moved. But in the middle of a verse, one note escapes her. The *wrong* note. A note that is not in her register anymore.

She catches it. Keeps singing. No one notices but her.

Later, she tries to find that note again. She cannot. It is gone.

> Det er ikke en stor note. Det var ikke vigtig. Men det var *hendes* note.
>
> Og nu er den væk.
>
> Hun ved ikke hvorfor. Hun er sikker, det kommer tilbage. Men hvad hvis det ikke gjorde det?

**A bard from the allied armies approaches her in the tavern:**

> *"Jeg hørte dig synge. Du er god. Men jeg hørte også det øjeblik, hvor noget brød."*
>
> Han sidder sig ned.
>
> *"Jeg var der, på slagmarken, da du sang under angrebet. Og jeg hørte en sang, der påstod styrke, mens verden var svag. Det var løgn. Det var en nødvendig løgn. Men det var løgn."*
>
> *"Nu høre jeg en sang, der accepterer sorgen. Det er smukkere. Og det er sandere."*
>
> *"Den note, du mistede — du mistede den, fordi du ikke længere kan synge løgne. Og det betyder, at du synger bedre nu end før."*

Twilight can accept or reject this interpretation. But the moment stands: her loss is also a transformation.

# Part 6: Hook for Continuation (Optional)

Depending on your group's desires, plant one of these seeds for a next chapter.

## If Full Victory

**Quiet Hook – The Cult Reformation:**

> Remallia approaches the party in Waterdeep, three months later.
>
> *"Vi har hørt rygter fra Calimshan. Orden af Dragen viser tegn på at opslamle medlemmer i fjerne landsdele. De taler om en ny leder. De taler om at Severin ikke var den sidste."*
>
> *"Det betyder ikke noget i dag. I har tjent verden mange år. Men hvis I nogensinde ønsker at tjene den igen — hvis I ønsker at vide, hvem der bygger det op igen — siger mig besked."*

This hooks into continuation campaigns focusing on rooting out cult cells globally.

## If Pyrrhic Victory

**Active Hook – The Well Vigil:**

> En metallic drage (Tazmikella eller Otaaryliakkarnos) lands at the party's location six months later.
>
> *"Porten blæder. Hver nat bliver den lidt bredere. Hvad kom gennem i dag, ville have været meningsløst før. Men hver dag, er det mindre meningsløst."*
>
> *"Jeg kan ikke være der hele tiden. Jeg er en drage. Jeg skal jage og bygge rede og leve. Men du — du kunde være der. Du kunne se til det."*
>
> *"Vil du?"*

This hooks into a campaign about defending a specific location against Avernus incursions (Descent into Avernus-adjacent).

## If Failure-Forward

**Urgent Hook – The First Survivors:**

> A group of refugees from Baldur's Gate finds the party in the Underdark or in Waterdeep's refugee camps.
>
> *"Vi hørt at nogle mennesker lavede en forskel mod hende. Mod Tiamat. I en tempel, før hun kom gennem."*
>
> *"Hvis I gjorde det før, kan I gøre det igen. Vi har ikke meget håb. Men vi har jer."*

This hooks into a campaign about global resistance, finding allies, and preparing for a final showdown where victory is possible but not certain.

# Part 7: Table Close-Out (Final Scene)

Approximately 90 minutes after the finale battle, once all scenes and moments have landed, run this final moment.

**Setting:** The party is resting somewhere safe — a tavern, a hall, a campfire. NPCs have come and gone. The world has been allowed to settle.

> Det bliver stille.
>
> Ikke tomt-stille. Bare — fredelig-stille. Verden respirerer på normal måde igen. Lyden af mennesker der drikker. En spiller der spiller en melodi. Nogen, der griner, fordi de stadig kan.

One by one, surviving NPCs and allies approach the party. They do not speak. They just *acknowledge*. A nod. A place at the table. A raised cup.

If your table is comfortable with it, invite each player to give a one-line reflection of what their character is thinking in this moment. Not a speech. Just a line. A thought.

When all have spoken (or chosen silence), read this:

> *"I kom til Brunnen af Drager for at stoppe en gud. Eller for at prøve. Eller fordi I ikke havde noget valg. Det spekulerer historikerne på nu.*
>
> *Men det vigtige er simpelt: I kom. I forsøgte. Og verden er stadig her.*
>
> *Det betyder noget. Det skal være nok."*

**End of campaign arc.**
