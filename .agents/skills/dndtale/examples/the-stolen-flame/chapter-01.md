# Chapter 1: The Stolen Flame

**Session(s):** 1 (full one-shot, 3-4 hours)
**Level:** 1
**Expected Duration:** 3-4 hours total

---

## Chapter Overview

**Synopsis:** The party arrives in Redbrook village to find it in crisis—the sacred Eternal Flame has been stolen by kobolds. They investigate, track the thieves through the forest, infiltrate the dragon's lair, and recover the Flame.

**Objectives:**
- **Primary:** Recover the Eternal Flame from the dragon's lair
- **Secondary:** Learn who took the Flame and why
- **Optional:** Minimize casualties; negotiate with the dragon

**Key NPCs:** Elder Mira Thorne, Brother Kellan, Sleetscale, Kreeg
**Key Locations:** Redbrook Village, Thornwood Forest, Dragon's Lair

---

## Chapter Hooks

The party is traveling through the region and arrives at Redbrook seeking rest. Alternatively, see campaign-overview.md for other hooks (personal connection, divine quest, hired escort).

## Scene 1: Darkness Over Redbrook

### Introduction

> You crest the final hill as the sun sinks toward the horizon, and the village of Redbrook spreads before you in the valley below. The village should be a welcoming sight after days of travel—but something is wrong. The streets are too quiet, too dark. No smoke rises from chimneys despite the evening chill. Villagers huddle in small groups in the central square, their faces drawn with worry. At the center of it all stands a stone temple, its doors thrown wide—but where you'd expect to see the warm glow of sacred flames, there is only darkness.

![Redbrook at Dusk](art/redbrook-village.jpg)

### DM Information

The village is gripped by fear. The Eternal Flame, which has burned for generations, was stolen three nights ago. Crops are already beginning to wilt, and the villagers fear worse is coming. Elder Mira Thorne is organizing the village's response, but they have no one capable of tracking and confronting the thieves.

**Hidden Details:**
- **DC 10 Perception:** The temple's entrance shows signs of a struggle—scratches on the stone, a broken lantern
- **DC 12 Investigation around temple:** Small footprints (kobolds) leading from the temple toward the northeastern edge of town
- **DC 15 Investigation:** Faint smell of sulfur and cold near the temple entrance

**Possible Player Actions:**
1. **If players approach villagers:** They're directed to Elder Mira in the temple
2. **If players investigate the temple:** Brother Kellan is there, can explain what happened
3. **If players search the area:** They find kobold tracks leading northeast

### NPCs Present

**Elder Mira Thorne**
- **Appearance:** Silver-haired woman in her sixties, practical clothing, worried expression
- **Attitude:** Wary of strangers but desperate for help
- **Wants:** Someone to retrieve the Eternal Flame
- **Knows:**
  - The theft occurred three nights ago around midnight
  - Brother Kellan was knocked unconscious
  - The thieves left tracks heading northeast toward the hills
  - The village's crops and protection will fail without the Flame
- **Will Reveal:** All of the above freely—she's desperate
- **Might Reveal (DC 12 Persuasion):** The village can pay 50 gp per person, and she'll give her family's heirloom weapon to whoever returns the Flame
- **Won't Reveal:** Her personal guilt about failing the village (she volunteers this if the party is kind)

**Brother Kellan**
- **Appearance:** Middle-aged priest, thinning hair, guilt-ridden expression
- **Attitude:** Apologetic and desperate
- **Wants:** Forgiveness and the Flame's return
- **Knows:**
  - He was guarding the temple when he heard sounds at midnight
  - He saw small, scaled creatures with glowing eyes (kobolds)
  - One hit him from behind; he woke to find the Flame's brazier gone
  - He heard them mention "Sleetscale" and "warm-warm for the mistress"
- **Will Reveal:** Everything; he feels guilty and wants to help
- **Might Reveal (DC 10 Medicine check on him):** He has a small head wound consistent with being hit by a kobold's club
- **Won't Reveal:** Nothing—he's completely honest

### Information Gathering

Players can learn the following through investigation:

**From villagers (various NPCs):**
- Old Hem the farmer saw "little lizard things" running northeast after midnight three nights ago
- The Eternal Flame is a sacred gift from the sun god, protecting crops and warding evil
- Kobolds have never come this close to town before—something is organizing them
- There are old caves in the hills to the northeast, about two hours' walk

**From searching around the temple:**
- **DC 10 Survival:** Kobold footprints lead northeast toward the Thornwood Forest
- **DC 12 Investigation:** A small copper scale caught in the temple door frame (dragon scale, DC 15 Arcana or Nature to identify)
- **DC 15 Investigation:** Scrape marks suggest the brazier was dragged, not carried—they had trouble moving it

### Quest Acceptance

Elder Mira offers the party:
- **Payment:** 50 gp per person upon return of the Flame
- **Reward:** One +1 weapon (her family heirloom) OR 2 Potions of Healing
- **Gratitude:** Free lodging and supplies in Redbrook for life

She provides:
- Basic supplies (rations, torches, rope) if needed
- Directions to the caves (northeast through Thornwood, about 2 hours)
- Blessing from Brother Kellan (+1d4 to first saving throw; one-time use)

### Connections
- **To Scene 2:** Party sets out northeast through Thornwood Forest following the tracks

## Scene 2: Ambush in Thornwood

### Introduction

> The path through Thornwood Forest is well-worn but narrow, hemmed in by ancient oaks and thick thornbushes. Shafts of fading sunlight pierce the canopy, casting long shadows across the forest floor. The kobold tracks are clear here—disturbed leaves, broken twigs, the occasional small footprint in soft earth. You've been walking for about an hour when you hear it: high-pitched chittering and yipping voices ahead, speaking in Draconic.

![Thornwood Forest Path](art/thornwood-ambush.jpg)

### DM Information

A kobold patrol (4 kobolds) is on its way back to the lair after gathering supplies. They're arguing about whether the dragon will reward them. The party can surprise them, negotiate, or be spotted and fight.

**The Clearing:**
- 30 feet across, forest on all sides
- Large fallen log provides half-cover
- Dense thornbushes on the east side (difficult terrain)

**Kobold Position:**
- 2 kobolds in the clearing arguing
- 2 kobolds flanking, hidden in bushes (DC 13 Perception to spot)

**Possible Player Actions:**
1. **If players sneak (DC 12 Stealth):** They surprise the kobolds, gain advantage on first round
2. **If players approach openly:** Kobolds screech and attack
3. **If players speak Draconic and call out:** Kobolds hesitate (can be negotiated with, DC 15 Intimidation or Persuasion)
4. **If players bypass the clearing:** DC 14 Survival to find alternate path through thornbushes (takes 30 extra minutes)

### Encounter: Kobold Patrol

**Combat Encounter:**
- **Creatures:** 4 Kobolds (MM p.195)
- **Terrain:** Open clearing with log (half-cover) and thornbushes (difficult terrain)
- **Tactics:**
  - Use Pack Tactics when possible
  - Hidden kobolds sneak attack if possible
  - Flee if 2 are killed
- **Morale:** If reduced to 1-2 kobolds, survivors flee or surrender

**Diplomatic Resolution:**
- **DC 15 Intimidation:** Kobolds cower and answer questions
- **DC 15 Persuasion:** Kobolds think party are "other servants of Great Sleetscale" and talk
- **DC 18 Deception:** Kobolds believe party are dragon's allies, escort them to lair

**Stealth Resolution:**
- **DC 12 Group Stealth check:** Party sneaks past without alerting kobolds
- **Failure:** Kobolds spot them and combat begins with kobolds behind half-cover

### Information from Kobolds

If a kobold is captured, intimidated, or convinced to talk:

**Will reveal freely (if scared):**
- They serve "Great Sleetscale," a dragon
- She lives in caves "that way" (points northeast)
- She ordered them to steal "the warm-light thing"
- There are many more kobolds at the lair

**Might reveal (DC 12 Intimidation/Persuasion):**
- The lair has 5 main areas
- About 12 kobolds total
- Kreeg is the kobold leader
- The dragon keeps the Flame in her chamber "to stay warm-warm"

**Won't reveal (doesn't know):**
- How to fight the dragon
- Exactly where the Flame is in the chamber
- Any secret passages

### Treasure/Rewards
- **Kobold loot:** 4 sp each, crude weapons (worthless)
- **Experience:** Standard XP for 4 kobolds

### Connections
- **To Scene 3:** Continue northeast to find the cave entrance
- **If kobolds escaped:** They warn the lair; Area 1 has 4 guards instead of 2

## Scene 3: The Dragon's Lair - Entrance

### Introduction

> The forest thins as you climb into rockier terrain. Ahead, a jumble of boulders forms a natural maze at the base of a low hill. The air grows noticeably colder, carrying a faint musk of reptiles and damp stone. Somewhere ahead, you hear the echo of high-pitched voices and the drip of water.

![Cave Entrance](art/cave-entrance.jpg)

### DM Information

The cave entrance is hidden among the boulders. Two kobold guards watch the entrance, but they're not very alert (currently playing a game with pebbles).

**The Area:**
- Cave entrance is 5 feet wide, opens into 20-foot chamber (Area 1)
- Boulders provide cover and concealment
- DC 12 Perception to spot the entrance from 30+ feet away
- DC 10 Perception to spot the guards once you see the entrance

**Kobold Guards:**
- 2 kobolds sitting just inside the entrance
- Not paying much attention (playing game, arguing)
- Perception +0, passive Perception 8

**Possible Player Actions:**
1. **If players sneak (DC 10 Stealth):** Slip past guards into cave
2. **If players attack from ambush:** Surprise round against guards
3. **If players approach openly:** Guards screech alarm (alerts Areas 2 and 4)
4. **If players wait and observe:** After 1 hour, guards change shift (opportunity to sneak)

### Area 1: Entrance Cave

> The cave entrance opens into a rough stone chamber, about twenty feet across. The air is cold and damp. Crude bedrolls made of hide and straw are scattered around the edges, and gnawed bones litter the floor. Two tunnels lead deeper into darkness: one straight ahead, one veering to the left.

**Details:**
- Crude living space for guards
- Bones are from small animals (rabbits, birds)
- Left tunnel leads to Area 2 (main route)
- Straight tunnel leads to Area 3 (flooded cavern, alternate route)

**Encounter:**
- 2 kobold guards (or 4 if alerted by escaped patrol)
- Fight same as kobold patrol encounter
- If guards screech before dying, Area 4 is alerted (takes 2 rounds for reinforcements)

**Treasure:**
- Worthless kobold belongings
- 12 sp in a pile (their "treasure")

### Connections
- **To Scene 4:** Take left tunnel through Area 2 (Twisting Tunnels)
- **To Scene 4 (alternate):** Take straight tunnel through Area 3 (Flooded Cavern)—harder but bypasses some kobolds
- **To Scene 5:** Either route eventually reaches the dragon's chamber

## DM Notes & Tips

### Pacing This Chapter

- **Scene 1 (Village):** 30-45 minutes—set up the quest, don't rush the investigation
- **Scene 2 (Forest):** 20-30 minutes—exciting encounter, show kobolds are dangerous but beatable
- **Scene 3 (Entrance):** 15-20 minutes—build tension as they enter the lair
- **Scenes 4-5 (Lair & Dragon):** 90-120 minutes—climax, let players strategize

### Common Player Approaches

**Stealth-Heavy Party:**
- Let them sneak through much of the lair
- Reward good planning
- Final dragon encounter still required

**Combat-Heavy Party:**
- Let them fight their way through
- Kobolds flee or surrender to keep it moving
- Make dragon fight feel epic

**Diplomatic Party:**
- Kreeg can be turned against dragon
- Sleetscale can be negotiated with
- Reward clever solutions

### If Things Go Wrong

**If players get stuck investigating:**
- Brother Kellan offers to come with them and track the kobolds
- Another villager arrives with new information about the caves

**If combat is too easy:**
- Add 1-2 more kobolds to encounters
- Have Sleetscale be more aggressive

**If combat is too hard:**
- Kobolds flee when bloodied
- Sleetscale offers deal when bloodied
- Elder Mira's blessing provides additional help

**If Sleetscale TPKs the party:**
- She keeps them prisoner, demands ransom from village
- Party wakes up captured, can escape
- This is unlikely at proper challenge level

### Connections to Larger Campaign

This is designed as a one-shot, but hooks for continuation:
- Sleetscale's mother seeks revenge or information
- The Eternal Flame was changed by its time with the dragon—what does this mean?
- Other dragon wyrmlings are claiming territory—who's organizing them?
