# Locations - The Stolen Flame

## Major Locations

### Dragon's Lair

**Type:** Dungeon (Natural Cave System)
**Size:** Small (5 areas)
**Population:** 1 dragon, ~12 kobolds
**First Appearance:** Chapter 1, Scene 3

#### Overview

A natural cave system in the hills northeast of Redbrook, now claimed by the young white dragon Sleetscale and her kobold servants. The caves are cold, damp, and filled with the echoes of dripping water and kobold chatter.

#### Atmosphere & Mood

The lair should feel oppressive and dangerous, but not overwhelmingly so. The kobolds are numerous but cowardly. The environment is hostile (slippery rocks, cold water, darkness) but navigable.

**Sights:** Rough stone walls, crude kobold decorations (skulls, totems), ice forming on walls deeper in
**Sounds:** Dripping water, distant yipping, the occasional rumble of dragon breath
**Smells:** Damp stone, kobold musk, cold air
**Feel:** Cold (50°F near entrance, colder deeper), damp, confined

#### History

These caves have existed for centuries, occasionally serving as shelter for bandits or wildlife. Sleetscale claimed them a season ago when she left her mother's territory.

#### Current Situation

The dragon has organized the kobolds into a crude hierarchy. They patrol the outer areas while she guards her hoard (including the Eternal Flame) in the deepest chamber.

#### Key Features

1. **Hidden Entrance:** DC 12 Perception to spot from the forest trail
2. **Narrow Passages:** Some areas require squeezing (difficult terrain)
3. **Underground Stream:** Cold water flows through the lower areas
4. **Dragon's Chamber:** Largest chamber, contains hoard and Eternal Flame

#### Locations Within

##### Area 1: Entrance Cave
Rocky chamber with evidence of kobold activity (crude bedrolls, gnawed bones). Usually 2 kobold guards.

##### Area 2: Twisting Tunnels
Narrow passages connecting the entrance to deeper areas. Easy to get lost. Kobolds know shortcuts.

##### Area 3: Flooded Cavern
Chamber partially filled with freezing underground stream. Kobolds avoid this area. Contains side passage to Area 5.

##### Area 4: Kobold Warren
Where most kobolds live. Crude living spaces, stored loot from raids. Usually 4-6 kobolds here.

##### Area 5: Dragon's Chamber
Sleetscale's lair. Ice forming on walls, small hoard of treasure, and the Eternal Flame in its brazier providing warmth and light.

#### Notable NPCs

| Name | Role | Location Within | Notes |
|------|------|----------------|--------|
| Sleetscale | Dragon | Area 5 | Guards the Flame |
| Kreeg | Kobold leader | Area 4 or 5 | Commands other kobolds |
| Kobold guards | Sentries | Area 1 | Alert others if alarmed |

#### Encounters & Challenges

**Potential Encounters:**
- Area 1: 2 kobold guards (combat or stealth)
- Area 2: Navigation challenge (Investigation DC 12 to find fastest route)
- Area 3: Environmental hazard (cold water, potential swim)
- Area 4: 4-6 kobolds (combat, negotiation, or stealth)
- Area 5: Sleetscale + 1d4 kobolds (climax encounter)

**Environmental Hazards:**
- **Cold Water:** DC 10 CON save or disadvantage on attacks for 10 minutes
- **Slippery Rocks:** DEX save DC 10 when running, fall prone on failure
- **Darkness:** Kobolds have darkvision; bring light or suffer disadvantage

#### Secrets & Hidden Elements

- **DC 15 Investigation in Area 4:** Hidden stash of stolen goods from farms (50 gp worth)
- **DC 12 Perception in Area 3:** Side passage that bypasses kobold warren
- **Area 5:** Dragon hoard includes small gems and coins

#### Treasure & Items

**Available in Lair:**
- **The Eternal Flame:** Sacred brazier (quest objective)
- **Dragon's Hoard:** 120 gp, 340 sp, 2 emeralds (50 gp each)
- **Kobold Loot:** Crude weapons, stolen farm tools (mostly worthless)

#### DM Notes

**Running This Location:**
- Play up the kobolds' cowardice—they flee or surrender easily
- Give players options: sneak, fight, or negotiate
- The cold and dark should be atmospheric, not mechanically punishing
- Sleetscale should feel like a boss, but not unbeatable

**If Players Skip Areas:**
- That's fine! Reward smart planning
- If they sneak through Area 3, they bypass Area 4
- Skipped kobolds might flee or join final fight

**Connections:**
- Reached from Redbrook via Thornwood Forest (2 hour travel)
- Footprints and clues lead here from village

## Supporting Locations

### Redbrook Village

**Type:** Small Village
**First Appearance:** Chapter 1, Scene 1

#### Quick Description

A farming village of about 200 people, nestled where a creek runs through the Thornwood Forest. Simple wooden buildings cluster around a central square with a temple.

#### Key Features
- **Temple:** Where the Eternal Flame was kept (now dark)
- **Village Square:** Central gathering place
- **The Grain & Gander Inn:** Where party can rest

#### Notable NPCs
- Elder Mira Thorne (leader)
- Brother Kellan (priest)
- Willow (innkeeper)

#### What Players Can Do Here
- Gather information about the theft
- Get supplies (basic equipment only)
- Accept the quest from Elder Mira
- Rest and recover

#### Secrets
- DC 12 Investigation: Find kobold footprints leading northeast
- Talking to villagers reveals timing and details of theft

---

### Thornwood Forest

**Type:** Wilderness
**First Appearance:** Chapter 1, Scene 2

#### Quick Description

Dense forest of oak and thorn between Redbrook and the hills. Well-used trails crisscross it, but easy to get lost off the paths.

#### Key Features
- Kobold footprints lead northeast through forest
- Halfway point: encounter kobold patrol

#### What Players Can Do Here
- Track the kobolds (Survival DC 10)
- Encounter and fight/interrogate kobold patrol
- Forage or hunt (if desired)
