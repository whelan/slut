# The Stolen Flame - Player Briefing

**Welcome to The Stolen Flame!**

This document is for players. Read this before Session 1 to prepare your character. Do not read other campaign documents—they contain spoilers!

---

## Campaign Overview

You and your fellow adventurers are traveling through rural farmlands when you arrive at the village of Redbrook seeking rest and supplies. However, you find the village in crisis—their sacred Eternal Flame has been stolen, and the villagers desperately need heroes to recover it.

This is a **one-shot adventure** designed to be completed in a single session. Expect heroic action, some humor, and opportunities for both clever thinking and sword-swinging heroics.

---

## Essential Information

**Campaign Type:** Linear adventure with player choice in how to approach challenges

**Starting Level:** 1

**Expected Length:** 1 session (3-4 hours)

**Session Length:** 3-4 hours

**Tone & Themes:** Heroic fantasy with light humor. You're heroes helping a community in need. Expect combat, but also opportunities for creative problem-solving and negotiation.

---

## Setting & World

### The World

This adventure takes place in a standard D&D fantasy world. Magic exists but is uncommon in rural areas. Gods are real and their priests wield divine power. Dragons and monsters are dangerous but rare.

### The Region

**Redbrook** is a small farming village on the edge of the **Thornwood Forest**. About 200 people live here, mostly human farmers and craftspeople. The village has stood for generations, protected by the Eternal Flame—a sacred fire given by the sun god that wards off evil and blesses the crops.

### Recent Events

Three nights ago, the Eternal Flame was stolen from its temple by unknown thieves. The village is gripped by fear—without the Flame's protection, their crops are failing and they feel vulnerable.

### Common Knowledge

**Currency:** Standard D&D gold, silver, and copper
**Languages:** Common is spoken by everyone; other racial languages exist
**Magic:** Uncommon in rural areas but not unheard of; the temple priest can cast minor divine magic
**Religion:** Multiple gods exist; Redbrook's temple honors the sun god
**Technology Level:** Medieval fantasy (swords, bows, no guns or advanced tech)

## Character Concept Guidance

### What Works Well

Since this is a one-shot about helping a village recover a sacred artifact:

- **Heroic characters** who want to help people
- **Adventurous characters** seeking excitement and glory
- **Pious characters** who want to protect the sacred
- **Mercenary characters** who work for pay (the village offers payment)

### Character Motivations

Your character should have a reason to:
1. **Help the village** (heroism, payment, divine calling, or just passing through)
2. **Work with a party** (adventurers band together for safety and success)
3. **Face danger** (why do they risk their life for others?)

Consider:
- Why is your character traveling through this region?
- How do they feel about rural villagers in need?
- What would motivate them to fight a dragon?

### Party Cohesion

**Important:** This adventure works best when the party cooperates. Your characters should be willing to work together. You can:
- Be traveling companions who met on the road
- Be a pre-existing adventuring party
- Meet in the village and band together for the quest

You don't need detailed shared backstories—simple "we've been traveling together for a few weeks" is perfect for a one-shot.

## What to Expect

### Gameplay Balance

- **Combat:** Several combat encounters including a climactic boss fight
- **Exploration:** Investigating the village, tracking through wilderness, exploring a dungeon
- **Roleplay:** Talking to villagers, potentially negotiating with enemies

### Challenges

- You'll face kobolds (small dragon-like creatures) and a young dragon
- There will be opportunities for stealth, diplomacy, and combat
- Creative solutions are encouraged and rewarded

### Risk Level

This is a Level 1 adventure, so **character death is possible** if you're reckless, but the adventure is balanced fairly. Play smart, work together, and you should succeed.

## Table Rules & Expectations

### How We'll Play

- **Respect:** Be respectful to everyone at the table
- **Spotlight:** Everyone gets their time to shine
- **Collaboration:** Work together; this isn't player vs. player
- **Fun:** The goal is for everyone to have fun

### Content & Boundaries

**Tone:** Heroic fantasy, generally appropriate for teens and up

**Content Notes:**

## Starting Scenario

### Where You'll Start

You arrive at Redbrook village as the sun sets. You've been traveling for days and are looking for a place to rest, eat, and resupply.

### What's Happening

The village is in crisis. The sacred Eternal Flame has been stolen, and the villagers are frightened and desperate for help.

### Why You're There

You're traveling through the region (specific reason is up to you):
- Heading to a larger city for work
- Wandering adventurers seeking fortune
- Escorting someone (who has departed/stayed behind)
- Returning home from somewhere
- Any reasonable explanation
