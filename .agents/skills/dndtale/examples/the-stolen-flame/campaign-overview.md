# The Stolen Flame

**Campaign Type:** Linear (Single Session)
**Level Range:** 1
**Expected Sessions:** 1 (3-4 hours)
**Players:** 3-5
**Tone:** Heroic adventure with light humor

---

## Adventure Background

The village of Redbrook sits at the edge of the Thornwood Forest. For generations, the **Eternal Flame** has burned in the village temple, a sacred gift from the sun god that protects the crops and wards off evil. Three nights ago, a band of kobolds snuck into the village and stole the Flame's brazier, plunging Redbrook into darkness and fear.

The kobolds serve a young white dragon named **Sleetscale** who has claimed a cave system in the nearby hills. The dragon covets the magical warmth of the Eternal Flame for her lair.

---

## Adventure Synopsis

The adventure follows a straightforward path with opportunities for player creativity:

### Major Story Beats

1. **Hook & Investigation (Village):** Party arrives in Redbrook, learns about the theft, gathers clues
2. **Journey to Dragon's Lair:** Travel through wilderness, encounter kobold patrol
3. **Infiltrate the Lair:** Sneak, fight, or trick their way through kobold-infested caves
4. **Recover the Flame:** Confront the dragon (fight, negotiate, or steal) and reclaim the brazier

---

## Adventure Hooks

### Primary Hook
The party is traveling through the region and arrives at Redbrook seeking rest. They find the village in crisis and are offered payment and the village's gratitude to retrieve the Flame.

### Alternate Hooks
1. **Personal Connection:** One PC is from Redbrook or has family there
2. **Divine Quest:** A PC cleric/paladin receives a vision about the stolen sacred flame
3. **Hired Help:** The party was specifically hired to escort a temple priest to Redbrook and arrives to find the crisis

## Climax & Final Encounter

### Setup
The party reaches the deepest chamber of the dragon's lair where Sleetscale guards the stolen Eternal Flame. The young dragon is arrogant but not stupid—she can be fought, but also reasoned with.

### The Final Challenge
**The Dragon's Choice:** Sleetscale wants warmth for her lair. She's willing to negotiate if the party offers alternatives.

**Success Outcomes:**
- **Combat Victory:** Defeat Sleetscale (she flees at low HP), recover the Flame
- **Diplomatic Victory:** Convince Sleetscale to return the Flame in exchange for something else (treasure, promise of future aid, alternate heat source)
- **Trickery Victory:** Distract Sleetscale and steal the Flame back

### Multiple Resolution Paths
1. **Combat:** Fight the dragon and her kobold guards
2. **Diplomacy:** Negotiate with Sleetscale (she's young and can be reasoned with)
3. **Stealth/Trickery:** Create a distraction and steal the Flame
4. **Other:** Creative solutions (magic, environment, deals with kobolds)

---

## Resolution & Epilogue

### Immediate Aftermath
The party returns to Redbrook with the Eternal Flame. The village celebrates, the crops are safe, and the temple is restored. Brother Kellan re-lights the sacred brazier.

### Long-Term Consequences
- **If Sleetscale was killed:** The kobolds scatter, the hills are safe
- **If Sleetscale survived:** She remembers the party (future hook)
- **If negotiated:** Sleetscale may become an ally or recurring character

### Campaign Hooks for Future Adventures
1. Sleetscale's mother, an adult white dragon, investigates what happened to her daughter
2. The kobolds were also stealing from local farms—treasure to recover
3. Brother Kellan discovers the Eternal Flame has been changed by its time with the dragon

## DM Notes & Tips

### Running This Campaign

**Pacing:**
- Scene 1 (Village): 30-45 minutes
- Scene 2 (Wilderness): 30 minutes
- Scene 3-5 (Dragon's Lair): 90-120 minutes
- Total: 3-4 hours

**Key Points:**
- Emphasize the village's desperation to motivate party
- Give kobolds personality—they're cowardly and comedic
- Sleetscale should feel dangerous but not unbeatable
- Reward creative solutions

### Player Agency

**Multiple Solutions:**
- Village investigation can go many ways
- Lair can be approached with stealth, diplomacy, or combat
- Dragon encounter has multiple resolutions

**What Should Be Flexible:**
- How party investigates the village
- Route through the lair
- Resolution with Sleetscale

**What Should Be Fixed:**
- The Flame is in the dragon's lair
- Sleetscale wants warmth, not destruction
- Kobolds are cowardly

### Potential Challenges

**If players struggle in combat:** Kobolds flee, dragon offers deal
**If players bypass content:** That's fine! Reward clever solutions
**If Sleetscale dies too easily:** She had 1d4 kobolds with her that arrive mid-fight
