# Campaign Research & Foundation Checklist

Use this checklist when beginning any new campaign project. Complete it *before* detailed content creation to ensure a solid foundation. This is a pre-creation checklist — for post-creation quality assurance, see [campaign-quality-checklist.md](campaign-quality-checklist.md).

---

## Concept & Foundation

- [ ] **Core concept defined** — elevator pitch in 1–2 sentences
- [ ] **Source material analyzed** (if adaptation — see [literary-adaptation.md](../modules/literary-adaptation.md))
  - [ ] Thematic spine identified
  - [ ] Emotional arc mapped
  - [ ] Iconic moments cataloged
  - [ ] Player-agency friction points identified
- [ ] **2–3 core themes identified** — questions the campaign explores, not answers it preaches
- [ ] **Campaign type selected** — linear, sandbox, event-based, setting-based, or hybrid (see [campaign-types.md](../modules/campaign-types.md))
- [ ] **Tone and atmosphere established** — heroic, dark, humorous, horror, mystery, etc.
- [ ] **Player level range and progression path defined** — starting level through expected ending level
- [ ] **Session count estimated** — one-shot, short (3–5), medium (6–12), long (13+)

---

## World & Setting

- [ ] **Primary setting researched and selected** — official setting, homebrew, or adapted
- [ ] **Key locations identified** — at least 3–5 mapped to game geography
  - [ ] Starting location fully detailed (Inner Circle)
  - [ ] Surrounding region sketched (Middle Circle)
  - [ ] Wider world implied (Outer Circle)
- [ ] **Political landscape sketched** — who has power, who wants it, what happens if players tip the balance
- [ ] **Active factions designed with competing goals** — at least 2 with genuine, sympathetic conflicts
- [ ] **History relevant to the campaign developed** — only what matters, not an encyclopedia
- [ ] **Environmental storytelling opportunities identified** — locations that tell their own story without narration

---

## Characters & Relationships

- [ ] **Major NPCs designed with full depth** (see NPC Tiered Framework in [world-building.md](../modules/world-building.md))
  - [ ] Tier 3 NPCs: full inner life, independent arc, mechanical weight, voice, relationships
  - [ ] Every major NPC has a want (surface) and a need (deeper)
  - [ ] Every major NPC has a secret
- [ ] **Antagonist(s) designed with sympathetic motivations** (see Villain Design in [world-building.md](../modules/world-building.md))
  - [ ] Motivation, Method, Vulnerability, Escalation, Mirror defined
  - [ ] Villain believes they are justified
- [ ] **NPC relationship web mapped** — who knows whom, who trusts whom, where the conflicts lie
- [ ] **PC backstory integration points identified** — where player-created details weave into the campaign
- [ ] **Character arcs for major NPCs planned** — they change whether or not the players engage

---

## Story & Structure

- [ ] **Emotional arc mapped** — what should players feel and when (wonder → dread → resolve → catharsis)
- [ ] **Chapter/session breakdown created** — focus and dramatic question for each
- [ ] **Tentpole encounters designed** — the 3–5 moments players will remember forever
- [ ] **Player-agency departure points built in** — 3–5 moments where player choice can fork the story
- [ ] **Multiple resolution paths for the climax** — combat, social, stealth, clever, unexpected
- [ ] **Consequences tracked for major decision points** — Session 2 choices echo in Session 8
- [ ] **Three Clue Rule applied** — for any critical conclusion, at least three independent paths to the information

---

## Mechanics & Balance

- [ ] **Encounter balance verified for party level and size** — CR, action economy, terrain
- [ ] **Encounter variety ensured** — combat, social, exploration, hybrid (see [encounter-design.md](../modules/encounter-design.md))
- [ ] **Skill challenge DCs set appropriately** — scale with level, reward expertise
- [ ] **Treasure and rewards paced** — match effort, provide utility, create excitement
- [ ] **Custom mechanics (if any) designed and vetted** — homebrew rules, special abilities, unique systems
- [ ] **Boss encounters designed with phases and environmental elements** — not just "hit the big thing"

---

## When in Doubt

Ask yourself what your players would love. The research serves the design, the design serves the story, the story serves the players.
