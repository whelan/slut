## DM Resources

### Describing Locations

**First Impressions Matter:**
- Lead with the most striking feature
- Use sensory details beyond sight
- Establish the mood immediately
- Give players something to interact with

**Keep Descriptions Focused:**
- 2-3 sentences for minor locations
- 1 paragraph for important locations
- Break up long descriptions with player interaction

### Location Improvisation

If players go somewhere unexpected, use this template:

1. **Type:** [What kind of place is it?]
2. **Mood:** [How does it feel?]
3. **Feature:** [One memorable detail]
4. **NPC:** [Is anyone here?]
5. **Hook:** [What can players do here?]

### Reusing Locations

Make locations matter by:
- Bringing players back to locations
- Showing how locations change based on player actions
- Creating memories tied to places
- Rewarding exploration and investigation

### Common Location Types

**Tavern/Inn:**
- Gathering place for rumors
- Rest and recovery
- Meet quest-givers
- Base of operations

**Shop:**
- Buy/sell equipment
- Meet specialized NPCs
- Quest hooks from shopkeeper
- Services beyond just commerce

**Temple/Church:**
- Healing and restoration
- Divine magic services
- Religious quests
- Sanctuary

**Noble's Manor:**
- Social encounters
- Political intrigue
- Wealth and luxury
- Upper class NPCs

**Dungeon:**
- Combat encounters
- Traps and puzzles
- Treasure
- Environmental storytelling

**Wilderness:**
- Travel encounters
- Survival challenges
- Exploration
- Natural beauty or danger
