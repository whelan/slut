## DM Resources

### Making Factions Feel Alive

**Show, Don't Just Tell:**
- Let players encounter faction members in the world
- Show faction activities through events, not just exposition
- Let factions respond to player actions
- Have factions pursue their goals independently

**Faction Activities Between Sessions:**
- Consider what each faction is doing while players adventure
- Update faction status based on time passing
- Let faction conflicts progress

### Faction Complexity

**Avoid Simple Good vs Evil:**
- Give "good" factions flaws and limitations
- Give "evil" factions understandable motivations
- Create moral gray areas
- Let players disagree about which faction to support

**Internal Diversity:**
- Not all members agree with leadership
- Factions have reformers and traditionalists
- Personal ambition can override faction loyalty
- Individual members can be redeemed or corrupted

### Using Factions in Play

**As Quest Givers:**
- Different factions offer different types of quests
- Faction quests can conflict with each other
- Completing quests affects reputation

**As Obstacles:**
- Factions can oppose party goals
- Create interesting antagonists beyond "evil villain"
- Allow for negotiation and alliance

**As Resources:**
- Friendly factions provide aid
- Access to special items or information
- Safe havens and contacts

**As Consequences:**
- Party actions affect faction standing
- Factions remember and respond
- Choosing sides has lasting impact
