# Campaign Types

Campaigns can vary dramatically based on design factors like length, scope, and structure. Choose the appropriate type before diving into planning.

## Linear Adventures

**Best for:** New DMs, tightly plotted stories, time-limited campaigns

**Characteristics:**
- Sequential chapters with clear progression path
- Each chapter builds on the previous one
- Easier to prep and run
- Strong narrative control
- Players follow a defined story arc

**Prep Level:** Low to Medium

**Example:** The party must travel from Village A to City B, encountering specific challenges along the way.

---

## Sandbox/Hub Adventures

**Best for:** Experienced DMs, open-world exploration, long campaigns

**Characteristics:**
- Central location (city, region, base) with many possible options
- Multiple quest hooks available simultaneously
- Player choice determines order and priorities
- Requires much more prep than linear adventures
- High replayability

**Prep Level:** High

**Example:** Players are based in a city with multiple factions, each offering different quests that can be tackled in any order.

---

## Event-Based Adventures

**Best for:** Political intrigue, time-sensitive plots, reactive gameplay

**Characteristics:**
- Focus on things characters do rather than places they go
- Timeline of events that unfold with or without player intervention
- Player actions affect outcomes and change the timeline
- Events trigger based on in-game time or player choices
- Dynamic and responsive world

**Prep Level:** Medium to High

**Example:** A war is brewing between two kingdoms. Events unfold over weeks/months, and players must decide when and how to intervene.

---

## Setting-Based Adventures

**Best for:** Exploration-heavy gameplay, mystery/horror, dungeon crawls

**Characteristics:**
- Makes the location the centerpiece for action
- Detailed location descriptions with hidden areas
- Exploratory emphasis with secrets to uncover
- Environment tells the story
- Players piece together what happened

**Prep Level:** Medium

**Example:** An ancient dungeon complex or haunted mansion where exploring and discovering the location's history IS the adventure.

---

## Hybrid Approaches

You can combine elements from multiple types:
- **Linear-Hub:** Main story is linear, but each chapter offers a hub location with side quests
- **Event-Sandbox:** Open world with timeline-based events that create urgency
- **Setting-Linear:** Players explore a large location, but certain areas unlock sequentially

Choose what fits your DMing style and your players' preferences.
